I've been thinking about immortality a lot lately. Not for myself, mind you, just about the idea of it. Thinking about if someone was immortal how things would start to appear over time.

You start off in life (as the immortal mind you) as everyone else, but then at your bodies peak you start to notice the people around you growing older. At a certain point all of your immediate family, and all of the first set of friends you made have died; And in their place lie their children, and your nephews and neices and what not. Had you had a wife and kids, they too have died and been 'replaced' by more family.

At a certain point you understand that you can't die. For some time you are filled with the sorrow of losing all of the ones you love and the realization that anyone you will ever meet; and anyone you will ever fall in love with will die, along with any children you have. During the beginning of your life you viewed death with the equal fear that others have felt for it. But overtime that fear is probably replaced with the desire to meet death. I'd imagine at one point you would view death as a release from the pain of the world as you see man making the same mistakes over and over again in life. You view from the eyes of someone who has lived hundreds of lives and see mans weakness, his failures, and his own self-brainwashing ideals. All of this wrapped up in the guise of technological advancement.

Knowing you would live forever; Would you really want to dedicate eternity to 'knowledge' and 'learning'?
