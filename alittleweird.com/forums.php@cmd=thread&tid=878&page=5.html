ShadowWolf
I cannot answer that seemingly rhetorical in full regards, but I can offer some insight. Were we to never acknowledge that the sun is what brings us light would the skies dim? No, the sun would shine whether or not we acknowledged it. Nor did the sun stop its journey across our sky from east to west when our kin of old assumed it was a god totting it behind a mighty chariot. The metaphysical will always exist and whether or not it is acknowledged by others will not remove it as a element, but rather distance itself. And even then the distance remains only an illusion in the eyes of man, but I digress to that matter at hand.

It is never wise to get arrogant, to be bold in such a way as to boast in the face of opposition. Arrogance breeds complacency, and from that yields stagnation. And from the midst of my memory a quote comes to mind, "A skeptical is a healthy one." There will always be guile, deception, deceit, and trickery and cons come in all shapes and sizes, all places of the world, and in all literary levels, but can one ever become immune? The short answer is of course no. Experience is your greatest, and in most cases concerning this field, only reference for what is true and what is not. The problem is experience can often be scarce and our understanding of the true mechanics of reality minuscule. The only real measure is to keep an open mind, but a closed screen door.

Although I apologize for my ramblings of philosophy must be much like a broken record. And beyond that it must be terribly boring. Stay true to the aspiration and never forget that if this is a dream then at least one does not dream alone. We are out there, people like us, people better than us, but this is underworld, an underworld that we must ultimately discover on our own.
