Have you ever hurt yourself?
Author Message
Amaroq
One day, I decided to try a neat idea I had for gathering energy into a ball.

I created a really large, thin, hollow sphere (taller than me while standing) that was designed to trap energy inside of it out of the air. (Since energy is everywhere.)

I began shrinking the ball, compressing the energy more and more as the ball shrunk. The more condensed I made the energy inside of the ball, the more this pressure began to build against my right temple. (Against, as in pressing from the outside.)

Just when I got the ball down to the size of a softball, it felt like something gave way under that pressure, quickly followed by what felt like someone hitting me in the right side of my head with a sledgehammer. It left me dizzy for several minutes.

After that, when messing with energy, I'd sometimes have a slight headache, just on the right side around my temple. It felt sorta like a stress headache. I didn't always notice it, but when I thought about it, I could feel the headache.

Many months later, I was thinking back on it and decided to try a neat idea I had. I gathered energy in the center of my chest, focusing on "repair" and "reinforce". I think my original idea was to see if I could strengthen whatever was taking that pressure to see if I could increase my limit. As I pushed the energy up into my head and against my right temple from the inside, suddenly I felt like something "popped back" into place.

I got the headache back a little bit for a while, but I eventually stopped feeling those slight headaches altogether.

So that's one time I hurt myself. There is a second time that I've done so, which I've already mentioned in another topic.

I was gathering energy all around my body just for kicks one day, and suddenly I felt the quick, painful sensation of being stabbed in the heart with something about as thick as a pencil. A friend of mine told me that I had probably exhausted energy in my body to bring that much energy outside my body, and that having more 'pressure' on the outside caused some of it to force its way in to make up for it.
