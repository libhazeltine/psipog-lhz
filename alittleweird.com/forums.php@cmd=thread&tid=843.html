Ah, I think I'll answer this one. ;)

How you would go about starting out depends entirely upon what you are trying to achieve with Magic, so, if you don't mind, I will first try to give an answer to your other questions.

Chaos Magic, Hermetics and Runic Magic are different systems. Chaos Magic is a form of sorcery wherein there are no real 'rules', so to speak. People from the Chaos Magic paradigm believe that faith and belief is the real key to success (= achieving what you want, aka a spell). You can use whatever belief you want, there are sorcerers who use the 'force' (yes, from Star Wars), or they make up their own 'system'.

Another system is Runic Magic, personally, I'd say this is more of a compliment in your training, and not a complete system. Technically, it cán be a complete system of magical development, but most people who try this out start writing down (sigilizing) some runes and are happy when they've cast them and get some results.

Hermetics is the form of magic I am personally practicing, (in a way, I am not a 100% Hermetic, though,) in my opinion, it is the most complete system of Magic. However, this depends on what you want. Hermetics focuses mostly on developing the spirit, so as to allow Divine Consciousness to flow through it (aka merging with God). If this sounds way too religious and weird to you and you only want to use Magic for material ends, then this system is probably not for you. If you are interested in this however, I suggest you take up the Initiation into Hermetics by Franz Bardon and STICK WITH IT. It is the best book available on Hermetics (in my opinion) because it offers a complete system of training with ultimately results in merging with the God-form.

You can probably look up Chaos Magic and Runic Magic yourself, I think Veritas has a good article on Runic Magic, and there are many, many articles on Chaos Magic. (Check out Phil Hine's website.)

As for the different 'types' of magicians. This is just a matter of personal preference, for me, as a Hermetic, I see a magician as the 'good guy'. Because it comes from the word magia, which means wisdom. On the other hands, people from the Hermetic paradigm use the word 'sorcerer' as a name for someone who solely uses Magic for material ends instead of spiritual development. So it is up to you, many people have different definitions.

~Falcon

I would recommend following a complete system, or joining an order, but do NOT just start some random tutorials on the internet. It's fine to read them to get a basic idea of what Magic is all about, but if you want to be good at it, a complete system is much better.

Kube, Hermetics isn't really religion-like, it's more for the spiritual person, nevertheless, I already had the impression you weren't looking for this. ;)

The difference between Magick and Magic is.. none. Magick is a term coined by Aleister Crowley to differentiate between the stage magician and the 'real' magician.

Spirituality != Dogma (or Religion). Rules or 'beliefs' are what makes a dogma/religion, but spirituality is when it comes to experiences.

As for your question about picking bits of different systems, that is a possibility. You can, for example, make your own 'magic ritual' wherein you go into trance using the Chaos method (gnosis), use the power of the runes (Runic magic), and the god-names of Hermetics. *shrugs* On the other hand, I would recommend first mastering a single system .(Which is going to take a long, long time, so think before you start "do I really want to follow this?") You will never increase in power if you take bits of all systems and end up with nothing.

~Falcon
