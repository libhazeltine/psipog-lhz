@lain

You make an interesting point, but I'm afraid that this...

When people meditate, they forget their worries and just relax. You can't fullfill your life purpose if you don't worry about it, even just a little.
...is where the line is drawn that separates our beliefs.

I believe that meditating dissolves the illusion of having a meaning for one's life. Nothing is forgotten: All is merely realized and made clearer. Without an ego which desires and judges, the objective truth can be realized. The ego is said to be the cause of all desire and therefore the ego is that which causes us to search for a meaning in our lives. Without desire there is freedom, which also translates into the freedom from having a meaning to one's life.

Also...

I would have to agree with you on the fact that our egos would shatter if life didn't need a meaning.
I see. This adequately explains why you believe what you believe. Ironically, "shattering" one's ego is more or less the goal of every Buddhist. It is believed to be the only way to obtain true freedom...even the freedom from "needing" a meaning to one's life. Do you not see that "needing" a meaning for your life binds you to it and supresses your very freedom?

There is a reason why meditation eliminates our worries. It does this because we are experiencing true freedom from all of the false obligations that we create for ourselves.

It is the metaphorical equivalent of ceasing to clean your house or apartment because you no longer care about what your guests think of its cleanliness. The freedom from caring what people think about you can also set you free and allow you to do what you truly feel like doing. However, it is very difficult to distinguish between free will and the will of one's ego because of the ego's firm and almost constant grasp over our actions, thoughts, emotions, and psyche in general.

Well, this is all what I have come to believe anyway. I will not be upset if you simply cannot agree with me. I will respect your right to believe what you want to believe if you will respect my own. This is all I have to say for now. Unless you want to discuss something slightly different that we may be able to at least compromise on, then I suggest that we discuss this no further.
