July 14th, 2008 by SeanLottery Analysis

I’ve analyzed my results from the past 17 months, concerning the lottery.

I devised a way to measure how “close” I get to the winning numbers.  My formula for measuring this “closeness” is somewhat arbitrary, but there is no correct formula, so it works as good as any.  In calculating the odds, I measure the “closeness” of all possible tickets - then see how many tickets are just as “close” as my ticket.

For the curious programmers, you can see my C program for measuring “closeness” here: Lottery Score Calculator Source Code

    Well thats the general theory based on a misunderstanding of probability..

    By playing the same numbers every game you’d imagine you have a better chance of getting your numbers pulled, because probability shouldn’t favor those numbers that have already come up. However because each drawing is completely random yours odds are always technically the same for any set of numbers you choose, even if they’ve won before.

    In theory your numbers will come up eventually but theres no assurance that it happens before every other combination has appeared 100 times as a winner.
