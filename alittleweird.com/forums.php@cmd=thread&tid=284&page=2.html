Corpse-I remember the term now. It's "Radiesthesia". It's divination using a pendulum. I use a very basic set-up when I do it.

On a piece of paper draw a simple cross. At each end of the vertical line, write "Yes", and on each end of the horizontal line, write "No".

The beauty of this form of divination is that you don't need any fancy materials, and it's relatively uncomplicated.

All you need is a length of chain, and something to weight on the end. You could use a ring or a pendant..You got a necklace like that? Excellent. You'd be in business.

Now, I know that there is an article about this on psipog.net. But I use a different method-the one outline in "Wicca for One" by Raymond Buckland. (Just in case this comes up, no, I am not a Wiccan. I just enjoy reading all kinds of things like this).

I don't agree completely with some of the things in the article. However, this may just be because I read the other information first, and connected with it immediately. In any case, I question what force it is that makes the pendulum move. Is it what Rainsong said-your subconcious? Or is it something else?

In any case, it doesn't matter in this instance. Because I use it to communicate with spirits.

Where you practice this doesn't make a difference. The last time I did it, I was in a brightly lit room using a floor table. I was not alone. I used no candles or incense.

I must throw out there, however, that since you are trying to communicate with an entity, you might want to be in a quiet place. You MIGHT want to be alone. You can't really guess what circumstances would be most conducive to contact with the spirit you are trying to communicate with. I'd experiment with different things, see what works. If nothing does, it may be quite possible that it doesn't want to "play" with you.

Simple set-ups work obviously. But it might be good to move on to a more advanced one. It will expand the kinds of questions you can ask - mainly ones that aren't just "yes" "no". There are different ways to set up the most advanced ones. These include letters or numbers and sometimes both. I'd recommend looking it up online, because it would be difficult to explain and best understood if you could see it for yourself in the first place.

Now, you don't have to close your eyes for this. You aren't "drawing" on anything. You're waiting for it to come to you. Eyes open or closed, you can't influence that. Unless you can somehow summon spirits O.o Unlikely.

Many people aren't sure of the proper way to hold the pendulum. Do you hold your elbow off the table? Or do you rest it on the table? I prefer to rest my elbow on the table, because it steadies your arm. You won't get any interference from your own body shaking.

The chain should be wrapped around your forefinger and middle finger. It should hang only a few centimeters above the paper. Let the chain hang down and steady it, so it doesn't swing. Then ask your question.

Back and forth swinging indicates "Yes" answers -follows the yes line. Side to side indicates "No" answers.

The pendulum at times may just swing in a circle. This means either your question cannot be answered, or you phrased it ambiguously. Rephrase and try again.

Some people may be going-Well, this sounds sketchy! How am I supposed to know that I've really contacted a spirit and not something else?

The answer is in the questions. You can obviously formulate questions that will tell you what you have indeed made contact with.

Other fun things that you can do with this.

You might set up a tape recorder. I'd recommend doing this. Not only does it give you a record of what you've done, but you can capture things that you couldn't hear with your own ears. You might get disembodied voices, answers to your questions in the verbal.

You could also set up a camera. If you're not sure what type of camera would be best for capturing spirits, look it up. Since regular disposable cameras with no special lenses or anything can capture a spirit, I'm sure you don't need anything fancy. Something regular would probably do. Just to clarify, I AM talking about a video camera. The latter was just an example.

However, onto that. If you do this with a friend, you can have them snap photos every so often. Who knows? You might get something on tape.
