mrj
I tend to practice in the same way, actually.

And yes, the same thing happens to me, and that is really the definition of "on and off practice" isn't it? As for advice, well...what is it you want? I don't ask that just because I need some more info first, but really, think about it. What is it you are trying to achieve? If you think you are subconsciously backing away from psionics, maybe you could do one of two things.

Try another system out

OR

Consult thyself

As for the "other systems" suggestion, Float's website has some interesting stuff on that topic. Lots of things about magic systems, and other ideas.

As for "self council" however, think about simply asking yourself some questions. Things like "Why am I practicing?" "Is practice unappealing in any way?". What does your subconscious come up with? You don't have to tell me or anyone, but rather, just come to terms with it. Someone told me once (yes its one of these sayings :P) that "If you're truly honest with yourself, magical things will happen". Now I'm sure he didn't reference the paranormal with that, but it leads into something. So what is it exactly? Where am I going with this?

It's just as simple as motivation. Let's go back to the question "what do you want?".

When I first was interested in actively pursuing psychic abilities, it was in 2003. I was into the old (and infamous) "Radki" websites, as well as alot of other "energy communities" and Psipog as well. I was a kid, and, like most kids wanted to do cool stuff I saw on TV. But I practiced every day, even dragging my brother into it. I had even gotten a few weird effects from practicing alot. But that started to change when I found conflicting belief systems, and the concept of "fluff". When it came to challenging and conflicting opinions, I was on both sides. What I mean by that is, I really didn't know where I was, but I wanted to have some sort of solid ground. After being exposed to the RadKi Challenge I decided it was fake, and "joined" Psipogs ideals. I still wanted to do "cool stuff", but the motivation slowly dwindled as the reality of things settled in. I stopped caring.

It's like flame. It burns bright and vigorously, but only for so long. And then you have to clean up the ashes. It is an example of an unhealthy motivation.

So what is a healthy one? Probably something solid, lasting and realistic. (A candle maybe?)

Another thing I've noticed is that having a person go through it with you, or at least, give you feedback on the subject, is very helpful. They don't even have to practice with you. It could be someone doing research. You just need feedback. But I believe they have to be a person in real life. I personally think that nothing else really cuts it, although online places are great to test yourself out.

Basically, motivation is the key.
_________________________________________________________________________________

But I do have one more thing to say. When you "subconsciously back away from psionics", confront yourself. Challenge any automatic habits or behaviors/thoughts. Ask yourself "why" and keep challenging the reason given to you, and resolve anything if necessary.

I say this because I may have posted something long and irrelevant if the motivation thing doesn't apply to you, and because it is simply a good way to make/break habits.

Either way, I hope you get something out of this.
