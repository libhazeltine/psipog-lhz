Hi, I'm Dream Shaper, and I have a problem.

There's this girl who keeps appearing in my dreams. I can never get a look at her face. It's either covered by a hood, she's moving too fast to get a good look, she's invisible, etc. Here's what I do know: she's medium-height and slender with dirty blonde hair that falls just past her shoulders. And she keeps trying to kill me.

It started rather innocently. She would just try to drop a piano on me as a prank. Now she's actively hunting me. This needs to stop.

There was a time when I was able to determine what I would dream about. From there the solution is simple: dream of her attacking me, become lucid, then find out why she wants to kill me. But I can't do that anymore.

So this thread is to document my process of raising my mental powers until I can confront her unafraid. Weird enough for you?

Anyway, first thing I need to do is regain decent dream recall. So, I'll meditate for 10 minutes, followed by meditating for 5 minutes on remembering my dreams. I know you'll just be bored with all the dreams' details, so I'll just rate them based on clarity, cohesiveness, quantity, length, etc. Wish me luck!

Some tips for dream recall:

This is something I just figured out, and it is stupid, but it seems to work well. When I wake up in the morning, and it seems like all dreams are just wiped, I tell myself that I would like to remember them, then I start thinking of a song, anything that comes to mind, and I keep this song playing in my head. Some minutes later the memories of the dreams return.

Another thing that helps is to drink a lot of water right before going to sleep, this wakes me up during the night, and while I'm up, I write down dreams. (I use beverages instead of an alarm, because if I were to use the alarm to wake up 2-3 times during the night, the neighbours would start to hate my guts.)

Ahh dreams. Dreams are as complex or basic as our mind wants to make them, but an important lesson is taught through dreams. This lesson can arguably be called one of the most invaluable of all. This can at least be confirmed by any with affinity to passive 'powers'.

One is not to judge by the appearance, the tell-tale visage of the world, but rather by its substance. Dreams are no exception. You see who she is in the confounds of appearance, clothing, demeanor, that is usually half the whole story and quarter of the real message and a sliver of what really matters. The realm of reality confounded within the matrix of our mind, dreams, they come with a certain "air" to them. One can identify others more accurately not by how they are, what they are, but who they are in the sense of how they feel. The air of the dream will tell treasures troves of what you need to know. Although curiosity must be spiked in what this rambling soul means when the world "air" is presented.

Just as "vibes" can be felt in the world of the waking, our humble reality, can we too feel them in dreams. It is an over encompassing thickness that comes from the atmosphere of your lovely dreaming realm. People too have feelings. Feel her, do not see her, understand who she is. Meditate on her, meditate on not the content of the dream, but the feeling of the dream. Just as emotions are linked to thoughts, the reasons, the information behind it, feelings too are linked to the information behind them. No question comes without an answer, but as a follower of the road of knowledge I must warn you that all too often one answer will only yield two more questions, fours more curiosities, and insight not on how much one knows, but about how much one does not.

Study the feelings of your dream and understand them well for no one can give you your true solution short of yourself, and even revered masters can only serve as guides. Although that is not to imply I am a master of any sort, but to serve as general word of advice. I hope it helps.
