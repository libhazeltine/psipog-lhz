Subliminal
I'm surprised this thread didn't get more attention...Then again maybe not...Anyways, I feel the need to resurrect this thread.

It seems like my family is a sort of epicenter for paranormal activity. I'm not sure why this is. Obviously most of us are sensitive to that sort of thing...But are we all just coincidentally finding places with manifestations, or are they drawn to us somehow? I think it might go both ways. All I know is that I've never lived someplace without some form of activity.

My father's old house was haunted. It wasn't the pleasant kind of haunting either. It was the evil feeling stuff...There was just really bad vibes in that house.

No matter what, it was always cold.

My father had built us this train table. Every once in a while, there would be really loud knocking underneath it...Take your hand and rap your knuckles hard under your desk. That's probably as close as a description as I can give you. It happened even when we moved it.

We heard voices a lot. My mom, dad, brother and I. Each had our own experiences with it.

My brother was up in his room once playing and he thought he heard the t.v. on in the living room, and all of us up talking together. He went out there, and there was nothing. We were all asleep.

I was lying in my bed one day and from my doorway I heard two voices. They sounded like teenagers...A girl and a boy. I heard "hey kid-over here!".

All of us frequently heard each other's voices...Which weren't really our voices at all...

In the kitchen, the cabinet doors would open and shut by themselves. Things would fly off the counters.

There were a lot of knocking noises. Mostly heard in the living room and my parent's room. The knocking started on one wall near the floor, went up the wall, across the ceiling, and down the other wall.

One time my mom was trying to sleep and the knocking started and she said "cut it out!" and it stopped.

My cat lived with us in that house. She kind of went crazy there. She would run around the house and dart her eyes everywhere. She ripped out all her fur that she could reach on her back.

I think that there was at least one nice entity in that house...A little boy. One day we were all sitting in the kitchen and we heard my cat meowing and we look over toward the doorway and there's this silhouette of this little boy hovering on the wall, and my cat was batting at it.

There would frequently be cold spots in that house. It's like you're walking, and it's warm (as warm as it got in there), then all of a sudden it's like you stepped into ice.

When we were little, there was a thunderstorm. My brother and I got scared and ran and jumped into bed with my dad. He had a picture of us on his dresser. It flew off the dresser and smashed into the wall across the room.

We were always so, so sick in that house.

One time my dad was up in the attic. We were getting down our stuff for Christmas. There was only one lightbulb in the attic. It just sort of hung down. It blew up. Literally just shattered.

Of the rooms, it felt like mine was the worst. Just the feeling in there. There was something odd about my closet. I never wanted to go in it. There was always a breeze blowing out of it...One night my dad and I had a dream that we got sucked into my closet. Literally. He woke up in the morning to tell me about his dream, and I filled in the parts. It was really odd. I've never had that happen since, and I don't understand why it happened then..

My brother's room felt the safest. I'm not sure why that is. Maybe it's because he didn't acknowledge it. Or tried not to. If something happened, he always plugged his ears and said "It's not real, it's not real." and ran into his room. They say acknowledging these things, fearing them, things like that. It gives them more power to materialize. That's the only thing I can come up with for why his room felt like a safe haven. We often bunked up there together. Our parents didn't know. I slept on the floor by his bed. Then my parents would come in in the morning and he'd act like I snuck in. Anyways off track.

One night we were up late playing in his room. My parents were asleep. We heard this odd noise coming from the living room. We had no idea what it was. So we go out there...My mom had this cukoo clock hanging above the train table. It hadn't worked in 50 years. No one could fix it. But suddenly it was working. This freaked us out-for apparent reasons. So my brother climbs up on the train table, and he takes off the pendulum. It stops working. We're like okay great. We go back into his room and play again.

Later we hear the same noise. We're like no way...So we go out there and it's working again. This time we take off the weights. It stops. No weight. Clock doesn't work. It SHOULDN'T work...We go back to playing.

Then we hear it again. By this time we're a little hysteric...There's no WAY it should have started working without the weights. So we just took it off the wall and threw it down on the train table. Then the knocking under the train table starts...

Remember this clock. It'll come up later.

One day my brother and I are watching t.v. We start hearing the knocking noises and it scares us. My mom's on the way out of the house and we told her we were scared. She's like well try talking to it.

Brilliant idea, that.

So the little peons we were, we're like "Hello Mr. Ghost...Do you like to watch t.v.? What's your favorite channel?"

Then the t.v. just went all blank and fuzzy. You know, that white static.

Guess it didn't like t.v. >.>

This is only part of the stories from there...

Alright. Weird things about the house (aside from obvious). My dad rented this house. The previous occupants were an old couple. Apparently they just left the house one day. Disappeared. Left all their belongings.

One side of the attic in that house was boarded up. We didn't know why. My dad regrets not taking it down and seeing what was on the other side.

Legends around the neighborhood about the house. There were some good ones.

I'll say the one that stuck with me the most.

There was a young couple living in the house. They had a newborn baby. They smothered it with a pillow and threw its body in the corner of the attic. The side that is now boarded up..


Anyways moving on. I've moved twice since then. When we moved out of that house, we ended up putting a lot of our belongings into a storage area. Later, when we moved again, we went through the storage area. We threw away things that we didn't want to drag with us across the country. We found the clock.

We smashed the clock into tiny pieces and we threw all the pieces into the dumpster.

Now we're in the other house. Been here about a year. Up until recently, a lot of our stuff has been in the garage. Lately we've taken to sorting through the boxes. My dad was going through one and he's like "AH!". We're like what what? He holds up his hand. He's holding the pendulum to the clock. At this point we're pretty stunned. We smashed that clock and threw it away. There's no WAY it could have gotten into the box. Even if we pretend that the pieces somehow flew into other boxes. THIS box he never opened. It was his old cassettes. It's been taped up to hell for years and years.

So we joked. We're like watch. Every box we open up will have a piece of the clock in it.

So we're going through more boxes...

And my mom opens this box, and she picks something up, and she's staring at it. She turns around. She's got the weights of the clock in her hand.

Creepy.

So we've decided, if we find all the pieces of the clock, we're putting it back together.

Anyways, I have tons more stories. But I'd love to get some replies first. So far no one has talked to me. *Cries*
