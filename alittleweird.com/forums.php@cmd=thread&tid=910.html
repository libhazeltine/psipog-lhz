Hey guys, this should've been my very first question for all of you ALW'ers
Author Message
Darkness
Now I know I just posted 3 (counting this one) threads within the same year.....almost...but I really need to know something. I'm not exactly sure if any of you have the same thing as me, but I feel like I have to be there for everyone otherwise something bad might happen to them.

My dad usually does flooring but recently he's needed to do roofing...on airplane hangars....big ones...I was worried at first, but not too much...that is, until he took us to where he does it. I "saw" my dad walking on the roof, and slip. They have harnesses but his broke and he plummeted to the ground. I was pulled back into reality when we reached the restaurant we were gonna have dinner at. I haven't been this worried about dad since his super cramps a few months ago.

Anyways, my grandma, whom I call "Tutu" because she's from my mom's [Hawaiian] side, is about 81 years old, and I worry about her alot. Just like dad, I see her having strokes, or heart attacks, or something really bad happening to her. Whenever this happens, I can't stop it. No matter what I try, it never stops on my command. It stops on it's own. But I still see the images. You guys gotta help me figure out what the hell is happening, why it's happening, and how to stop it...I'll get down on my knees and beg if I have to, just make it stop. I don't wanna see the death of someone I love a thousand times before it really does happen. Until then, peace...

It sounds to me like you are merely experiencing fear. You're afraid to lose the people you love. Because of this fear you are actively imagining these things happening. Why? I don't know. Maybe ask yourself that.

Life is unforgiving. Just as I will die, my family will die. Some sooner than me, some after me. Some by accident, some by old age. This is life.

Let go and accept it, and these images will probably stop happening.

But I'm not a doctor. If you're really feeling bad about these things, perhaps you should speak to a professional a therapist or psychologist or something.
