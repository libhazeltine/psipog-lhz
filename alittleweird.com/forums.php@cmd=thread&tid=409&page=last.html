I think that your ego is the hardest thing to get passed. We need to accept that we are not doing it for them, but for ourselves. We need to understand that we are not doing it to show off or strike fear into people, but for the hope that we can give people an awareness to an unseen world. Zorro
There will never be a "right" time to tell/show anyone and many people will be skeptical no matter what you say and probably no matter what you show them. I agree with Ether, doing it yourself is the only way that cements a belief in it. The only way that I truly came to believe this myself was by doing it, fortunately I picked it up rather quickly or I would have dismissed it as nuts. A lot depends on the person too, if they do not trust you and think that you would pull their leg, well, they probably aren't going to believe you.

In other words, we could all talk to the world about this until we are blue in the face, we could show them a hundred times over, and yeah, a few people might believe it, but until we get them to try it, they aren't going to really believe it.

And as for the ego thing, you have to have confidence, but not an ego. You must know what you can do, but that doesn't mean you have pride in it. It should never be a look what I can do thing, but a look what we can do, a sharing of abilities, but never a display.
