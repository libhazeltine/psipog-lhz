Hmmm, while I'm sure that that is the most likely explanation for this scenario, I do not completely doubt that true invisibility could be a possibility.

I spent quite some time debating with someone about how true invisibility would work back in the days of psipog. I no longer disagree with this person and have accepted it along with my own theory for how true invisibility may work.

The way I see it is that true invisibility might possibly be obtained in one of two ways:

1. The person changes the resonance of his/her atoms so that photons (light particles) are able to pass through his/her body. I very much doubt that changing one's atomic resonsance would even yield this result, but it's probably worth thinking about at least.

2. The person manipulates photons (preferably with a construct) so that they bend around, rather than hit and bounce off of, the person's body and bounce off of a (visible or invisible) "substance" located behind the person's body where they again bend around the person's body and enter the observer's eyes.

The latter may seem a little complicated without a visual representation for some, so here is a more simplified explanation:

The light rays simply "avoid" the person, go behind that person, bounce off of something, and "avoid" the person on the way back to the observer's eyes. The result is that the observer sees the imagery behind the person and not the actual person since the light does not touch him/her.

The theory just described definitely seems very possible to me, while the other theory probably has less than a 1% chance of yielding any positive results.
