Emotions, intent, and all that stuff
Author Message
SheepKing
It seems to me that back in the day I was really excited about "psi". I remember when I first started practicing psychokinesis all those years back (Yeah, I guess it has been that long), and just practicing hours on end with no results, but still excited and still waiting for the second that something moved.

I remember walking home from school everyday imagining myself covered with energy, moving it around, shaping it, playing with it. I'd walk through the hallways projecting images of random things in areas. Clouds, Spiders, Fire... And I got some really strange responses from people.

There was this sort of unyielding desire to be able to do this amazing stuff.

But now; I'll be lucky to be excited if anything psychic happens. It's just not a big deal anymore. Sometimes I still think "That was pretty cool.", or I'll briefly discuss something that happened with someone else. But most of the time, playing with psi doesn't make me feel like I want to keep doing it. If I close my eyes to meditate and practice, I'll get bored and fall asleep.

Where'd the emotion go?

I can see this as a fundamental problem that people in general have. They are initially excited for a while about something, but as time goes on that feeling fades. This question of where did the emotion go brings us back to many portions of our life. Perhaps identifying why it happens, can help identify how it can be solved.

What is an area of your life that you where excited about, that you have you dreamed about... Yet as time passed you slowly lost the enthusiasm?

To Sheep's original post...

I notice myself doing that for a lot of things. My solution was to collect as many "hobbies" as I could handle. The minute I start getting bored with one, I can shift my focus completely to another. Work on that for a while... then shift to another... then work on that... then eventually come back around to where I started.

My current cycle is like: ALW (Lottery -> Trance -> OBEs) -> Programming (codeTank -> game programming) -> Music (Finding new bands -> Guitar -> Piano -> Composing)

Lather, rinse, repeat. Over the years I've become good at a lot of things as a result. I never fear becoming bored in one area, because usually that means something else has grabbed my interest, and I work towards that instead.

~Sean
