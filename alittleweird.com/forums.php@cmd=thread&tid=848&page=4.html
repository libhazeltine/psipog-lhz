@lutasi:

Don't forget to practice correctly as well, because practicing anything wrong can lead to poor results or even harm, depending on the skill. Correctly meaning, something that allows you make progress. Analyze what you just did, and think about how to change it to make it better. That last step usually takes guess-work, but that can be enjoyable, finding new things you can try. Something to keep you engaged.

Oh, and feedback is also very important. "Normal" skills are different from psionics from the very start because you have a direct source of feedback. You can measure things usually from the beginning. Learning to throw a javelin? Lets see how far you toss it the first time, and measure how much you get better each throw. We'll need a measurement stick. Learning PK?(or any psionic skill). Well from the start, it's difficult to tell if progress is being made, because there is no feedback for a while (until the goal is met; you've moved an object, made a psiball, whatever). We need a measuring stick. But how? My suggestion: Sensitivity. An analogy approaches. If you want to break into a metal safe, then would you hammer away at the hinges until they break? Or listen to the tumblers (assuming you're trained in that sort of thing :P) and enter the right combo? Both achieve the same goal, getting the safe open. But one IS quicker. Of course you have to learn to listen to tumblers, and thus must learn sensitivity separately. You can approach sensitivity directly, without having to force your way to it through something else.

So give it a try, there are articles all over that teach it.

And no, I'm not trained to break into safes :P .
