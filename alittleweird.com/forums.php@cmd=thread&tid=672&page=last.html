Depends on what your trying to do with the meditation. None of the meditation methods are wrong, some just have different goals, and some are just different.

For example, if your trying to meditate in order to raise your energy, your not going to focus on your breath (a mind clearing meditation) Youll probably do some sort of visualization, intaking energy.

Though if your trying to clear your mind, youll probably use focal meditation. Meditating on a candle, or your breath. (Most common)

Do wichever fits your specific needs at the time.

Claudius
The basic goal of meditation is so that you can practice staying in a focused state of mind, nothing more. People will tell you different things because there are several methods of achieving this. Some might tell you to focus on your breathing, while others will tell you to just relax and empty your mind. Some will tell you to go to your place of Zen, while others will tell you to focus on one specific thing. It is important to understand that the reason they tell you to do these things is only because it makes it easier for you to stay focused. If you try to think of nothing, your mind will naturally start to wonder in random directions. By telling you to focus on breathing, or to focus on a specific thing, your mind will not wonder as much as if you were thinking of nothing, because you are focused on that specific thing. Naturally, this will get easier over time and practice. Everything takes time and practice to learn, so if you practice meditation you will eventually get better at it. Do not be discouraged by your first few attempts at meditation. With time, you will learn which way works best with you. Some prefer to focus on deep breath, while others like to explore their own little world. Just make sure you stay focused on whatever it is that you are doing. With time, you will improve, and you just might notice a difference in your ability to do other things.
