DigGil:

The gas is spelled "ether" never aether.

Secondly, the Aether concept, referred to as the "Luminiferous Aether" was originally the logically assumed medium for which light (and all energy) propagated. Indeed in the early 1900's, physicists including Einstein refined our theories to exclude the need for the Aether. But note the wording. The Aether was never disproved.

Einstein himself said in the 1920 paper "Aether and the Theory of Relativity" -- "More careful reflection teaches us, however, that the special theory of relativity does not compel us to deny ether." and "To deny the ether is ultimately to assume that empty space has no physical qualities whatever".

It served its purpose by giving us a "fundamental platform" and we grew from that. Interestingly enough, once wave/particle duality was understood, the role of the Aether changed. It being a horribly archaic concept in the modern world, we were quick to dismiss the term. But as we delved deeper into our understanding, we once again saw the need for an "all-pervading fundamental energy" from which reality is supported. Concepts like "the Medium, Zero-Point Energy and the active vacuum" exist today calling forth energy from the "time-domain." Everywhere you look in quantum mechanics, you see reference to scalars and active energies. Realms of potentials and seas of possibilities.

The Aether as is stands is an archaic concept that has been replaced by our modern thinking. Aspects of the Aether were reborn in our new theories and are seeing the light of day once again.

Einstein was a brilliant man, but so trapped was he in his own paradigm of thought, he was quick to dismiss that which he did not understand or could not explain. His theories have carried us far, but they only really refer to the scope of the direct physical. If you research much of quantum mechanics, most theories tear his to shreds and even replace them entirely. He could not unify electromagnetism and gravity, but we may and it will be due to a single unifying framework that this is accomplished; a role the Aether played until it was replaced with something better.

If we have any hope of understanding what lies beyond the physical, we need to look beyond what we think is possible.

Akio:

To expand on your theory, I will turn it on its end to look at it from a different angle.

As I stated before, the role of the Aether was to provide a medium for the propagation of light. As light was revealed to not need such a thing, the aether drifted out of favor. But the aether was not just an idea for the propagation of light. Newton proposed the fluidic properties of the Aether produced the gravity effects as well as regulated the density of matter. Basically the "fabric" of space and time. It was the notion that all space is in this "fluid" that it was assumed that light too propagated thusly. Early physical constants were inferred from the Aether as the "glue" that kept everything together. "Every action has an equal and opposite reaction" was described as being the pressures and stresses created in this fluid of energy.

Today, quantum mechanics suggests a quintessential energy that is regarded as a vast sea of chaotic energy. It has gone by many names, Dark Energy, quantum wave state, vacuum energy, zero-point energy and even quantum foam. Supposedly, this chaotic store of energy is the basis for everything and quantum states describe the order that comes from the directed stress put on this equilibrium-seeking energy.

To bring this full-circle, the concepts that evolved from the Aether make a good foundation for describing what could possibly be the energetic potential in psionics. The mind itself could be functioning mechanically on a level with direct interaction to this energy and through engineering information (thought) this chaotic energy can be engineered to produced defined future states with the changes you intend.

In a nutshell, all classical energy theories are convening at quantum mechanics. Classical physics is doing well to describe the physical universe, but beyond that, especially the time-domain and the realm of the mind, we are creating a new field of study with hopes of understanding what goes on and how to control it.

So many times people wind up disagreeing on a subject but it turns out it's only on the level of semantics and not concepts. Depending on who you talk to (and their educational history) Terms like Aether, Psi, Chaos Energy, and the sort have different meanings. They were introduced to them in different ways and they integrated the concepts in to their paradigms differently.

That being said, I agree with what I think it is you are saying, but I have taken a bit more time to be more careful with the wording. ^__^

I regard "psi" in the same way "wind" is. There is no "thing" called wind. It is just moving air. By that same token "potential energy controlled by the mind" is what I deem "psi."

Psi is a very neutral term but it can get brought in to take the place of other (more appropriate) terms to give the subject a mystique and class that "guards" it from empirical testing.

In any event, most of the data I have (and that's a lot) suggests that the very framework that allows for physicality itself and the passage of time is what allows for our mind to impregnate our thoughts and intent on to our environment and commit changes for future events.

Think of it like this:

Quantum mechanics suggests that something "isn't until it is." and "until it IS it could be anything." Now obviously that's a gross over-simplification of probability and likelihood but it serves the point. ^__^

History describes the passage of "something" through time. The arrangement of information from the past will describe the likely future state of the "thing" in the next frame of time. Now, looking ahead, the further you get into the future, the less exact, and likely, the future states become. The lines of probability diverge hyperbolically on to infinity.

Now, "permanence" can be described as "adequate information describing future states." If you place an object on the table, it is there. There is adequate history to describe it being there and it will continue to be there until action is taken to change that. This is in stark contrast to the photons that we see "pop" into existence from an unknown source. They have basically no history to describe where they appear let alone their trajectory or even their physical properties. They could show up anywhere in the universe. Interestingly enough, many theorize that they appear mostly where we expect to "see" them. ^__^

I don't call the power coming from my wall socket "hydroelectricity" even though that's what it is. It was generated at a hydroelectric power plant using the energy of flowing water. It's just "power."

On a semantic level "psi" is just "energy;" the same energy that is with everything else. The qualifying factor here is that it was catalyzed using the mind. But as you go deeper and look at other energy interactions, even those that exclude the mind, "psi" starts to get watered down.

I have experienced, first-hand, PK done in person both by myself and my colleagues countless times. The changes you cause to the entangled counterpart in your mind will be communicated to the physical object in tandem. Provided there is enough available energy to execute the changes, the next future moments in time will be created reflecting those changes.

Basically, we write "an addendum to the future" that commits the changes we visualize.

This theory has held up to lots of testing though there currently doesn't exist a means to directly observe this interaction.

The key is having enough available energy to fuel the actions.

With grass-roots PK, there is often enough available energy for tiny effects like moving wheels and such. Gravity can be seen as a "potential vector" where being able to affect the trajectory of this energy AS potential, you can use the natural properties of the material to move it.

But most people when moving on to larger things hit a wall. I suspect this is due to inadequate energy for the action.

A small foil psi-wheel has very little friction to impede the rotation, but the metal has adequate density to "carry" gravity.

ErikJDurwoodII
Leverage is about how the energy is distributed, not augmenting the energy requirements.

If a certain amount of energy (work) is required to move an object, then that's how much is needed. Leverage allows you to distribute the mechanical force to require less "effort," not less total "work".

But yes I understand your point. That carries into another point that is very important. Efficiency. I feel that while "more work needs more energy" is a true statement, it can also be assumed that we are not making best use of the energy we currently use. It could be seen as using leverage or other techniques to make more efficient use of the energy at hand.

Inadequate energy could be just "overly wasted energy" in disguise...
