Basically, like he said, theres only bout 4 questions trig related. If you totally suck at trig and have minimum time to study, I would focus more on algebra and geometry kind of things. only go to the extreme on trig if you already have a good foundation with the rest of the stuff.
Basically, you will want to take the ACT more than once. The first time you should just get used to the format and how things go. Youll quickly learn that speed is one of the biggest parts of the test. If you cant get something, you move on. The reading usually has 4 stories you have to read, which is hard to do in the time limit, and the science section has 3 or 4 experiments that ou have to read through, read the results, and answer questions about.
BAsically, reading was my weak point because of this, but science, you can usually just scan it so you have a basic understanding of whats going on, then read the questions and try to find that data they want amongst the rest of the data.

But yeah, get used to reading and finding what you are looking for fast. And then take it again, its worth it to try again.
