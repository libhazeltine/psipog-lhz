I've been meaning to practice the geiger counter for awhile now, and today I came up with this idea to make it a group activity, once again inspired by Sean's newest blog.

I suggest that from today, all the way until NEXT (not this) sunday, you practice the geiger counter daily and independently. Why? Because I want to see what kind of results we can get. I want to see what's the highest we can achieve. Then, come here and chronicle your results.


Now, to help keep people from lying, if you are going to participate, take a screenshot of the highest score you got FOR THAT DAYS practice. If a new number that comes up is higher than the old one, go ahead and replace the current one ( you'll understand what I mean when I explain how to take them) with it. Better yet, you can just take multiple ones of you're fast enough.

Quick How to:

1.) When you have the score you want to take a screenshot of on the screen, hit the 'PrtScrn/Sys Rq' key next to 'Scroll lock'.
(If it helps, your results stay on the geiger counter graph for about ten minutes and you can hover your mouse over a bar on the graph to get a little data icon to pop up.)

2.) Open up MSpaint (or Photoshop if you know how) and look on the left side of the screen. There should be a lot of buttons, and the uppermost right of these should be a dotted-line rectangle. Click that, then right-click the blank canvas and click 'paste'.

3.) Use the same dotted-line rectangle to surround the portion of the picture you want (Otherwise it will be huge and annoying to deal with), right-click, then hit 'cut'.

4.) In the MSpaint toolbar should be the word 'Image'. Click that, and then find 'Stretch/Skew...'. Click this.

5.) A box should have popped up. Replace the number 100 in both the 'Horizontal' and 'Vertical' boxes with a smaller number like 5 or 10.

6.) Now when it's teeny tiny, click the dotted-line rectangle again, right click, then hit 'Paste'.

Now you only have the important part! Save the image, of course.

7.) Now you will need to upload the picture to an image hosting site of some sort. I suggest www.photobucket.com. From there on out it should be pretty self-explanatory. Get the image uploaded then copy and past the 'IMG' tagged link and whulah!


You can organize your own post however you want, but this is how I will organize mine:
