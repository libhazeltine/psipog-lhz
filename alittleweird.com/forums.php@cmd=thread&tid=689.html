Sussch
I was exhausted from a busy schoolday, sitting on my bed and just looking at the mirror next to my bed. When I found that I have been staring at it for 5 minutes already, I thought it was time to go to bed.

I had a dream:
There was the same mirror. Someone appeared next to it and said: "You should already know that having a mirror next to your bed isn't good for you."
I answered: "Okay, lets cover it up then.." and covered it in some kind of blue cloth.
"I don't actually even need that mirror there. Actually, I am the best mirror for myself.."

Then it felt like that someone wished to thank me for this.

The dream faded out and I was drawn into the void (there was nothing around me).
I started hearing some noise. The sound slowly grew louder and it was possible to feel it. At that time the sound reminded me the crunching of snow under my feet. The only difference was that I felt it in my head. The feeling grew stronger and less bearable while moving downwards from the head. It felt like my brain was boiling and my body was shaking.
I then thought: "What's going on with my body?" and imagined myself shaking in the bed with white foam coming out of my mouth. Just in case I reoccupied my body, ran a memcheck, performed some simple calculations and verified that my limbs were still moving. Everything seemed to be working.

After that incident I spent a lot of time googling those feelings. That's how I found PsiPog.

FreedomBud
I'd have to say the weirdest thing for me was almost slipping into an OBE by accident when I was about 10 or 11. I was dead tired after a long day of traveling, and went to be as soon as I went home. It felt weird to me that I felt completely relaxed like by body was asleep but my mind was still very much awake but I contributed that to the fact that I had spent over 8 hours in a moving vehicle (the vibrations from the car). Slowly as I forced my mind to stfu I started hearing things (I heard someone scream '49') but this was a normal sign to me that I was about to fall asleep and didn't let it phase me. The next thing that happened though shook me up literally. My bed started shaking violently, which needless to say scared the everliving s**t out of me and immediately woke up. The only logical thing to me at the time was that the boogey man had come and was trying to attack me so I spent the next few hours hiding under my sheets scared to death.

It wasn't until many years later that I discovered psipog, that I felt the incident bared a striking similarity to the description of an OBE. And now, in retrospect it's probably one of the coolest but weirdest things i've encountered as far as "supernatural" experiences go. I however, have not been able to reproduce it since then.
