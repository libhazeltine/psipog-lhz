...Psionics is the study and/or practice of using the mind to induce paranormal phenomena. Examples of this include telepathy, telekinesis, and other workings of the outside world through the psyche...

Don't understand what I'm saying? OK, so you want to succeed at telekinesis and you start with something small (a psiwheel).You get good results but when you try to roll a pen nothing happens.This is the time to think things out. Get a piece of paper and write out all the things you beleive are true about telekinesis, like, for example:

1. Moving bigger objects is harder.

OK, common sence tells you that it should be harder to roll a pen than to spin a wheel. But wait! We are talking about the paranormal here.In order to roll the pen you need to beleive that it's easier than the psiwheel.And why shouldn't it be? Is it really that hard for your brain to roll a single pen? Even moving your muscles takes more effort than that. Just think about it.And most important, beleive it!

2. Levitating objects requires lots of skills and practise

Once again, you probably think that levitating is really hard.In fact,it's not! (Hell yeah,I've done it).You just need to convince yourself it's easy as fuck and if you truly beleive it you'll levitate your first piece of paper in no time...

I can go on and on giving you examples but by now you should have figured it out - If your beleif is strong, there's no limit to the things that you can do.Remember --reality defines normality :P

So to determinate if that's true you'll try making a a psychic disco ball. You believe it therefore you succeed. Hold on a moment! You actually practiced, or did it.
Now let's have a practical case scenery: You make so you believe then you try. It don't works. You ask yourself about your beliefs "Old vs. New". Now you really start to process the new beliefs at new level. It don't works. You think that your new belief is wrong. Okay: you never believed it deeply enough (usually you don't have concrete hang points to support your new belief apart from "someone wrote about it on the Web"). Still you like to make invisible psiballs and try again. You notice a light brightness. Maybe it's just the light bulb's reflection on the floor, but you did not realised. Wow! let's make that over and over again. You became so fixed to the idea of it flashing that one day it makes light ovals on the ceiling. Great you did it.
What happens is that you practised, but was that the facts? You took time to get used to the idea of a flashing psiball. You got fun and it felt like something more truth worth. It's like homework: you only start actually learning the lessons when you feel your are actually studying... better said: doing something worth.
Honestly I think that we tend to mix "believe" with "faith": "faith" don't needs hang points.

You have to remember also that "feeling" that is the most important.
Then how to know what beliefs are acting on you? Only you know, but try this: Ask yourself something and see what are the different thoughts that pop up, and examine the "correctness" they feel like, having special attention to the first ones: that's what you (or your subconscious) will find most true blocking the other contradictions.

mrj
DiGil, I liked what you had to say, part of what I was trying to say in my earlier post was that ones mind must get used to the idea of some psionic actions. (Although I don't think I got it across well)

Perhaps "practice" is not just in the skill itself, but our beliefs as well. After all, we are learning to think in a new way that applies to the skill. Physical training for example, may suggest you learn about nutrition as something to help you along that road. It no doubt will help, in one way or another, but not everyone has to do it (Michael Phelps anyone?).

Psionics is similar. Whether you believe a space god is giving you your psionic skills, or that it comes from some magic stone, you will certainly have to apply yourself to the task of doing something. Even with a "space god" belief, you can still hold beliefs about how you go about doing something.

What might this lead to? It might lead to psychological techniques to help you handle these skills. Of course if you believe some physical action has to take place in order for things to work, then surely, the mind is what expects it, and may subconsciously (or unconsciously) take action, and produce results.

I still think that practice and belief must intermingle as well as be side by side. This may sound like a cliche "balance" idea, but it is the most I can make sense of it at this moment.

Anyone can have a custom diet, healthy or not, And anyone can have a custom belief system, healthy or not. You can nonchalantly go about and ignore the idea as well, and just go with it. (Though this may be hard at first because many believe they don't know how to do psionics in the beginning)

Even if we are "unlimited" in a certain skill such as this, there may be a setback of sorts. Think of a sport, say...rock climbing. You can go and get better over time by continuously practicing the skill (Ideally). However, can we call it rock climbing after a certain point? If you are able to climb a route nobody in the world can do, and crawl to the top in 2 seconds, can you really call it that anymore? (come to think of it, can you be called human even?) I think, after a certain amount of skill in a skill, that the skill changes, into something we may not be able to do. In this case, "rock climbing" becomes "super strength". Psionics (I believe) has the same sort of limit, although I don't know what to call it once it goes beyond that.
