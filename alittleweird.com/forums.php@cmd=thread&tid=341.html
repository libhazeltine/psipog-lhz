So, this is what I've been seeing lately.

Two weeks ago I decided I needed to start following through with my plans. I realized that it is better to start 'acting' on your instincts to accomplish something then to sit around and try and figure out what to accomplish. I have had a little more energy than usual, and feel like I'm getting a little more things done than normal.

Now; Several days after I realized I wanted to uh, improve my situation, a friend forwards me an email he gets that's like one of those motivational e-mails from some guy who sells, call it motivation. It's about focusing and acting on your goals, and even if you don't have goals, to start acting on what you already know about yourself and what you want. As time goes on, I start doing a little more here and there, and start knocking out the small things I have been putting aside. There have been several small things up to this moment that have made me say "Huh, I've been thinking about this." Or "Hmm, I've had this idea in my head for a little while." And now a blog I read normally (steve pavlina's) has a piece on acting on things you know rather than waiting for the "new great idea".

I can't help but feel like that's a sign saying "You're on the right track."

But the cynic in me says, "Coincidence" or "You're making something out of nothing."

From where you stand, if this happened to you. How would you react?

Wandering Wolf
I support Akio's answer. It is healthier to just go along with it and think positive. Negativity will never do you much good.

If you see one that repeats itself way too many times, however, you are better off ignoring it and saving yourself the torment. I literally wish I had a dollar for every time I "just so happened to" look at a clock when it "just so happened to" read "9:11". Eventually, I just stopped caring about it. I always knew that it probably didn't mean anything because I knew that, if it did, there wouldn't be much of a point of it repeating itself so many times in such an ambiguous manner. Repetitive symbolism is almost meaningless. I'd rather hold onto my sanity than be that open minded.

I even have my own proverb that concerns the act of keeping an open mind. It goes like this,

"Keep an open mind, but not so open that your brain falls out."

- Wandering Wolf

One-time coincidences, on the other hand, are worth looking at in a positive light. Such coincidences will at least give you hope for what could be.

Anyhow, you do sound like you are on the right track, so keep at it.

- WW
