Machine Talking - Egregores, Constructs, and the Soul of the Device
Author Message
Goggle Hat
Occasionally I talk to my car. Not as in, "I am the Ford Focus Whisperer" but more along the vein of "Hey, Roy, let's head to Walmart. I gotta turn in my application." Occasionally, I will also imagine a response based on how the car is preforming and how I'm feeling. Like an imaginary friend, but they have an address. And the address is my car. I've even over time constructed a personality based on quirks the car has. He's prone to getting sick, gets grumpy when he's hungry, likes long trips, will occasionally skip songs on the CD player, ect...

Anyway, the point of this anecdote is that I believe that when we do this, we subconsciously create what the overly scientific group here calls a "Programmed Construct", and what the magical community calls an "Egregore" or "Thoughtform." A sort of energy being, or information creature, attached to the idea of that device.

I wonder if it would be possible to, depending on your perspective on the nature of these phenomena, either communicate with, or reprogram, these ambient ambulatory imaginings. I'd certainly like it if my car, computer, game system, or any other device, would listen to my input, or at least react in a meaningful way before I resort to my most treasured swears.

I'm sure that many of you already have Ideas about the application and execution of this theory. Care to discuss? Personally, I plan to get my car to warn me Before he decides to play up and shut down.
