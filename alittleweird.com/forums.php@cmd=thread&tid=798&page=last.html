Perhaps it's about the mindset that you're in. As soon as you start RP-ing, you try to play a different character and thus get in a different state of mind. If you do this often, you will probably create a mindset wherein you think totally different than you do normally. At first, it is simply a conscious effort to think like the character you are playing, but after a lot of times playing the same character. You just 'become' that character in a split second, so it can seem like that character has thoughts of it's own.

Okay, this is one of the most I-don't-have-any-evidence-to-support-this-things I have ever written. Still, I think it might be this.

joke-lain
I know exactly what Linked is talking about. Falcon is more or less right in what he says. You want to go into detail of your character, and therefore start to pretend you are him/her. There is not actual proof except those who have already experienced it. I am a writer so I work with a lot of characters. Some of my main ones really get into my life and sometimes I swear they are real. I can feel them near, or I can hear them. It is not exactly schizophrenia, its just more of a subconscious thing, you want your characters to come alive so badly, therefore your mind creates them for you, making them real.

There is nothing wrong with it, as long as you can always tell the borderline between your mind and theirs.

This is nothing like Dissociative identity disorder. Sometimes the switch may be involuntary on the outside, but it is not on the inside, and there is no memory loss what so ever.

So, to answer the main question: Yes, you can make your own fabricated characters think.
