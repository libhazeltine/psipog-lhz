I actually did all the math like a month ago because I was bored, wanted to know how much energy I'd release if I'd meet a copy of myself in antimatter... It would basically destroy the Earth and have a lot of leftover energy to cause more damage.

A little about antimatter: As the name implies, it's the negative form of matter. Instead of being made of Protons (Positive charge) and Electrons (Negative Charge), it's made of Antiprotons (negative charge) and Positrons (positive charge). When an anti subatomic particle, like a Positron, meets with it's regular subatomic counterpart, it annihilates, meaning it disappears, along with the other one, releasing all of the energy stored in those two particles (Which can be calculated in Joules using E=mc[sup]2[/sup]) You then just have to convert the amount of Joules released into kilotons and you've got the approximate power of the atomic blast it would create. Luckily, we don't have the technology to create enough of it quickly and even less the technology to maintain and store it, as it would annihilate with it's container. We'd basically have to build a container made of Neutronium (or Nilium), a particle made only of Neutrons, which don't have an electromagnetic charge. (I'm not sure if they have an antimaterial counterpart, so that's my best bet.)

How I learned about it? I read a book, a manga actually, called "BLAME!" (It's a mistranslation, it was supposed to be called BLAM!) It's basically the destroy of a guy in a post-apocalyptic world, trying to save humanity and his only weapon is an antimatter gun. So I looked it up and here I am :P (Actually he has an antimatter gun in the French version; in the English version it's a Gravitational Beam Emitter. I don't know what it is in the original version.)

The LHC is the Large Hadron Collider. It's a huge underground ring built under the frontier of France and Switzerland, near Geneva. It's a particle accelerator, meaning it uses magnetic coils to speed up atoms in this circular tunnel (Which is in a vacuum, so there are only a few atoms there) and speeds up others in the opposite direction. When they collide, they're going at 99.9999991% of the speed of light. Because of the vacuum and all, and also because they are trying to replicate the Big Bang's coditions, it's temperature is maintained at 1.9 degrees Kelvin. Just to give you an idea, the temperature in outer space is 3 degrees Kelvin. When the atoms collide, for a fraction of a second, it's extremely hot. So in other words, it's a factory to create antimatter and try to replicate the conditions of the Big Bang.It's also the coldest AND the hottest place in the universe at the same time, and houses the man-made machine closest to firing an object at the speed of light.
