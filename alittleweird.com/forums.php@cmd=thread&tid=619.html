Well, at least limited teleportation is not forbidden by physics.

There is nothing in modern understanding of physics that prevents time travel.
http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=X02WMNoHSm8&feature=related


Now, lets stand in (absolute) position X on earth and travel one minute into the future.

Now, when we arrive 60 seconds later into that same "absolute" position in the universe, earth below us has moved and rotated in space at the speeds we know.

So, we would have moved some distances along x,y and z axis in relation to earth, and thus achieved teleportation :P

By definition, this is teleportation.
defined as: "When an object disappears from one place and reappears in another."

Edit:

Although it is still relative... Since the subject actually does not move at all during experience, but meh.
