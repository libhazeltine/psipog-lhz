Zeanoth
Therianthropes, which is greek for a combination between a "wild animal" and a human, are known to experience a variety of "shifts". The most common one being mental shifts (refered to as m-shifts) where the therian's mind changes from the a human to the mind of a different animal. Shifts always involve something changing, whether it be the mentality, the aura, the astral projection, etc.

Physical shifting, known colloquially as p-shifting, is the most rare of all shifts and by most therians is considered an impossibility. P-shifting is the full or near full transformation of the physical body from one animal into another. There are also partial p-shifts, in which the body assumes the similarities of other animals, such as morphing of the fingers or eyes.

Hope that clears up a bit what p-shifting is.

Zeanoth
Short answer: Can you think of how many books are written on cellular division? P-shifting would be a process incomparably more complex. The average human simply doesn't have the necessary components to accomplish it.

Long answer: The human body doesn't possess the biological mechanisms needed to shift. For instance, the skeletal structure itself would have to be reshaped into that of another animal's frame. Your brain doesn't naturally know how to do this, but if the neurons were altered, say through a virus or a bacteria, an instinct could be implimented so that the brain does know what to do to shift. Also, the immune system would have to be altered to accept the new DNA and not make you suffer from an autoimmune disease right after you shift. Not to mention the energy requirements would be astounding. Organ and muscle sizes are different as well, and depending on the animal, some organs would need to be created/destroyed (I don't believe humans have poison glands if you were aiming for a snake)

Seriously, very few people believe in p-shifting, fewer have an idea how it could happen, and infinitly fewer (perhaps even none at all) have actually accomplished it.
