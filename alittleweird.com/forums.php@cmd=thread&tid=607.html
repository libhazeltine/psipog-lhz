anisus
It might be a waste of money and maybe time. I don't know if you've ever heard of the EyeQ method, but it's basically a speed reading course. Sort of like photoreading. I actually did the whole program and didn't gain anything from it. What they do is give you a reading test every session and then another one at the end of the session showing how you improved. It was a scam because the first reading test involves a story or information you've never heard about or read before and the end of session test involves a very popular subject or story like the Wizard Of Oz or something as popular. So of course you end up reading the second story faster because you already know it and the graph shows how you improved when actually you're just skimming a story you already know.
Anyway, I went a little long with that, but just wanted to say that you should probably save your money. And if you really do want to learn how to photoread, you could probably find some good books at the library for free. And just one more thing about speed reading, the best way to get fast at reading is to just practice, like anything else.

I know I've not been active for a long time, but I'd just like to say that I was taught this on some sort of gifted and talented summer school when I was about 12... I tried to photoread a French dictionary. And I honestly don't know whether it worked at all or not. Also, the technique didn't seem to be that hard. It was just a kind of meditation, followed by staring at the inside spine of the book and clearing your mind, then holding that whilst you turn the pages. The theory is that your subconscious can take a kind of picture of each page. Perhaps I should try it again.
