Sean
I like to ask about why I feel the way I feel, and what blind spots I have...

For example, in your case, perhaps you might ask, "Why don't I have any good questions to ask?", which might lead to (for example), "You struggle with the idea that there is meaningful truth. The hypocrisy that exists in your mind is that everything is perfect just the way it is, versus the reality that there are certain things a mind must realize in order to better itself."

To which you might reply, "So what is the correct?", and maybe you hear, "Where there are two conflicting truths, each of which seem equally correct, always have faith that both are true to some extent. It is correct that things are perfect the way things are in this moment, and it is also correct that there is a progression of revelations a mind must go through to better itself. You can't think of any meaningful questions because you are attached to the idea that things are perfect now, and applying that truth incorrectly. Things are perfect now as a result of perfect execution of cause and effect. The correctness of this moment is only correct in terms of how it's come about from previous decisions. It's those previous decisions that are imperfect. Incorrect decisions, with correct execution of cause and effect, lead to suffering in the 'now'. Instead of focusing on the perfect execution of cause and effect, instead focus on how you can make better decisions. Then you'll be able to find all sorts of questions to ask."

That's what I hear at least :-P.

~Sean
