Antisankari
Me was having a boring day, so I decided to google out: "How to levitate" just for some shits and gigles.

I found out bunch of more or less BS pages full of people claiming abilities such as telepathy, telekinesis, energy manipulation, and such.

I read through hordes of pages, until one link from an unknown forum led me to a very intersting place. It had tons of articles, active community, and apparently critical yet accepting viewpoints towards the skills. I red the stuff. First came the famous pinwheel, after about two weeks, the bugger started to move after changing a sharper pin ^^. This road led me all the way to small cans and bottles.

Psiballs however were something i was NOT willing to accept. In such a intelligent webresource, promoting such naive things seemed foolish, and almost convinced me that they were sham.

I tried doing stuff for about two weeks without any real results. Until one evening I was sitting alone in my room, watching for a tv-show "Fear Factor". I still remember the very episode, because I had been practising alot before it, and decided just to give it up and watch some tv.

I held my hands the same way as did while making a psiball, but I was mostly interested on stuff that went on in television. I relaxed and kept watching the tv. Suddenly, I felt something VERY strong and tangible between my hands. At first I was certain there was something solid between them. Tingling, buzzing and cold sensations felt absolutely real. I spend some 10 minutes in awe of what I had just experienced.

I realized that if a concept as absurd as psiball can be felt, then there must be at least some phenomenon behind most of the things claimed on the website.
So I begun my studies from the resource we call : "Psychic Students In Pursuit Of Guidanse" or Psipog.

Had the events taken a different course, had I not been bored on that particular day, My life would have been different.

I sincerely feel privileged having been able to study and learn stuff, that a huge majority our people only daydream about! Although the effects I have met have always been modest, or even unimpressing, they still mean alot to me.

After time passed, and the once magical things grew mundane, I lost the perspective on things. I began thinking that EVERYONE knows about this stuff, but just doesn't care. So eventually I pretty much quit practising on a daily basis.
Every once in a while I came back and noted that all the acquired skills come back rather easily. A day or two of intense rehearsing is enough to get back on the saddle.

Now I am just drifting. Trying to seek motivation to really make a "push" for it. However, the amount of effort versus results gained just seems appalling.

Eventually it has came donw to this question: "Is it worth being able to do something incredibly amazing and feeling good about it, instead of using the time to please yourself on a short term quick-reward activities, such as computergames, internet stufing or tv watching".

Nobody is guaranteeing the results. Should I really give it "all I've got"?

And just for a reference, the time-frame of my story is over 5 years long.
