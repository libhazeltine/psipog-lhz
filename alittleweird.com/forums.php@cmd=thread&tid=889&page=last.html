Karma (Cognitive reality)
Author Message
savemydonkey
Hello friends, I'm sure there's already a post about Karma, I just didn't want to look :P

I've recently come upon the theory, for those who believe in Karma, promoting the "thoughts create one's reality" hypothesis.

First, karma is looked at in different ways by the way. Basically it is the concept that the consequences of one's actions will return to them somehow. Some say that karma effects one's position in their next life, some say that there's immediate karma that will return the consequences of one's actions to you their current life, and some go on to say the same, except the consequences will have increased by a tenfold (relaying it somewhat to a "snowball" effect). Those are just a few of the various outlooks on karma.

A way in which it could philosophically support the idea of "thoughts creating one's reality" is as follows:
Whenever someone hits another person with their car, they receive different karma depending on if they did it with or without intent, right? If someone maliciously ran someone down, they'll receive pretty bad karma later on for it. If someone accidentally hit someone, depending on the situation, they will receive a different kind of karma. The only difference in the aforementioned situations is what the person's intent or thoughts were. Their karma will affect or create a situation in their reality (or in some cases, their next reality) depending on what they thought when they hit that person.

What do you guys think?

Notes:
- I'm unclear if "cognitive reality" is the appropriate term for this, so if it isn't please let me know! Thanks!
- I found this off another website which I have been having the hardest time in the world finding again to source, so if you know where this theory came from, please do post! Thanks!
