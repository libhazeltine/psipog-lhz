NewAgeAlchemist
even if the machines didn't change, your odds are still 1 in whatever to win that level prize. Statistics can tell you if its more likely to come up a number thats not been drawn yet or one that has, but it its never certain. The multiple machines really exist more to deal with bugs in the system, rather than complicating the statistics involved.

Without the multiple machines it might be open to error such as the balls in a specific machine having a lean toward specific numbers because say the balls are smaller or others are heavier.

At a certain point it would become more likely for a number already drawn to be a winner but I believe we are many years from having run that many drawings (between 40 and 60% of the combinations pulled is really neutral ground that could go either way). Since statistics include the breathe of extremes from statistical near impossibility to perfect examples at no point is the game ever predictable enough not to be profitable for the house.
