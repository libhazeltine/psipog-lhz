Yeah, it's the same basic Idea ^_^

Think of time as water flowing through a hose, and your perception of time as a hand gripping the hose itself. The more perception of time passing that you have, the tighter you grip the hose and the slower time flows, and the less perception of time you have, the faster time flows.

When you're in class, you tend to keep a constant eye on the clock, you're absorbed in keeping track of time(because you're anxious to get out of class? ^_^). The more perception of time you have, the slower it will seem to go... This is also seen in the saying "A watched pot never boils."

Losing track of time is a little self-explanatory.

To put it in more complicated terms, your perception of time itself controls the flow of time for you ^_^, don't actually picture a hand holding a hose, but rather control and alter your perception of time.

Chillpill: Time is a tricky subject indeed, which is why i am just going to clarify a few things you mentioned.

I believe we are stumbling upon Einstein's theory of special relativity. http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Special_relativity


I'm not going to go over the entire thing because this area of physics is long and very complicated. I'm already having a hard time wrapping my head around this subject.

What you are referring to is called Time Dilation. In simple terms, Time Dilation is when time itself moves slower IN THE PERSPECTIVE of an outside observer. The mass of an object does not necessarily matter.

If we take a closer look at the simple Time Dilation Equation we can see mass of the object does not matter.

γ = 1 / √(1 - β²) where β = v / c v is the velocity
c is the speed of light.

There is another area in the subject called Relativistic mass which somewhat agrees with what you were talking about, but in the case of Time Dilation, mass is irrelevant.

Basically, what this theory says is that closer you travel to the speed of light, the more slower time goes.

Heres a common application.
Lets say you have a twin and you are both 18 years old, born on the exact same day etc. when suddenly, your twin decides to go on a space adventure! He takes off at the speed of 70% the speed of light. When he returns to earth 40 years later, he will only be in his mid 30's while you yourself will be past 40!
If you were to somehow observe him during his trip, you would notice his heart beating slowly. 1 second in his time will seem equivalent to 3 seconds in your time. However, in your twin's perspective, 1 second will be equivalent to 1 second, and his heartbeat will be beating at a normal rate.

That means Time Dilation is only noticeable by outside observer, which then leads us to ask ourselves, "what is the absolute time speed?" or "what if our time itself is being dilated, according to some aliens watching us from far away? afterall, the earth is moving constantly around the sun, which then must mean that I am not in the same spot, but rather moving at several thousand kilometers per hour while at the same time typing this over the internet. But then...if i'm not really moving anywhere, according to most people, what speed am I really moving at and OMG brainfog @_@
Gotta love science :P

but anyways, as for any practical psionic use for this, i'd say there isn't any, unless you can somehow manage to move yourself close to the speed of light, which is next to impossible last time i checked.
