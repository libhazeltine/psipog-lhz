Eldibs
And over here, it's partly cloudy with a chance of freezing rain.

Seriously though, I do something similar from time to time, but less complicated. If my hands are a little cold, I'll fill them and surround them with energy, which I'll program to be warm, and have it follow my hands. Not very effective, but it does have a certain novelty to it, plus it does give me a chance to practice making warm psi-balls. When I'm actually having trouble with cold weather though, and find my coat isn't enough, I'll clear my mind, blot the cold sensation out of wherever it's hitting me, and then completely ignore the fact that it's 10 degrees outside ("there is no wind, there is no cold..."). As long as I can maintain my concentration, it's incredibly effective (for me, anyways). Back in college, I used it to get back and forth to class through a foot of snow without a coat, noting that I'm used to the hotter Texan climate rather than cold, wet Missouri, if that tells you anything. Of course, I wouldn't recommend this for extended periods, as it'll likely lead to frostbite/other coldness-related nastiness, as it's more of a palliative (getting rid of the feeling of the coldness, rather than actually warming up) than anything else.
