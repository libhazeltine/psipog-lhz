The 'Closed-Box' Condition of Psychic Studiers
Author Message
cinderwild
For awhile I have thought about this and I decided it was time to voice my opinion. I don't know if I'm simply projecting my own feelings onto everyone else, but from when I first found out about Psipog I was very lazy about training. Why? Well, I was a regular daydreaming kid. I had other things to do, and while psychic phenomenon was what I felt I cared about most, it was more of a daydream idea than something for me to actively pursue. I didn't feel like I could.

The second thing that discouraged me was the condition of the community. Sure, the articles were written by friendly sounding people, but once I had read them and saw the terms they used I thought, "Really? That's it? " Psychic phenomenon was mysterious, and here I had these people who weren't even that developed themselves (by my super hero influenced standards) saying that it was this way.

Then I visited the forums, and that's were it got old. Most of the forum goers were just like me: Little kids, or much worse, people who thought they knew everything. Everyone had a theory, and plenty of them really thought ONLY MY THEORY IS CORRECT. To be honest my entire experience there is pretty vague, but it led to my still current habit of just skimming over theories and such that people have because I didn't feel like they knew anything at all.

(SKIP TO HERE IF YOU DON'T WANT TO READ ANY OF THAT)

My point is, I've been with ALW since its inception and I see a lot of limiting behavior. Psychic phenomenon is by far one of the most mysterious things that we have, and guess what we as a community say about it? We say it is a tangible concept. We say it's possible! But so many people say, " Well, you can't do it to that extent. " Why, because you can't?

This isn't meant as rude, but how many of these skeptical ( I realize I'm a skeptic too!) people who immediately shutdown someone's fantasy actually have tried their hardest to develop these skills? Are you still practicing and trying to get better, or have you reached a mental climax where you just say, " Oh well, can't get any better. " and start telling everyone else they can't too? How many of you who are making up theories have spent more time developing the skill than the theories?

Well... there's a balance. Surely, open-mindedness is important. But at the same time, you can't be so open, that you entertain every wild idea with complete seriousness. The truth is that there is only a finite amount of time, energy, and resources in the world. So, we have to prioritize.

Now, if I prioritize things differently than someone else, then that's ok. If someone else wants to spend time and energy on trying to fly, or teleport... go for it. I personally think there are better ways to spend your time, but it's your time. And if you succeed, then I would definitely be interested.

But what realistically happens? A kid becomes excited, sees a movie, and thinks that if they just "BELIEVE!" then they can become a super-hero! I have a hard time taking these people seriously.

But sure - if that kid is successful, then by all means - bring forward your evidence! I will be very interested in seeing it! But that's the big if.

The evidence I've seen suggests that there are limits. Now, I am open-minded to the point of allowing someone to show me otherwise. But I am not open-minded to the point of ignoring all the evidence I've seen, just so I can indulge someone in their fantasy. I believe my approach is realistic and balanced.

~Sean
