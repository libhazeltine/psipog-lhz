I absolutely agree. There is always doubt in one's mind,
especially with the psiwheel. For me, moving a covered
psiwheel was a bit of a turning point as far as belief goes.
One must also constantly be testing for false positives.
For instance, I've given up using plastic straws altogether bc they
are very susceptible to static electricity and I was getting some false results.

Ice is better than the psiwheel in that respect, because
one practically eliminates the wind factor. Let's not think
that the laws of physics stop applying just bc we are doing
something a bit unusual. That is definitively not the case. With
psionics we are able to cause small movements in small objects
subject to low friction. We can usually act on the way the object
responds to the Earth's magnetic field (the Northern magnetic
attraction) but rarely are we capable of escaping the main force
which is Gravity. Legit levitation vids are extremely rare.
