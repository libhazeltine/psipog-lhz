Dash and Kube, it depends on the system of magic you are trying to teach. To use myself as an example, the system of magic I teach is pretty close to Psionics, since it starts with basic mental control, and gradually moves to more advanced subjects. Below, I tried to point out the greatest similarities and differences between them.

Similarities:
- In the system of magic I teach, I focus a lot on achieving mental control, 'direct magic' as some might call it, meaning one should not use rituals and the like at first, ritual should only be done when it can be understood completely, and once the basic occult faculties have been trained sufficiently.

Differences:
- The focus on spiritual development, opposed to just gaining magical powers. In the system of magic I teach (which more or less originated from original Hermetics), we incorporate working on the 'balance' of the character. We believe that the greater the balance of the character, (which is called the elemental equipoise, though there is too much to be said about this subject to deal with it here,) the greater the abilities of the magician.
- The use of a spirit-energy model instead of pure-energy model. The difference between those two models is practically that the spirit-energy model (Hermetics) allows the magician to evocate spiritual and astral entities later in his training, whereas the pure-energy model (Psionics) uses constructs at best, and only raw energy at worst. (Though some might disagree, this is what I generally see as a major flaw in Psionics.)

Let me make this clear, people WILL be divided if you let them practice two different systems, Psionics and Magic. The question raises, however, where is the line? Can we draw a clear line between them, or do they merge (partially) into each other? So, it's up to you, depending on what your goals are with this school. If it is primarily, as stated before, trying to revive Psionics again, then I personally think adding Magic to it will not be a good thing to do. If there is something else you are trying to achieve (as well) however, then, who knows, adding Magic might just be what you need.

~Falcon
