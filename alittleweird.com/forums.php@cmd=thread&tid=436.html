Psionics and Religion
Author Message
Amaroq
This particular theory of mine was once expressed on Psipog, but my posting of it quickly got the topic locked. Since religious discussion is allowed here, I'm gonna post it here for consideration.

What do most religions seem to have that psionics does not? Deities! In almost every religion you look at, there is always an all powerful being, or in some cases, a central source of power.

Meanwhile, believers in Psionics practice that power is everywhere and that you can manipulate it with power of will.

Are these two really so separate from each other?

When a child is laying in the hospital with a terminal illness, chances are her family is there with her, praying to God to save her.

What if this child was dying in a hospital, and her family is instead trying to heal her?

What's the difference? Does praying to God make you any more or less likely to be successful than drawing upon your own power?

What if the family praying to God is actually using psionics without knowing it?

Why do we have gods? They are someone we can look up to. Someone we can look to for answers. Throughout the entire course of intelligent civilization, we have always had deities.

How did they get there? How did they come to be? Why does every culture have a different one? Why do they all believe in their particular deity? Is theirs the right one? No, that can't be. Else there would be no others.

What if our early ancestors created the idea of a higher power to try to explain the magical unknown of the world to them. The deity made this happen. The deity watches out for us.

What if they unwittingly created a psionic entity from their collective belief. An entity that really does watch out for them and take care of them, provided they believe strongly enough in it to fuel its power.

What if there really is no difference between the family praying to god and the family gathering their own power? What if God is a psionic entity that christians created with their belief in him, and who tries to grant us what we need with the power given him by our collective belief in him?

If this is true, then there is no such thing as a right or a wrong god. No true and no false god. Every culture really does have their own personal god, in the form of an individual psionic entity that their collective belief created.

If these god entities rely on our belief in them to survive, then surely they must be weakening as more and more people lose faith in them, only praying to them when they need to use their power towards their own personal ends. And with the rise of devil worshippers (Actual people who actually worship satan. Not to be confused with pagans.), is the christian god being weakened even more? Is there now a competing Satan entity whose power is used for evil influence rather than to grant us what we need?

So many questions arise out of simply stating this theory.
