Haha... I thought this would find its way here eventually... Highlight the following white text if you don't mind spoilers. If you want this to remain just a series of mysterious and creepy videos, don't read what I wrote...

Marble Hornets is fake, guys... It's a series of videos relating to "Slenderman". Slenderman is a monster created as a contest entry on Something Awful in which you had to create a creepy image or something like that... At about half the thread, the entry for Slenderman was posted and then he slowly took over the entire thread, becoming a full-blown urban legend.

It started with regular images and somewhere in the image someone would use image editing programs to add in an extremely tall man with several appendages wearing a suit. Sometimes he stood on two long legs, sometimes he was supported by the appendages. Kinda like Where's Waldo...

He's said to lure people to him by hypnotizing them somehow (<--- I think that's what I remember reading...) and also to be responsible for many disappearances (Let the "The mutilated bodies of [insert number] children who were said to have disappeared last month were found in the forest near [insert location]." flow; that's what usually happens in the stories...)

Marble Hornets was created as a creepypasta (Creepypastas are creepy stories, videos, etc. for those who don't know...) relating to Slenderman.

All this can be confirmed by looking at entry #4... At one point, you see a man with no face (Like Slenderman) wearing a black suit, walking just out of sight when the cameraman jumps at a movement behind him. You can also see it on his balcony in Entry #1... (They're the only 2 that don't have any sound...)

Those who decide to actively look into Marble Hornets either don't know this or are just in it because it can get pretty creepy.

EDIT: Here's a fun fact about Slenderman. Slenderman was the nickname of William Clark, some kind of threshing contractor from the UK. In March 1877, he murdered a man named Henry Walker and was then hanged at Lincoln Castle, Lincolnshire.

I found a biography by the 3xgreat-grand-son of a man named William Clark, born in Lissington, Lincolnshire, but the dates (Put in psychiatric hospital in 1866 and dying in 1886.) and cause of death (Suicide by hanging himself at Lincoln Castle.) aren't the same... Maybe one of the sources got the dates wrong, but 2 on 3 say March 1877.
