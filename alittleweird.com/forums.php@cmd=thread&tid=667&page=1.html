Help! Story of my life, help me get past hadephobia, and fear of unknown.
Author Message
Saiya
Sit back, long story.

Well let me start off with my life. I'm half Japanese, half African American, and born in Japan. When I was five, my father, missing his family (and out of a military job), I was forced to move to America. Since I was born I grew up in a strict Christian home, where I was forced "Do this, do that, you'll go to hell.". I often times wonder what my life would be like if I were Shinto. However, I was a protestant, better than being catholic I guess. My parents were zealots, and naturally, I began growing up as a zealot as well.

A close-minded Christian child who couldn't stand people bashing it, and would defend it with a heartbeat. My only hint of doubts began, when my life started to turn to shit. and my thoughts about Christianity began to contradict my fathers. I believe this happened around the time when my father became a pastor, and started his own church. My father was a tyrant to me and my older sisters, treating us very unfairly. I won't go into that right now, but my father has always had serious anger management problems. The only people I've talked to about these doubts were online, as I could barely trust any of my real life friends to understand.

One, happen to be a wiccan, and a good friend. He didn't really push his beliefs on me, and I still go to him today for wisdom. The other, happened to be a Christian. I was encouraged by this person, but not pushed, to keep trying Christianity. I don't know which of the two next events came first, but one, I would talk to my wiccan friend. We would talk about the astral plane, a subject that was totally new to me. I couldn't see him as a liar, so I did what was forced into my mindset. Assume that his astral projection was an influenced vision by satan.

However I asked him how he entered the astral plane, and he told me "You let your body sleep, but keep your mind awake." The next night, out of curiosity, I relaxed all my muscles till I couldn't feel them, and tried to stay awake, with my eyes closed and trying to see beyond my sight. Suddenly these vibration overcame me, and I felt this amazing sensation like I was floating and falling at the same time and I began seeing a bright light. It scared the HELL, at of me so I "fell back into my body". This creaked open the closed door of my mind, however, I still used the devil as a scapegoat. I told my wiccan friend about it, he wasn't suprised at all. I told my Christian friend, and she was very... Skeptic. Now the other event is that is when my hatred and agnst for my father began to grow, so I didn't go to church one day. He would glare at angrily as we talked that afternoon, I believe i said "grounding me would be pointless" and he immediately spoke of my punishment. I sprung up enough guts to speak at least a little of my mind that day. I told him "You are a liar..." Suddenly my dad lunged at me, pushing me through the door behind me into the enclosed corner of my small bathroom.

He basicly started hitting me across the face in a defensive manner and shoving me against the wall. Yeah, a well built African American grown man, WHO WAS A PASTOR, beating up skinny (I believe I was 12 years old) kid. I begged him, pleading him, to stop, telling him I'm sorry and crying. Not only that, but once my mother finnally pulled him back and I ran to my room, my father threatened to KILL ME, if I called the police. That day I tried to run away from home, however I my mother saw me jump out my window and she immediately told my dad and sisters. My sisters chased after me and my dad yelled at me to come back as I was half way down the street with no shoes on, and a domestic violence phone number in my hand. If it wasn't for my sisters, I may have just ran, but my father problably would have caught and stopped me anyways. When I came back me and my father talked in the living room, he justified his beating me up with "Maybe that's what you needed". I felt like getting on and punching him across the face at that point. Anything afterwards in that day, is now a blur to me.
Now soon later, I talked to my Christian friend online, I told her "Where was god when me father was hitting me across the face huh?!", I pretty much exploded, as she couldn't justify me with an answer. Soon enough, we stopped talking. When I told my wiccan friend, he only sympathized for me.

My faith in Christianity quickly began to dwindle, yet somehow I still believed in it. About a year later, I decided "I'm not going to believe in a religion unless I believe in it, and not because it was forced on me." That's when I stopped being a zealot. My wiccan friend hadn't spoke with me in quite a while, and he told me I grew and matured a lot, not just in my beliefs. As I often asked god to show me proof of his existence, and nothing ever happened. Half my mind believes in Christianity out of fear of hell, the other had serious doubts, and often times was even against it. A long while later, last year. I haven't had a successful astral projection to my name, yet I still believed in it, though I feared trying. I met a friend online who I found out knew a lot about OBEs and the astral plane and the sort. He grew up in a strict Christian home too, and 15 was the age he stopped believing in it. Amazing, because that's exactly how old I was, (And still am).

It was infact only a few months ago I believe, that he put my world into an amazing perspective. One where right and wrong are so clear. One where I don't have to be afraid of hell. However, since it was forced into me, it was like a sixth sense to fear it. He recommended me to books with excirsizes for OBEing like Robert Peterson's. HOWEVER, for the past few months, I've been trying to astral project, but it's become very difficult for me. I rarley start to get that spark of vibrations, and even when I get the SMALLEST spark, my breathing starts to become heavy, and it disappears. Not only that, but I still have a fear of hell and demons and fear of the unknown, it's ALOT less than before, but still large an irrational. Not to mention ironicly, somewhat a fear of the supernatural. Especially ghosts (I could never sit through horror movies.) So that's my story. I've left out some interesting events, but I think I've written A LOOOT already, and this isn't some autobiography. xD

By the way, I'm 15 and still living in my parents house.
Also I'd like to know about your astral projection experiences.
