Antisankari
Well, we could say that for human there is no other life in the universe.

60 light years would pretty much be the theoretical maximum for us to explore with the fastest possible speed (300 000 km/s).

First, 60 years of travel. Then 60 years for signal to reach earth.
Assuming that we actually could move at the speed of light.
And assuming that the crew would be sent as infants and die within years from their arrival. So 30 lightyears might be more suitable.

If we could only travel at 1/2 of the speed of light, the area would be reduced to 15 light years. And even that is alot! 1/10th speed of light would again cause 15/5 = 3 lightyears to be our practical limit. (Assuming the same 30 years / one way trip that is somewhat doable physically. Psychological factors are a whole other game)

And that is nothing in cosmos.

Does not help either that distances tend to grow exponentially between every interesting target.

So there could be life out there, but there is no way to see it, hear it, or communicate with it. Now we have argument in our hands that is very similar to problem with religion and God. We believe something is out there, but we cannot really interact with it.
