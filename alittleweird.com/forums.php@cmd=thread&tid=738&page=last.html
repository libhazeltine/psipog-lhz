[In My Opinion]Why psionics is being hard to teach
Author Message
DigGil
I think that the human communication by speech (words and sounds) is being each time more ineffective and inefficient as we try to advance in areas of highly 'abstract' or mind related like learning/teaching psychic abilities. That's my theory to explain why people learn these things at different rates, why there's the infamous Analytical Overlay, why there are so many techniques and beliefs (and why they work for some only) and such. Things that require experience (not talking about trial-and-error), certain specific-first-hand knowledge, understand the handling of emotions/thoughts/senses, etc....

The problem is that words and speech are too ambiguous and general (without speaking of the lack of enough concepts because of the inability to describe them). For example (a practical one):

There's a cake.

Then there's a cake.

When you read "cake" the brain, depending on people, may handle and/or associate that word as a bit of information only (without any kind of anything than the "impression that's a cake"), a picture, a smell, a sound (why not?), a taste, a touch, an emotion, a special feeling, a memory or a mixture of them.
Then when you read the "There's a(...)" a different processing approach cames up.
This is a lot of trouble already and we should bear in mind (among other things like the belief system) that however the "cake" is being handled it will be handled differently even from people whose brain uses the same approach to the word "cake", for example: Person A reads "cake" and imagines a Strawberry Pie, but Person B reads "cake" and imagines a Chocolate Cake.
When you see the picture of a "cake" there's a lot of less ambiguity: "It's made of chocolate!" Person A says.
But does Person C notices that's a cake? Does he even knows what "cakes" are? Here we have what I've been talking about: past experiences, notion, knowledge, etc....

How to overcome that? I've once made a thought experiment:
Two people are in a room. Person A is teaching Person B how to make psiballs.
A says B to channel it's energy (or psi) to his hands and concentrate it there. B says "WTF?".
A tries a different technique: "imagine your energy in you, and then imagine it flowing to your hands (blah, blah)". B asks: "How is that energy?", "Where does it cames from?", "Blah, Blah".
So, again, A tries some technique improvements: "Make all that as you feel better, follow your instinct". This may lead to B thinking he is doing it right when, he's only pretending stuff. So where's the intention, sensing the realm of psi and the real work of understanding and "talking" with the subconscious? Unfortunely A don't knows how this works or how to explain this, he just does it. But it still does it!
What if B already knew empathy (the telepathic kind) and telepathy? That would be a lot more easy! A could make kind of a virtual room with his imagination and project all his emotions, intentions and everything else to B, because he would 'feel' all that.

Back to our Cake example (hope you are not hungry :D):
A writes down cake for B to read, but this time tells him to feel his notions of "cake" before reading. If B's successful (supposedly) he'll say (in this case): "The grandma's fluffy-yellow-great-tasting cake! From 2005's my (Person A not B)birthday.", then he could reply: "OMG, I felt how good is that nasty cake!".

At the end he learns how to appreciate grandma's cake and how to make psiballs because he "saw" the gears of a machine working, not the buttons that make the gears rotate behind the plastic case (if you know what I mean).

The inefficiency of speech is like this large post for some simple concept. The ineffectiveness is using expressions like "just knowing" where there's no actual word that directly leads to understanding what I'm talking about.

This is a large post, but it would be shorter if in pictures, sounds and "just knowing". Thanks for reading (if you made it).
