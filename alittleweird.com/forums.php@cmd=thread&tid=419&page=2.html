If your looking through someone, in the same way you duplicate their image, and it appears as if theres an outline of their body with the solid part being a layering of the two images. Part of the problem with color is a thing called synaesthesia, a good number of people have it in some form, it causes you to respond to stimuli with unrelated sensation. Commonly its known for people tasting a shape, smelling a color or some other odd association. A fraction of those will see their own emotional responses to a person as a color or feeling near or around the person (generally it pertains to known people, but it seems to me not out of the question you could cultivate it to work on a level with judging a book by its cover, a mix of trained observation of people and first impression).
