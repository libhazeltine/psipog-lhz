Sean
The problem you mention in your original post is something that really gets on my nerves.

I believe the issue exists because - even though psionics is real - a lot of the stuff we use to measure psionics is pseudo-science. I don't mean that in the derogatory sense, I mean it in the literal sense.

Psionics is very hard to study scientifically, according to the scientific method. How can we measure if you're correctly making a construct or not? How can we measure if your dream was really predicting the future? How can we measure the validity of the emotions you feel from your neighbor?

These things are very hard to measure scientifically. Even with knowledge that these things are real, that still doesn't solve the problem of measurement.

So... as a result... some people see this problem of measurement, and try their best to do what they can. Other people don't see this problem, and just think psionics is "whatever you want it to be". Want to create a shield that opens a non-existant energy portal in your left ear? Sure! Go ahead! No one can deny you anyways.

The solution is to find ways of scientifically measuring psionics. The geiger counter is a great start, but there's so much more to go. Only objective measurement can distinguish between the loonies and the legends. If we stay in the realm of pseudo-science, then we'll never be able to distinguish between those rooted in reality, and those in fantasy land.

~Sean
