It is hard to really define the actual line that is between empathy and telepathy. Any psychic interaction between to people isn't always clear cut.

A problem that many people have is they define "telepathy" as "Hearing someones thoughts." Which isn't entirely correct. The better definition of telepathy, (my reference is www.m-w.com ) is:

Telepathy: communication from one mind to another by extrasensory means

If you use that definition you are not limiting yourself to simply 'emotion'; or even just 'verbal thought'. You are encompassing ALL interactions between another persons mind to your own. This can include, in my eyes, the transmissions of thoughts/emotions/physical sensation/dreams and what have you. Maybe it should be called telempathy :-P

Empaths who feel vs. ones who just know... I believe it really depends on the person. So, I'd say yes there are 'two types'. However, at any given moment someone can be either type. It's not something set in stone. I myself fluctuate on how I perceive another persons emotional state from 'just knowing', to actively feeling.

It is a useful ability and if applied right you can develop very strong relationships with people based on your intuitive knowledge of how they are feeling. Just about every girl I've had a significant relationship has told me, " You know me so well, how I'm feeling, what I'm thinking. I don't know how you do it. " This is direct application of psychic perception and my general good nature/intuitive intelligence.

The most important thing someone who wants to develop a sense of emotions for someone else should understand is that you must be able to discern your own emotional state before you start trying to listen in on someone elses. If you do not it is easy to confuse the source and be in doubt. Another important thing you need to understand is how to APPLY what you know about how someone else feels to either help them, persuade them, calm them down, or to just get the most out of knowing what you know.

ryan
Many people believe they have empathy, when in reality all they can do is "notice".

When we see people do things that we normally do when we feel a certain way, we might begin to think that they are feeling that way too. Most of the time, we are right, because people are people, and people usually have certain ways they go about expressing themselves.
That is not empathy.
That is, actually, a very closed minded way to interpret people's emotions, because we are assuming that everyone else expresses themselves the same way as we do. Not a very "out of the box" perspective.
Empathy IS a very "out of the box" perception.

E.g. If we see someone tearing, sniffing their nose with their cheeks turning red, we might assume someone is sad, because we might do that when we are sad, however they might just be cutting onions, or they might have allergies.

Don't get me wrong, I believe people can successfully perform empathy. However, I'd prefer to see empathy happen while someone is blindfolded, with their ears plugged and with someone they don't know. It CAN happen. Just people aren't always doing it.

EDIT: I realize saying this might sound discouraging or skeptical, but I do believe in Empathy 100%, I just don't believe that what everyone is calling empathy is empathy.
Don't call a kick a punch just because it hurt the same.
