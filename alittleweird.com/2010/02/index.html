February 14th, 2010 by SeanOBE, Dead Guy

Had an interesting experience this morning at around 7am.

I had fallen asleep on the couch earlier in the day for 4.5 hours (from 7pm-11:30pm), so my sleep schedule was a little out of whack.  As per usual, this helped to induce an OBE when I finally got to bed the second time around 5:30am.  The OBE wasn’t intentional, but I knew it was likely, because of how I restless I was.

It was fairly normal at first.  I floated around my house, played with my vision, inspected my body parts, etc.

At one point, I decided to yell out for “someone”.  I do this randomly in my OBEs.  I’ve never gotten a response… but I don’t really see the harm in trying, so I do it a lot when I get out.

Usually when I try to talk, I use the same method as when I speak in real life.  I use my mouth, and say things.  For whatever reason, I decided to try another technique this time around… I specifically focused on my thoughts.  I focused on clearing the gibberish in order to deliver a nicely focused non-verbal message.  I thought the message “hello? anybody there?”, with a specific type of mental clarity/wavelength.

I then continued on my normal process of playing around in my environment.  Sort of like when you walk into a dark room, and say “hello?” — you don’t really wait or expect a response… you just say it and move on.

To my surprise, I got a response.  “Yes, hello?”  I was blown away.  It was an older gentleman.

I tried to remember my technique, and started asking questions: “Where are you?  Why can’t I see you?  Can you see me?”

“I’m right over here… Yes, I can see you.” (I still couldn’t see him.)

Me: “So what are you - a dead person or something like that?”

He took a moment to think.  “Haha, yeah, something like that I suppose.”

Me: “A spirit?”

He kind of sent a confused shrug/nod.

At this point I was starting to get really excited… I started formulating a strategy.  What would be the best thing to ask?  Who he was when he was alive?  Or tips for how to move around while out of body?  He struck me as just a normal person, not like some spiritual sage or anything crazy like that.  Imagine visiting a foreign nation… would it be appropriate to walk up to a random stranger and start grilling them with questions?  But I should certainly ask something, or do something… Or was this some sort of dream?  Should I do something that I can verify later?

By this time, my thoughts were all over the place.  I think in the midst of all these thoughts, I heard him say he was leaving… so I focused on sending gratitude for taking the time to talk to me.  I couldn’t really think with clarity from the excitement, so I hope he got the message.

I returned to my body.  I still am debating about the best thing to converse about.  But at least I know how to talk correctly now.
