Measuring Psi Activity
Author Message
FreedomBud
Getting bored with my normal pk practice, I decided to try something different. Since I seem to have a knack for knocking computers out of sleep mode when i'm practicing, I decided to try to influence my computer in a more controlled manner. My first few attempts to make the keyboard type a letter and make the mouse move proved to be futile. I then had the idea of holding the mouse in my hand and try to influence the optical laser by means of psi.

You may be thinking that this would produce erroneous results due to the highly sensitive nature of optical mice, and I would have to say that you are right; but that does not mean it can't be used to simply provide quantifiable results of psi activity. There was some movement that I know was just created by physical movement, but as I learned to negotiate for this, I could hold the mouse without it moving. And, then, when I started getting into the right mind state the cursor would start to shake and move around the screen. To me it is proved further by the fact that when the mouse went into low power mode and then I tried to force it to move, it would light up and start moving; I have even been able to accomplish this without even touching the mouse, but simply have it turned upside down.


So with this, I encourage other people to try this experiment to see if it does work, or if I am just going crazy :P because with all experimentation comes trial and error.

Some tips that i've encountered while experimenting: My wireless mouse did not seem to be as responsive since (my educated guess) it takes move movement to take it out of double low power mode. However please do not take this tip as fact, see what works for you; and also realize that this in no way proves the existence of psi, but rather my be a way to measure it more precisely. I'd love to hear back from anyone who has tried this.
