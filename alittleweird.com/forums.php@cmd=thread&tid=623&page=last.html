Over the past week or two, I've been having a lot of thoughts. Mostly of bunch of philosophical stuff that I've been wanting to let out. So, if you'll just sit back in that chair of yours and listen, I'll continue. Who knows? Maybe you'll be entertained, learn something, or maybe just fall asleep.

Okay? Good. Now, recently I've been thinking of this: "Why did I want to learn psionics?" Lemme give you some background information. I discovered PsiPog not too long ago. I was bored and was on the internet. I'd always had a belief in the paranormal, so I started searching up more information that I hadn't already found about telekinesis. Eventually, I found a site hosted on www.freewebs.com that was about telekinesis (I forgot the name). Reading through it, I thought it was stupid because the author sounded REALLY fluffy in the way they described things. Luckily, though, they started talking about psi wheels. Interestingly, they had a picture that they claimed that they had forgotten where they got it from, though, because it said the picture wasn't available because that site had last been updated in about 2004, by me right clicking and following the link, I got 404'd on PsiPog. Me, being curious and wondering what PsiPog was, erased the extra stuff on the URL. And thus, I learned. And I learned it well. In about 2 days, I had made a psiball, moved a psi wheel, and read just about every article available. To cut a long story short and not bore you with the details, I got here today, typing all this.


...you still awake? Good. So... let's discuss a new thought. "What am I going to do with these skills?" It took me a long time to come up with the answer to this. I decided to think of this in a different way to get the answer. "What am I good at?" So I thought about it and found it: controlling others with telepathic suggestion. No joke. It's fun. Look at a non, think to them, "Move your head a bit to the right." I smile every time it happens (it happens a lot). So I thought about it and found out what I should do with it: create mind-slaves. I'm evil aren't I? Now, this probably won't happen for a while (it probably will happen about the same time that I can prove psionics is real to doubters by using telekinesis to force-choke people while saying, "I find your lack of faith disturbing."), so I'll be practicing until then. Don't be surprised if one day you think, "Gee, I think I'll stop what I'm doing and go worship Raathuus." Muaa-ha-ha-haaaa-ha-ha-haa-ha-ha... *cough*... *cough*.......

Well now, where was I.... oh well. It seems the Fountain of Thoughts has run dry. Maybe I'll think more stuff later...

Well, I've been thinking for the past week and 3 days, and I've got more thoughts. This post's topic: Why We Should Keep Psionics Fairly Select. You might be thinking right now, "What?" I'll explain.

Did you ever look around and want to show everyone what you can do with psionics? I did. But luckily I didn't show off. Instead I thought of what would happen by posing a hypothetical situation: What if someone went on a popular televised news station. They then showed off some telekinesis quite well. Then what? There would be controversy. Skeptics would go after every slightest thing to attempt to debunk them. Religions (particularly monotheistic ones) would call it the devil's work and ban it. Some people would go crazy about it and want to learn how to do it. They learn, and more follow. Many have learned, and thus some of the crazier use their newfound skill as a way to commit crimes. As more find this effective, governments make efforts to ban any practice. Some become infuriated and go against the government in a rebellion. Chaos follows.

While this seems quite worst-case scenario, it could happen. Least-case would probably be the whole event goes unnoticed by the people. And while I'm all for working with others, try not to show off to random people. I say it's also okay to say "I can move stuff with my mind" to people you know, because if you don't intend to
show them, they tend to think you're joking, which is okay by my standards.

Besides, if everyone does it, it's not cool anymore.
