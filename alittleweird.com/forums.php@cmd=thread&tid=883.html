Hey friends :)
Here's a BBC Health article to read that I think will interest some of you guys.
It is called "Phyiscal Training in your dreams"

http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/health/1668247.stm

In spite of the title, there's nothing really about dreaming or physically training in one's dreams, however there is a relation between the topic and the title.

For those who don't want to read a boring BBC article (although I suggest you read it anyway to gain your own interpretation of its message, rather than just accepting mine) it says we can physically train our muscles just by visualizing we are physically training our muscles. Doing so uses our brain to fire up motor neurons and causes actual results (In a 10 person study, a 12.5% increase was shown in muscle mass, just by imagining flexing one's arm as hard as they could over the course of a few weeks). There were some stipulations that this type of training would not replace flexibility training and aerobic exercise, but I definitely wouldn't refute getting a little buffer solely because of my brain and neurology ;)

So, any thoughts? Opinions?

About the video link arzhul posted:
http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bjBFw95GCCs
I don't want to be adversarial to the concept or topic of the video, especially not to the power of meditation and the mind, however the example the practicioner uses in the video in particular is very questionable, and I will tell you why.

As you know, the human body is adaptive and can be very flexible. Really, any time that somebody stretches out a muscle, after breathing and letting it relax some, one can stretch out just a little further (progressively) because, in a very simple sense, you stretched out the muscle (which is why one feels strain when they stretch out). Meditation and chi ghosts are not required, and in fact when I was in high school, it was a "phenomena" that one of my track coaches introduced our team to, which we were able to do without such meditation or chi ghosts on a regular basis.

The practicioner in the video seems to be profiteering, perhaps unknowingly, on very fundamental capabilities of the human body. Also, it should be noted that although there are no comments criticizing his video, he also moderates the comments allowed to be viewed - strong concepts allow criticism, while he does not seem to.
