I agree on your other points. But the whole core of the matter is that materialism is an un-disproveable theory itself. You can’t call up evidence in either direction in these matters, because evidence is only just what it is: a certain fact (or it contains certain facts). Only when you attach an interpretation to it, it gets a certain meaning. But that interpretation has to happen within a certain context of thought, and on the basis of that context of thought lies a certain assumption.
Let me give an example: Modern research in neurology has shown by brain scans that there is a very strong correlation between certain cognitive functions and activity in certain regions of the brain. This is a fact. When you want to interpret it, when you want to attach meaning, you must start assuming. When you want to assume materialism, you can conclude from this fact that the brain is the cause of these cognitive functions, that it creates them. When you want to assume for example such-and-such form of consciousness-matter dualism, you can conclude from the same fact that the activity in the brain has in such-and-such way a connection with consciousness.

By the way, these assumptions are in 9 out of 10 cases hidden assumptions, you have search for them and make them explicit in yourself. They often show themselves in “yeah, but this just seems like the most logical or realistic option to me”. What is at the core of that “most realistic option” feeling, is that it correlates with your whole view of reality more than other options. And your view of reality is an assumption, a belief (unless it is a direct perception within a thought context that is completely free of any assumptions, but that is a whole different story, and that would make this a very very long [and off-topic] post).

I’m also not saying materialism is a bad thing in itself, that completely depends on what you want out of life. If you want to know the truth about the nature of reality (which a small subset of the population, including me, seems to experience as their single goal in life), then materialism is a bad thing, because you are not finding out the truth about reality, you are finding out the logical consequences of assuming materialism.
And yes, if the core of your thought to reject materialism is “I want a free will so I reject this theory”, that doesn’t make much sense. Then you are doing the same thing the materialist does, you make an assumption first and then start thinking. Only you make the opposite assumption. You should make no assumption and see things for wat they are, which is a nice and solid beginning (at least, if truth is what you’re looking for).
