January 2nd, 2009 by SeanAre We Real?

If all my decisions can be traced back to the automated processing of my subconscious… do I ever make real decisions?

Why do I even exist?  Why am I even aware of this moment?

Evolution, psychology, sociology, chemistry, physics… these disciplines outline a very clear message.  I can see the fear laced beneath the surface of the scientist’s dissertation.  The message is that we don’t exist.  Everything has been accounted for.  We know who, what, when, where, how.

It’s not that I don’t see beauty in this system… I see amazing beauty.  Numbers can do amazing things; I get it.  But they’re still just numbers.

…

Here’s a fun exercise: identify something you feel is sacred.  Maybe God, or music, or poetry, or a religious text, or reproduction, or relationships, or family, or perpetual motion, or secret government, or morality, or correctness, or cleanliness, or technology, or whatever.  Then, immerse yourself in it, and learn and experience everything about it.  Push yourself to the very brink.  Do not deny yourself any piece of relevance.

What will you learn?  The object itself was never sacred.  It’s perceived sacred-ness was a projection from the inner workings of your own mind.  There is no such thing as “sacred”.  It’s just your mind.

Everywhere I look, all I see is mind.  This computer is mind.  This music is mind.  This air.  These feelings.  I cannot escape mind.

I know I am not mind… but I can’t not use mind in order to search for myself.  And I can’t not use mind to express this.

The point is this:

Mind is not real.  By extension, reality is not real.  I’ve been chasing my shadow, for a glimpse of my face.  Is Nothing worthwhile?  Will Nothing satisfy my thirst for existence?

Ramblings.

    Apologies about the length.

    I believe this is a nature of consciousness question, relating to your last post regarding Uncertainty Vs. Determinism although presented in different context.

    So what is consciousness? How can you determine that *you* are in fact existing and perceiving REAL things?

    Answering this question poses a big problem. A prerequisite for an accurate description of a system is that the observer must place his or herself outside that system. This means existing outside our own universe looking back in is the only was to gain accurate knowledge of what is actually taking place.

    But lets explore consciousness in a quantum context and see if it is not possible to satisfy that requirement and thus deem our observations legitimate.

    I don’t wish to delve into a lengthy explanation of quantum physics but what follows will be infinitely easier to understand with some quantum background. If you should find yourself confused, go get some background and things will begin to make more sense.

    The brain is as extraordinarily complex as it is efficient. You would be hard pressed to find another system that can control such mass and allow such processing capabilities with only the tiniest inklings of electrical signals. These characteristics create the perfect environment for quantum effects to manifest, and are in turn amplified by the brains immense organizational powers i.e the ability to visualize 3D objects, predict trajectories, etc.

    Before going further, let me propose my interpretation of quantum superposition. Superposition indicates that a particle is existing in all of its possible states simultaneously. I (and I am certainly not alone in this proposition) propose that a particle in superposition is existing this way because of its ties to other universes. That’s right, I believe in the multiverse.

    Now lets present a scenario. The electrons of a neurological signal are in superposition, their orbits exist in a cloud of probability incorporating every possible future outcome all of which will very soon be played out in another universe, save one for our own universe.

    The signal is recognized as such by our brain, say it is a signal sent from the visual cortex carrying information about what our eyeballs are picking up. When processed the wavefuctions of each electrons superposition collapse to form a definitive signal, they do this in our universe as well as their counterparts in all other universes. Our brains do some insanely quick calculations and place an image of what it was we saw in our heads.

    So what happened? What does this mean? Well for starters we successfully satisfy our original problem of needing information from outside our own universe in order to legitimize our perceptions. The very nature of a wavefunction collapse facilitates this information exchange that we required. Secondly however, we also learned something about our own perception of reality. What we perceive is slightly different than what is actually there.
    Quite possibly, the wavefunction collapses of the electrons containing the data about the location of an object were collectively processed to perceive the object a tenth of a millimeter from where it actually existed. In NO universe was the needle perceived exactly how it existed, that perfection is saved for platonic forms (some would call this perfection “God”, but as to the nature of this perfection; I politely suggest looking to eastern religion). And by this logic, I agree with your statement that all men strive for a sense of that sacred platonic perfection; the closer the better. Even though our perception is not perfect, they can be said to incorporate data from outside our own universe thus giving us the ability to deem them real.

    This in turn poses a new question of whether we have any control over the wavefunction collapses going on in our heads. Can these probabilities (Alas, our behavior is not pre-determined) be somehow manipulated by our own brains? This is a question that neurologist may one day be able to answer, but judging by my experiences with synchronicities as well as other psi phenomenon, I would venture to guess that we have that ability.

    ~PotentiaL
