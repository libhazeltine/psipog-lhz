seraphimfire
Well, cinderwild, that's difficult to explain. I actually can't really explain it. I can only tell you what happens.

My face.....well, physically changes. And more or less, I can control it. I've taken to calling it the Goddess Effect. It's actually sort of funny, sometimes.

So, on one hand, the physical changes make me....well, very pretty, physically. When it first began to happen, when it was at its strongest, I used to have people stare and point at me, people telling me how beautiful I was, wanting to take my picture. (Awkward!) When these changes, I would say, regress, I look more average, I wouldn't say ugly or anything, lol, but I definately do not strike people the same.

I've come to think of it as a sort of metamorphosis, because, as the years have progressed, so has it. I regress less and less every year, it seems.

I hope I don't sound crazy, lol. But it has made socializing hard for me, absolutely. People see me one day, and of course they expect that is how I look all of the time. When they see me another day, in what I call the regressive stage, they're shocked, scared; they don't what to make of it. So they generally avoid me or call me a witch (sigh). I've grown used to it, though, so no hard feelings on that account.

So, yeah.......My daily life is very bizarre and hard to deal with sometimes. But after five years, I've come to accept it. I can't imagine being any other way anymore. It's who I am now. No matter what I look like, I am still the same person, every day, "stare-worthy" or not.

I came here because, as insane as my story sounds, it's true, at least for me. And for anyone who's seen me for more than two weeks, it's true. Unless, of course, they ignore or deny it, which I believe most people do, to cope. I was hoping I could find anyone who might have had the same experience. I've always felt alone in this, and I imagine I can't be the only person to live with such a thing.

Anyway, sorry if it's confusing. I'm always confused because of it. :)
