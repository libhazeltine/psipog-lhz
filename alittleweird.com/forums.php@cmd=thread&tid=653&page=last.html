I'm sure everyone of you had and has nightmares now and then, me too. They often were that terrible that I woke up screaming with my heart beating like wild. Bbut lately this all has change dramatically.

Since 1 or 2 months I have a lot of nightmares, well i think you can call it nightmare when werewolves are hunting you, giant insectes are attacking your house and you are stalked by demons. The special thing with this nightmares is: I don't fear them, its more like they start to fear me.

The werewolves, were not hunting me, but a girl who was running to my flat and beating on the door. I let her in and shut the door. The upper half of the door was glas so I could look outside and waited for something to happen. then 3 werewolves can running and jumped at the door. All i did was watching them until they ran away.
The giant insects were beaten up by me through usage of a flamethrower and,...I guess it was a rockt launcher or a laser gun, can't remember correctly.
The demon (This nightmare I guess was caused by wathcing The Exorcism of Emily Rose) lurked around my home and some slightly strange things happened, like the door to my room wouldn't shut, some time later I caught that bastard and tied him up with silver wire (watched Underworld the day before, so I thought he had a Silver-weakness).

Has something like that ever happened to you, that you were not afraid of your nightmares?
Before someone askes, I haven't been lucid in any of them. If I was then believe me the nightmares would have been over easier

Sussch
I haven't had scary nightmares for many years.

For some reason I have stayed totally calm in every dream. I had the most amazing "nightmare" after playing the Silent Hill series - my calmness amazed me even while I was still dreaming.

Maybe it is because of watching too many brutal films or playing too violent games?

The scariest nightmares I have even seen were the ones, where I knew I had to climb up the stairs, but gravity was playing tricks on me (it was constantly changing direction) and the stairs never had upper steps. It was an impossible task, but I could never stop climbing up and falling the whole way down again. This type of nightmares used to scare me for more than a single night..

It was then that I started to practice waking up just before the scariest moment. It has given me the ability to wake up when I have to without using an alarm clock (of course, if I'm not too exhausted). I have not been able to have any lucid dreams though.
