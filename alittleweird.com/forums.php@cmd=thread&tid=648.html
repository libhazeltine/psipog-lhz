Antisankari
posted by joke-lain:

As for the Parallel Processing/Programing, I have never heard of it before. I would love to hear more, though.
Easiest way you could describe its goals would go along these lines.

"If you have a housemaiden who cleans your house in one hour, How would you organize 120 housemaids in a way that they could clean your house in 30 seconds?"

Its not easy. If there is only 1 mop, and 1 bucket and possibly just a single broom, they need to be very well organized. Otherwise some maids will wait for some others to release their resources for others.


On the other hand, you must do two calculations that both take 1 second to perform. With a single core, you would first calculate the first one and right after you would do the other one. With multicore processing you can actually have one core performing the other calculation while the other core is doing its own one.

Naturally it only works if the calculations do not have any dependencies.


This is the near future of the computing as they have almost given up on silicon based single chip based development.
