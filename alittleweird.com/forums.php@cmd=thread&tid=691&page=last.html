Instead of getting a concussion, I floated down, and was even able to pull up a bit. Through trial and error I discovered that I could get higher and higher by swooping low and then pulling up, and I repeated the process until I landed on my roof.
Strangely undaunted, I leaped off, a soared away.

I have had countless flying dreams. Many of which are very similar to f3lick's and Sheepking's. I have never thought much of dreams, and still don't. Even if the dream relates to an emotion you experienced during your day, or is playing with the happenings in your life--so what?

If the dream is only messing with the past, it doesn't help us at all. We were there; the dream is only reviewing what happened in a strange and twisted way, if even at all. If the dream said something about the future, however, then they would be slightly more useful. Some people believe they do speak of the future. Still, my thoughts on this is negative as well. Even if a dream spoke of the future in some way, usually the dream is so twisted and confusing that you would never be able to make sense of it anyways.
