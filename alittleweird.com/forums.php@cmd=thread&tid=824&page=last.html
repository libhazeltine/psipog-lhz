Non-locality of the universe, willpower, wavefunctions...
Author Message
AngelsFall
I have a personal theory that, as we are extensions of the universe [by being conscious parts of it], our wills mean something. Think about it; we're just a collection of atoms that isn't really that different from any other collection of atoms, except for the fact that our entropy is very, very low compared to our environment... but we're conscious nonetheless. Anyway, my thoughts are that willpower has something to do with the collapse of quantum mechanical wavefunctions [although I don't have any good ideas about how to connect this to decoherence yet]. If this is true it could explain magick, which would simply be collapsing wavefunctions from probabilities/superposition into a definite manifestation, one which isn't very likely and hence it seems like we're acting at a distance to manifest a reality which also isn't very likely [such as, I dunno, setting things in motion such that it ends up with you getting money from someone].

I know magick and accounts of it are muddied by hindsight bias and the extreme subjective nature of it, but I think I'm onto something here.



The universe isn't local, and I think that points to a very deep unity that people unwittingly exploit.
