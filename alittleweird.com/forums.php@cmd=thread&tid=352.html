Well, I started kung fu and Qi gong/Tai Chi classes a while ago, and I've learned how to manipulate energy from a chi perspective probably a lot more than I did from a psi perspective (and I studied psi for..almost eight years).

I use chi mostly in my kung fu classes plus my daily training a lot like what you would see in the videos above or with Bruce Lee, like you mentioned.. though I find it's also pretty useful externally.. occasionally when I find out one of my friends is having a bad time, I take a few minutes to 'pour' my own energy into them to comfort them and help them think more clearly.. and lately I've been looking into it a lot for physical healing purposes.

As for as chi goes with affecting my energy manipulation in general, I have to say it's helped multiply my own skills much more than what they were before, especially local sense and empathy.
For comparison.. before I started Tai Chi, I could "feel" a slight tingle of other peoples energy when I walked by them, but after a year or so of dedicated practice, I started physically feeling like I was walking through a.. "Thick Soup" of energy, thoughts, and emotions everywhere I went o_o
(That got pretty annoying though, so I had to start shielding from it a lot xD)


In the end..It's helped so much and emphasized the connection between energy manip and other abilities(Like TK), that whenever someone comes to me to learn and asks where they should start to learn this ability.. or that ability..(anything from pyrokinesis, to phasing and AP), I always tell them to meditate and develop their ability to manipulate and control their energy as much as they possibly can, not just as a jumping off point, but as a fundamental practice that they should continue to practice through their entire path in psi or chi.

It really does seem to have a base connection to everything, and like it shows in the videos above, your own abilities can become increasingly powerful the more dedicated you are to it.

Hey guys, a great martial art to check out would be Aikido. Like other martial arts, it focuses on drawing from one's personal power, but rather than psi or chi, they call it "ki" (hence, aiKIdo).

In Aikido, they say to draw your ki from your "hara", or your center of gravity, through deep breathing, balance and concentration. Your hara, for most people, is just above your naval.

Any input on this?

Zectra
I took Aikido for about four years, and I'll agree with chillpill on this, it is a great Martial Arts to take.

It also depends on what you're looking for in your training though.
Aikido is a soft form Martial Art(Don't mix that up with "internal") with the intent on manipulating your opponent and their weight in order to throw them off balance with the littlest of energy. They also throw some Kendo into the study to help you become better attuned with handling weapons; receiving or dealing.

Oh-Sensei developed the system a long time ago by studying different martial art's an their joint manipulation techniques and such, as well as through his own study, then adding to them and expanding on certain qualities.

Like a lot of Martial Arts though, the key points to it's system can be strung back to origins in China and Kung Fu.
I've seen at least half of the techniques and ideas expressed in Kung Fu and it's Chi Na(Qinna) areas.

It'd be a good idea to look into both Aikido and Kung fu;
Kung fu giving a large variety of insight on both hard and soft form, while Aikido specializes in soft form and weapon disarming.

Coupled with Tai Chi, the three make a great combination.
