I honestly have no clue. I was raised atheist, turned christian in my middle school years, and now am...agnostic-christian? I believe in a God, and believe that the morals taught in the New Testament are the 'correct' ones, but I'm not a devout catholic by any stretch of the imagination. With that all in mind, I think that the meaning of life is to balance the freedom to choose of those around, the happiness of those around you, and your own happiness, in that order. You do what you can to make other people happy, while still giving them choice to make themselves miserable if they so choose, and all the while making sure that you take at least a minimal care for yourself. As for the afterlife, preserving your soul and bringing new sheep into the flock, idk. Seems like a kind God wouldn't threaten us with hell.

Don't intend to start a religious flame war or anything, you people seem level-headed enough for that not to happen, I just don't think the topic at hand and my faith can be seperated.
