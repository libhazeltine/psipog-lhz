Okay, I've had enough of this. I'm glad I found this board so I can get input on my attempts at lucid dreaming. I think I found out why I forget the vast majority of my dreams. They are all horrific nightmares.

I've always been intrigued by the idea of lucid dreaming (I have had only two, both by accident). I know that the first step is to remember your dreams, but this has always been super hard for me. I can only do it probably once every two weeks, and everytime it is a nightmare.

I go up, and she is having sex, I know this in the dream, but I cannot see or 'sense' what. After which, she is cold and emotionless to me. She demands in a flat voice that "you should hold me because I'm cold."

I guess it might not sound bad but I'm almost sick to my stomach. This kind of thing happens every time. Do you think it's because I'm forcing it, or do I really dream like that all the time? Any advice or ideas would be appreciated.

Teradonus, here's what I think you should do.

Recalling dreams is merely a part of the process. I'm sure you've read many things on the topic. Dream journals, etc. I would highly recommend this. But I'd also recommend something else.

Reality checks. But kindof... modified for your situation. The best way to overcome this in your dreams through lucidity is possibly this way. Because these fears are, as seemingly stated by you, unjustified, unlikely, and unlike the real life you live in, you need to embrace the dreams you've had. I would say that by thinking about them, even aloud, about all the bad things that happened would be enough to get gears turning. Upon thinking of these things, you must rationalise that they are not reality and not things that are likely to happen. You must further state to yourself (again, in your thoughts or aloud would also help) that you must be dreaming when things like that happen.

That should be more than enough to help you become a littlem ore rational in your dreaming and realize it's all just a, well, dream. :D
