Good morning (12:44 AM) Everyone,

I stop in occasionally and peruse the forums (Sheepking is watching, scary huh?) and just thought I'd drop a few lines to you all.

You may or may not know that I'm in the military (special forces drop out all the way)and thus find myself very busy/occupied building up my future goals for when I am out, working, and staying healthy.

I think about Psi, memories about the communities, and all the friends I've met along the way. I recall how things evolved from me being a regular on the forums at Psipog to becoming an author/moderator, some of the events that led to falling out and falling in with Sean, and just the whole circus that was.

I think about my perspective on Psi when I was a kid (13? 14?), when it all started for me and these awesome ideas I had about what Psi could do, and the whole magical feel it had. Times do change. At 22 years old my perspective is much different than it was before; things don't "excite me" as much as they used to. Things became familiar, and then boring for a time.

Occasionally my interest is renewed, as is how it normally goes, by some sequence of events, boredom, or strings of coincidence that make me wonder (is something calling to me?). Instead of preaching some false sense of confidence that this time is different, and declaring my intentions to me part of some grand scheme to reorganize things I'll offer some insight to you that I have learned in general.

Insight:

1. Never stop practicing. Day in and day out find the time to try and use your skills. If you have trouble finding applications or ways to practice you have a serious disability if you wish to pursue psi. Your ultimate goal should be to integrate your psi skills into REAL LIFE situations.

2. Goals. Form goals, short term, mid term, and long term. Assign dates and deadlines and hold yourself to these. Without these goals you can not see if you are advancing or retreating. Any person or organization who wants to go anywhere needs goals.

3. Grow. Always seek to expand yourself, your mind, and help the people around you to do the same. Without growth you go no where. Explore your ideas, document them, catalog them, learn from them.

4. Truth. I consider truth one of my highest, if not the highest belief. Truth is the ability to see reality as much as possible. This is not about life from your view point, it's about life in general. Imagine as if you were omniscient and attempt to see things from that perspective, understand how everything plays out, and how everything fits together. Our minds put us at a disadvantage of sorts, and society conditions us away from Truth. Detach a little and look inward and outward. Remove self deceit, recognize failures, speak the Truth.

5. Research. Find new sources of information; Find documentation and ways to support or disprove yourself. Do not be biased.

6. Balance. Balance your time, set quotas of some sort to focus on the things that are important to you. How much time do you or did you spend on online communites, vs. how much time were you practicing, reading/researching, or trying to understand reality and psi? The last year or so of PsiPog I socialized more than I practiced, expanded my mind, or grew in some way. This also applies to life outside of Psi. Exercise, Live healthy, Socialize, and damnit... Get laid.

7. Change. Do not allow yourself to stay a constant, unless that constant is change. Evolution requires trial and error, change, mutation. Change is the only way to evolve.

8. Push. Push yourself everyday to achieve more and more; Want SOMETHING. Go for it.

----

The above insights are some of the things I use to push myself each day.

I still practice Psi, and can see how profoundly it has affected me, my social life, and my ambitions. I credit the people like you who have defined my social interactions online, and who have challenged me to go out there and do something supernatural.

There is so much potential here. Psi and Otherwise. Do not limit yourself. Catch you around :)
