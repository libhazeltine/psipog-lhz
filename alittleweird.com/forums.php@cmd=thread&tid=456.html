This reminds me of a theory I have about house flies. Ever notice that when you're not actively doing anything offensive to them, that they hang around and annoy you? Then as soon as you pick up a fly swatter, the fly is nowhere to be found.

I guess this theory is really that all animals, and our cavemen ancestors, had some instinctive form of psionic abilities that protected them, helped them hunt successfully, etc.

Back to the flies. I remember reading about a type of shield that is designed so people do not notice you. I theorized that flies had some sort of similar mechanism that would trigger when someone was intent on killing the fly.

I tried an experiment. There were probably a dozen flies in the gas station I worked nights at. They would annoy the hell outta me, but they'd all disappear when I grabbed a fly swatter. The experiment: I picked up the fly swatter as usual, and as expected, the flies that had been annoying me could not be found. I focused on the idea that the flies were psychically diverting my notice, perhaps with an instinctual shield of some sort. I then focused on 'overpowering' this, by concentrating as hard as I could on finding the flies.

Not only did I find and kill the one or two flies that had been annoying me, but I continued and found and killed at least one or two dozen flies around the store. Even when they flew away before I swatted them, they could not escape my detection anymore.
