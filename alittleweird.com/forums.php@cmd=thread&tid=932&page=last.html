There is a state called Bose Einstein Condensate, in which atoms have gone through an identity crysis and act as one. Liquids closest to Bose Einstein Condensate (superfluid helium) have been achieved by cooling them to near-zero Kelvins. Supersolids aka solids, in which atoms move frictionless and thus are able to penetrate other solids, are thought to be Bose Einstein Condensates as well. Supersolids are only a theory without any sound scientific proof though.

In my personal opinion, we don't necessarily have to cool atoms in order to relax them and make them all work as one (or vibrate with the same frequency).

Before studying Bose Einstein Condensates in school, my father had been explaining his theory on phasing almost the same way (he had accidentally phased his thumb through a car door and was looking for an explanation). He compared the state to a cloud of mosquitoes that usually fly quite randomly. However, he had noticed that sometimes the cloud of mosquitoes kind of synchronizes its sounds and starts moving as one entity.

Twitching sometimes comes from practicing psionics too much, in which case I would guess that trying to stop it just by concentrating on it wouldn't work. I think it's more like twitching muscles - you just need to rest.
