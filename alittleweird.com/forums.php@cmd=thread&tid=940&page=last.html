Well, I've only had 2 OBE's, one was questionable and the other not very remarkable. But I've read enough to explain to others.

1
In an OBE, your consciousness is somehow projected away from your body. Some authors note that in the most "low level" form of OBE your surroundings are the same as the physical world. There are dimensions other than time, x, y and z axis, a good amount of physics depends and would not function without this. Try looking up a "hypercube" (geometry, not cinema) so you'll get a feel for this. It is then understandable, that if your consciousness is not tethered to normal spacetime, you might be able to move in "new directions", but this could be false... in any case, you are where you are, trying to explain it would only make it more complicated.

2
Look up "sleep paralysis",

3
If a blind person projects they can still sense what's around them. According to "Astral Dynamics" (book) the author was actually MORE sensitive than usual. Only being able to see is probably a side effect of us more and more putting the other senses on the sidelines, food will usually taste and smell good and we are usually sure enough it isn't spoiled so we don't pay as much attention to it as we should.

4
From what I've seen (saw this happen to someone) it's usually the case that the person is already very sensitive and/or project without much effort. This is usually not the case with most people, so I wouldn't worry about it. Being stung by a wasp is much more likely and from what you wrote would have similar effects.

5
was it night time when your friend tried it? Night time can already be pretty scary, especially if you experience something like "projecting out of your friggin body" for the first time.

aaand that should be it, 2 months and a week after you psted the question :P
