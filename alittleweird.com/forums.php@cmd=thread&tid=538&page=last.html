Sean
Most things I believe in because I've seen it with my own eyes, and confirmed I wasn't self-deluded. It can be hard to confirm whether something is real or not... but what is even harder is to take all the things that have been witnessed as real, and start coming up with an explanation for them :-). That problem is light-years ahead of just witnessing something bizarre :-).

So I'll go down the list...

Psi Balls/Constructs - exercises produced results. Confirmed I wasn't making it up in my head via getting others to feel the construct in my hand, and throwing it over a digital thermometer (and watching the temperature go up). Advanced constructs produced "unexpected" results, which is evidence that my expectation isn't what's driving the constructs... some experiences outlined in: http://psipog.net/art-advanced-constructs-log.html

Empathy - lots of experiences where a reading led to interesting predictions, followed by the prediction coming true. I could name a few, but they are emotional, and personal :-).

Telepathy - not as many experiences, but a few stand out. Sending images in the PsiPog practice room ( http://psipog.net/art-images-with-subc.html ). "Hearing" a friends voice, followed by them saying exactly what I just heard. One interesting experience (that convinced my father I wasn't insane), was one where I was away from home for a week. During my time away, I connected to my father. I closed my eyes and saw a silhouette of a person laying down in front of me, and it felt like I wasn't at my father's home. Upon returning home, I asked my dad, "Did you sleep at home on Tuesday night?" Upon which he replied ".......... Nooo.... why?" :-) I was correct in my perception, and freaked out my dad in the process, because he was keeping it a secret from me :-).

Clairvoyance/Remote Viewing - overlaps with telepathy.

OBEs - hundreds of experiences. One in particular is what convinced me that what I was experiencing wasn't purely a hallucination. http://www.psipog.net/blog/2005/11/awesome-obe.html Since then, I've had a few more that have re-enforced that conclusion.

By extension, I believe a lot of what Robert Monroe, Robert Peterson, and to a lesser extent Robert Bruce have written about OBEs - because my experiences align with theirs a lot, so it's reasonable to assume that as I keep going, I will continue to experience similar things that they write about.

Psychokinesis - not a lot of personal experience, except for the psi-wheel, and a little bit of wobbling of pens. Not enough to personally convince me of anything. However, seeing the geiger counter results with JoeT, and just having a relationship with JoeT, NI, and Float, has convinced me of it's existence.

DMILs - tried it once, and succeeded. Didn't really interest me enough to keep going, and my experience made sense (it doesn't even seem "paranormal" to me), so not a big deal, imho. Basically used visualization to alter my body (bigger bicep muscles, at the time, without exercise).

Precognition - I have a hard time with this, because it overlaps with my empathy. I can make some accurate predictions, but to me, it seems like I'm just playing out the emotional landscape of events, and coming to an obvious conclusion. I've read some books claiming that what I'm doing is actually precognition, but my belief system won't allow me to admit it to myself :-).

Anything I missed?

~Sean
