i learnt a breathing technique which i find quite useful for meditation. its called the ocean breath.

before you start doing the ocean breath, inhale deeply, and when you exhale, breathe out through your mouth like the way you would when you fog your glasses or a mirror.

when you get the hang of this, try to inhale and exhale again, this time with your mouth closed. you might hear some sounds coming out from your mouth, and it sounds like the ocean.(thats why its called the ocean breath).

i personally find that whenever i do the ocean breath, i would 'sink' into my subconscious state very fast, and so my meditation feels much better.

Okay then...

While in meditation, rather than deep, calming breaths, you want to take a little shorter breaths than you would on when acting normally. keep your mind focused, but relaxed at you breath. Then, while keeping your breath relatively within the same region, shift your focus towards your eyes. At this point you may feel a pain in the third eye area, this is normal, but even at that, prolonged periods in this state is not recommended.

The Idea here is to keep your meditation strict and precise, rather than a deep trance. It is to be kept in mind that the Alpha brainwave is all around a feeling of alert calmness, so the normal approach to meditation often will lead one to break from ones alertness.

Well, that's all I've got for that. I hope it helps, by the way, you planning anything for this or just want to know what it feels like?

(note: you can do this meditation with your eyes open if you want)
