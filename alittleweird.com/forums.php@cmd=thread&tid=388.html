What would 'they' have to gain?
Author Message
funkydeath
Lately i've been thinking about what knowledge 'they' (the government or some other organisation) actually would have to gain or even lose if they told us the truth (that is if they haven't already). all the conspiracy theories about the alien autopsy, roswell, the Bermuda triangle ect. they all state that there is something that that the government or some-other body is keeping from the public. my question is if it is true what would be the point of keeping it a secret. For example lets say for sake of argument that the roswell landing actually did happen or the alien autopsy, so what? it would prove that there are aliens out there, why is that worth keeping a secret? the alien in the autopsy was dead so its not like it gave 'them' any info, and it happened so long ago that if it did it hasn't made a huge difference to the world. i dont know im probably just ranting here, but i dont see what the problem would be, sure some may panic with the knowledge that we are not alone in this vast space we call the universe but others would rejoice , and its not like having the knowledge would really effect day to day goings on. maybe a public holiday on the day the landings happened. I dunno just thought id share and see what you guys thought.

Sean
I think a realistic answer is what Sheep said about national defense. If the gov't has a secret, then it's very easy to keep secret. But if something is made public, then the entire world knows. So, if (for example) the gov't had just some junk from a crash site, it's much better to keep that secret and research it secretly, than to let the cat out of the bag. Because once it's out - it's out. I don't think it has to do with any sort of "public panic", or any of that. If the secrets exist, it's for practical purposes - like, not letting Russia or China get technological secrets.
