DarkTiger
Faceless: I would be VERY skeptical of the pictures on the link you posted. They look a lot like simple camera tricks suck as using slow sync flash and long shutter to gain the haloing (color). The last photo can be done with a slow shutter speed, flash at the start of the photo and literally walking towards the camera. These photos can be done photoshop free, but it looks like they were touched up a bit because there's no exif data on the pictures (unless imageshack deletes exif)...

"Shannan believes this to be a 4d multi-dimension capture" *cough* fluff *cough*
This is a simple technique I call ghosting as I demonstrate with this (unedited, very crappy) photo of my arm:


http://www.mediafire.com/imgbnc.php/2a6a77d93e5565db9fc0bd445160904a4g.jpg << Link to the image (Updated!)


Not some "4d multi-dimension capture" . All you do is take a long exposure (2 seconds or more) and have the flash then change positions during the photo.

*Edit: I just looked at the exif data on the photos and it seems I was right. The exposure length of the shot was 4.29 seconds. A typical photo is around 1/60th of a second O.O . Also he used photoshop, but I don't know to what extent.*

Your pictures look alright, my only concern is that the pictures used flash which can cause ghosting and haloing on your photos which could give you what looks like a psi flare but is really just light that reflected off of your hand as it moved slightly or caused by the image being too bright. Not saying your photos are fake or anything, just that you might be getting false results.

Try a few photos without flash and see if anything turns up. You shouldn't need flash to capture a flared psiball since it's emitting or reflecting light, just maybe a slow shutter speed and low aperture. Also, the camera would be able to see the light regardless of distance (five feet shouldn't make a difference let alone five inches, though I'll test that, only difference should be less pixels when farther away) and you don't necessarily need a tweaked camera to capture psi, though older or cheaper cameras do a better job of it (in my experience). If your camera has manual mode I suggest using that.

*Edit: I checked out the exif data on your photos so I know the settings now... If you want to check them, on windows XP it's right click on the photo, properties, summary tab, click advanced. Should tell you the settings that the photo was taken on.

Psiball: Do you mean color the psiball? If so, it's simple programming, use intention to make it the color you want.
