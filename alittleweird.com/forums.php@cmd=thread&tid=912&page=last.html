Trick
First, cover the psiwheel.

Next thing..I never really see progress from actual practice. That doesnt mean you dont practice, but let me give you this. Experiment in practice. I always find that almost every time I progress (aka, move onto the next thing, or I'm able to move something I couldnt before) its always from some sort of trick or something. Its never that I just hammered away at it and it started working (at least if it did, it didnt stay)

Let me give an example. Youve been practicing with a (covered) psiwheel for about a year (wich is way too long IMO) You every once in a while get a twitch, maybe every few days or so youll get it to rotate a little. But then one day, you have the bright idea to start visualizing wind spinning around inside the container, and to your amazement, it moves. Now you can do it all the time without having to do that, because you finally broke the barrier.

Another example. You want to be able to roll something, but you cant. Youve been going at it for months and it hasnt rolled. So you decide to go back and take another path. You get your psiwheel moving, and start stacking them ontop of one another. Now your moving 10 stacked (covered?) psiwheels. You go back to whatever you were trying to roll, and now it suddenly rolls easily.

See, what I'm trying to say is progress doesnt normally happen in practice. If your trying to move something that doesnt move, you probably wont break the barrier if you just keep trying to move it. Maybe you will, I dont know. But I could never do it that way.
