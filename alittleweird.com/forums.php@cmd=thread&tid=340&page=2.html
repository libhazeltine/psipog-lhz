"But what happens to our sources? Like if I absorb energy from a rock and use it for something, what happens to the rock? Will it physically change? Or will nothing physical happen at all?"

Well according to the data from the experiments, very little "excess" energy can be pulled from an object, say a rock, before more energy is required to extract it than you get out of it. The "surface potential" is a small amount of transient energy that it stored in the object. One observed effect is the object becomes lighter. Not by very much, but by enough to be reliably measured. Other versions of this method have led to PK theories as well as some anti-gravity research. Another effect is spontaneous movement of the subject.

One way to look at this is (as Zectra I believe was pointing out) is relativity. Instead of thinking that you are pulling energy from the rock itself, think of it more like "the rock, by occupying space, curves it." as Einstein predicted. "This curvature creates tension on space/time which is supposedly normally manifested as gravitation. Well, instead, take that potential energy and use it elsewhere. The gravitational effect is supposedly lessened.

Now don't think you can suck the gravity from a rock and make it levitate easily. It has so far required massive electromagnetic coils to cause this effect:
