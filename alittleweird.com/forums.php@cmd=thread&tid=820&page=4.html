Interesting topic... here are some of my random thoughts, not really well organized...

I find a lot of people gravitate towards 'were-wolf' more than anything, due to media influence and other factors, when infact they may have been something else in a past life and not wolf. But that can also be because they're not looking and just jumping to conclusions. And also, dreams are usually metaphorical, not literal, but can have some meaning towards past lives... They can also use this 'were-wolf' thing as a disassociation from issues in their life, as a sort of escape route. This is obviously not always the case.


Many ancient societies had some sort of 'were-creature'; the creature differed by what was around. Obviously, where there were no wolves, there wouldn't be any 'were-wolfs', but other creatures such as 'were-tigers'. In some societies, shamans were said to be able to completely take on the mindset of the creatures to help the society and teach them lessons and tell stories (I'm assuming mental-shifting). And some stories even include hunters being able to physical shift into wolves to help them. But then again, these are just legends and tales, and cannot be taken as one-hundred percent fact...


There are a few different types of shifting:

M-shifting or mental shifting: When one's state of mind becomes more animal like.
S-shifting or spiritual shifting: This one can be broken down into auric-, astral-, and dream-shifts, which are all fairly self-explanatory.
P-shifting or physical shifting: There is a lot of controversy upon weather this is possible or not. It is when aspects of one's physical body take on attributes of another creature.


Also, one other note.. Watch out for bio-feedback, cause if you think you are shifting your mind might feel things that are actually not happening...


One final thing... Kube, I do not remember where I heard this, but I heard that sometimes the first few times when a 'were-creature' physical shifts, they black out...
