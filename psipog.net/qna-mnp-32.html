annie replies on December 28, 2003, 10:39pm:

This is a pretty complex question, believe it or not. The nervous system is a fascinating, complex, not completely understood mistress.
For your sake I'll give the abridged version of what happens, and then proceed to answer.
Basically the nervous system is divided into two categories: Central and Peripheral. The Central (CNS) contains your brain and spinal cord and is generally involved in involuntary responses. The Peripheral (PNS) contians the rest of your body (other than brain and spinal cord) and tends to be more associated with the response to environmental or outside stiumli.
When your receptors detect pain, neurons (nerve cells) meet at a junction called the synapse. They send the message "Ow" to your brain (CNS), where the brain responds by moving or removing you from the stimuli that causes pain.
What I suspect you're asking is how to increase the quantity of times your neurons meet.
This is a much more simple answer.
Keep yourself physically and intellectually fit. Exercise, or at least stretch daily. Keep physically active. Move your butt. Staying intellectually fit is more of personal choice. But when you learn or retain information, you create new neural pathways. Read. Play chess. Take a new route home. Put up a poster board that says R-E-D but written in the color yellow, and do this for all the colors. It makes you think, and indirectly helps with what I think is your ultimate goal.
Sorry for the lengthy reply, but you asked a nervous system question to a nervous system nerd, and this is what you get. Good luck.
