eonthepeon asks on May 30, 2004, 8:32pm:

Ok first ill give an example when we start out as a fetus we are quite small when we get older we grow, in order for matter to not be created is that saying that the amount of matter in our fetus stage is the same amount of matter we have right now? What im trying to do is explain that I think the "matter cannot be created thing" is hogwash..heh maybe I learned something wrong

eonthepeon asks:
If our energy can be percieved by one or more of our various senses thus stating that it is a form of matter and it is said that matter can not be created nor destroyed then how is it that we can regenerate/lose energy?

Rainsong replies on May 31, 2004, 5:31pm:

I put your two questions together because they concern basically the same thing. You have learned a little Physics and have not yet learned the context for the "law" in question.

The "matter/energy cannot be created nor destroyed" does not refer to a single organism, but rather the system of which the organism is a part. Matter and energy can be converted to different forms.

For example, carbohydrates "burn" to release heat and water (with or without other byproducts, depending on how cleanly they burn and how many impurities are involved).

In your fetus example, the child in question is acquiring both energy and matter from the mother. Once living free of her womb, the child either takes in more energy and matter to maintain itself and grow, or it dies. Either way, the energy and matter still exist, just not necessarily in that one person.

The fact that a form of energy can be detected by our senses does not make it matter: we can detect heat, we can detect movement, we can detect pressure.

The stuff referred to as "energy" used in psionics is another question entirely: it is probably some kind of matter. Its exact composition is still in question. It might be a particle of some kind. (The mechanism for gravity is likewise still in question. The hypothetical "gravitron" particle is not proven.)

Like all sciences, Physics has some missing parts, surely, but before dismissing it as hogwash, it might be wise to investigate it further.

HahaImDead76 asks on May 27, 2004, 9:14am:

I'm stumped, how exactly are you supposed to awaken your third eye.

Rainsong replies on May 27, 2004, 2:25pm:

There are two kinds of "third eye". One of them is a physical structure on certain kinds of reptiles. The other is a "chakra", a supposedly energetic structure mentioned in certain religious beliefs: Hindu, Theosophist, and some of the New Age religions derived whole or in part from Theosophy.

Given the phrasing of your question, my guess is that you are referring to the Theosophist version. A quick web-search on "Theosophy" should find a great deal of information.

There are many people who believe that chakras are necessary in psionics. In fact, I've been told many times that I'll never be able to do any psionic effects unless/until I believe in chakras. However, so far my lack of belief in these things has not noticeably inhibited my efforts. That said, I've seen "chakras" on some people, but only folks who believe in them. Therefore, until presented with evidence to the contrary, I will proceed on the assumption that they are some kind of construct.
