Peebrain replies on February 27, 2004, 12:44pm:

one way or another, this is probably a dumb question, but what do you (any of the mods) think are the logical limits of psionics, in general?

Peebrain replies on February 27, 2004, 12:42pm:

Keeping in mind this is just my uneducated opinion on the subject, I could formulate some basic beliefs...

I would suspect that TK takes up energy and chemicals in the body to exert the force. I don't believe it is just a magical unlimited force, therefore, I believe there is some limit to what can be done before the body passes out from chemical imbalance. Rainsong has reported moving canoes and small boulders... I would suspect the actual limit would be larger than that, but it would be very difficult to get that big. Perhaps something like... an entire car. Again, this is pure "in theory, just guessing" .

Telepathy seems to be affected by distance... But you can override distance if you have a strong connection with someone. A random psion "shouting" for help close by might get my attention, but if the psion is a few miles away I probably wouldn't feel anything. However, if an online friend like another mod of PsiPog gave out a shout, chances are I would pick it up, even though we live very far apart from each other. I would suspect that the actual limit in distance would be so big, that it really wouldn't be a big deal. People on opposite sides of the world can communicate... I don't think many telepaths will be surfing through space in a shuttle, so who really cares about pressing that issue further.

RVing seems about the same as telepathy... I've heard people can RV other planets, but who really knows if it's accurate or not? Not like you can confirm it...

What other skills? Constructs can basically be made to do anything, given enough psi, and enough skill. House shields are somewhat common... I'd imagine you could make an enormous shield as an upper-limit on the skill.

And then you might want to think about what the limits are when two or three people are involved in a specific goal. Does it add to the limit? Does it multiply the limit? Is it exponential?

Heh, I could go on and on and on about theory. That's why I try to stay focused on practical things... The field of psionics is so unknown that theories almost seem like a waste of time. Get out there and learn something! Don't just sit there and think about "could this, and what if".

Pally asks on February 13, 2004, 2:41pm:

Uh oh...

I just saw a program on tv... There was this James Randi guy, who bent spoon with his finger tips just like that!

Then he laughed as he bent one stainles steel spoon just like we did about a week ago... Then he said that its very simpple trick with nothing supernatural.


Any comments?

Im just after the "Truth"

Peebrain replies on February 15, 2004, 8:44pm:

Let's look at what's going on...

What is Randi trying to do? He is trying to say "look, I can fool you, this is not supernatural, therefore all psychics are fake". Or maybe "look how easily you can be fooled". Proving that he can fake it does not prove that it is impossible to do. Nor does it prove that everyone who has done it is a magician.

Think of it like this... In movies, it looks like people do some crazy things. Hollywood magic. That doesn't mean people CAN'T do it, it just means it's possible to fake it. Brad Pitt got in some bloody fights in the movie Fight Club. That was fake. Does that mean no one can ever get in a fight? Of course not.

If Randi is trying to prove that all psychics are fakes by faking something, that's pretty flawed logic.
