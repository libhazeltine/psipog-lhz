Let's talk about a new topic before wrapping this up. I have a
really cool secret that I don't talk a lot about on PsiPog.net.
It sounds kind of silly and pointless in theory, but if you
actually try it, it's very effective.

My secret is "affirmations". So what's an affirmation? And how
do you use it to do something kick ass? Affirmations are another
way to program your subconscious to do something. It just so
happens that it's a VERY effective technique. All you do is
repeat a few sentences to yourself, and eventually it will "sink
in". The best time to say affirmations to yourself is while in
a slightly meditative state - like before you go to bed, or right
after you wake up.

You have to be careful what you tell yourself. If you say some-
thing with the wrong wording, or with confusing language, your
subconscious might get the wrong message. It's best to make
affirmations very straight forward, simple, and positive.

For example, when I want to leave my body, I get in a meditative
state then tell myself (in my head, not out loud):
"I will have an out of body experience. I will be conscious of
my out of body experience, and I will remember the entire thing."

It's very assertive, and clear. I repeat those two sentences in
my head over and over again, until I completely zone out. And
guess what? No, it doesn't work 100% of the time, but I do get
better results. Notice how I say "I will remember", and I do
NOT say "I will not forget". "Not" is a negative. You have to
be careful of things like that. Stay as positive as possible.
Give your subconscious INSTRUCTIONS. Do not give it a list of
things to avoid. Also, don't say "I want", say "I will". You
can sit around all day and want something, and it can never
happen. So do you want it? Or do you will it?

If you're interested in affirmations, try looking up things on
self-hypnosis and other trance information on the internet and
library. It's a very popular method, and there is a lot of
information on it.
