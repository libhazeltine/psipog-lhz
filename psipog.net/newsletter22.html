greenlanterntexas writes:
"I was sitting in speech class and my friend was up doing her
speech. Well, since see gets extremely nervous and since I had
been reading your website and practicing I tried to put empathy,
I guess you could say, to practical use. I was sitting there
visualizing a tube attaching from her to me and I vizualized her
anxiety absorbing into me. I felt sick like I had "butterflies".
Then when she sat down it went away. So I thought, well that's
just a coincidence, but then I tried to do it to this other guy
presenting his speech and I felt the same thing. Do you think
that you can relieve someone of their emotions or is my mind
playing tricks?"

My comments: In a situation like that, it's hard to tell whether
your mind was playing tricks or not. A better way to test it is
to read someone when you can't predict what they might be feeling
by "normal" means. I.e., if someone is reading a speech in a
speech class, it's easy to logically conclude they might feel
nervous. However, if you can find situations where it isn't easy
to conclude what someone will feel, and try to feel their
emotions in those settings, you will have a better idea of
whether it's all in your head or not. Experiment! :-)

Michael writes:
"Hello. I've been trying to program my psi balls for over two
years. I know they're there, but as soon as I move my hands away
from the ball, it disipates. I've used all the methods in the
articles, and none of it's worked. Is there anything in
particular that may help?"

My comments: Take smaller steps, and work your way up to it. For
example, at first take one hand away, and only have the psi ball
in the other hand. Then when you get the hang of that, move the
psi ball along your arm (like rolling it along your skin). Move
it to different parts of your body. After that, try throwing it
from one hand to the other. Start with a short distance, then
make the distance bigger the better you get. Continue to
increase the distance until you are happy with the results. I
hope that helps :-).
