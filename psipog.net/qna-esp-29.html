Do most empaths try to learn bio-pk, or is it something that they can do or what? Cause it's like empathy is like a nose, people are almost good food, and a stomach is bio-pk. It seems useless to know if someone is hurt or if something smells good unless you can eat it or help them,... Right?

Peebrain replies on February 6, 2004, 12:40pm:

Heh... what an odd analogy...

Let's define some things so we're clear:
Empathy - In the psioinc sense, it means to feel someone elses emotions psychically.
Bio-PK - Influencing a biological system through psychokinesis.

What is empathy used for? Usually sensing what another is feeling. What is Bio-PK used for? Usually altering a physical trait of the body. Like blood pressure, or eye-sight.

Empathy is not only used to know if someone is hurt, it is used for other things as well. If the emapth finds an emotionally hurt individual, the empath sometimes doesn't want to help the person who is hurt. If the empath does want to help an emotionally hurt person, the easiest way would probably be to talk to the hurt person. Just interact with them, and talk things out. Bio-PK is more of a "I would like to change a feature of my body". Not really a "I would like to alter the emotions of someone else". Even then, if you find out that someone is in emotional pain, chemically altering their body to make the pain go away (which is what I would assume you're talking about, referring to Bio-PK) is only temporary. You can project "happy emotions" at a hurt person, and that can help them, but that's temporary as well. The best way to help, in my opinion, is to talk to the person about what is bothering them. If it is serious, get a psychologist. Get to the root of the problem, don't just try to force it to go away.
