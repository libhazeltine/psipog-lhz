Johnny writes:
"Okay if your body produces PSI and you control the product that
YOUR body creates and control the creation of the object (PSI),
then would that not make energy manipulation, Bio-PK?"

My comments: Wouldn't that make moving your arm Bio-PK? It
depends on your definition of Bio-PK... A more common/scientific
term for Bio-PK is DMILS, which stands for Direct Mental
Interaction with Living Systems. Don't worry so much about
where to categorize things... Does it really matter if
controlling psi falls under the label of Bio-PK or not? It's
just a label. If you're curious about Bio-PK/DMILS, do a search
on google, or go to a library. Sometimes interesting studies are
categorized under just "Psychokinesis" that could apply to
Bio-PK, so don't be afraid to pick up a few books :-).

Greg writes:
"Me and my friend have been practicing psi for over a month now
and have been practicing psi and telepathy/empathy. A couple
nights ago as we were practicing we decided to try something new:
seeing people from a distance. I went first, as I had pioneered
this before, though it was in vain. I sought out the person's
face, and my scrying was accurate. We tested this on two other
people, one of which I couldn't find, so my technique is not
completely accurate.

Here's my procedure:

1. Meditate, self-center, clear your mind, whatever exercise you
prefer.

2. Imagine the person's face, and examine how you feel about that
person and how your mind detects that person. This is the
"signature" (in my opinion).

3. Relax and search for the person.

4. After searching, a picture will most likely pop or ease into
your head. It will most likely be the right person, but I've had
a few misses, so to speak.

5. The next time you talk to the person, ask if they did what you
saw."

My comments: I agree that in Step 2, you are finding their
signature. The hard part seems to be Step 3, which you didn't
elaborate that much on... let me see if I can describe what I
think you're talking about. (Correct me if I'm wrong, and I'll
post it in the next newsletter.) Close your eyes, and stay
relaxed. Keep their signature in your mind, and mentally "reach"
outwards (does that make sense? :-P). The goal is to find the
person - you can imprint this goal on your mind in a number of
ways. Personally, I just ask myself "where is joey at?". You
can try other things though, like just holding onto the feeling
of the question (and not verbalizing it), or you can try to
visualize a map with cross hairs searching for the location, or
you can try extending a part of your awareness out and feel for
the right direction to search in. After that, moving on to
Step 4 might make more sense - you'll probably get some pictures
popping into your head, or a "feel" for where the person is, or
you might "hear" a reply in your head, "joey is at the mall".
Try it out, and see if you're right :-).

j_w_b_1 asks:
"I play tp games with my sister (as i told you last time) and I'm
wondering is that just a kind of family link or is it telepathy
in general?"

My comments: I haven't really analyzed my personal differences
between family-telepathy, and random-person-telepathy. In some
ways, it's easier with a family member, and in some ways, it's
harder. For example, if I'm trying to read my sister, sometimes
my brain interrupts and says "well, your sister believes blah
blah and blah, therefore a logical conclusion is that your sister
is thinking blargh bleugh and blarck". And that can interrupt
the reception of what she really is thinking. I mean: my deeper
knowledge about her as a person can activate the "logic" in my
head to try and logically conclude what she "must" be thinking.
So with family members (for me at least), it's harder to shut off
this process, and just WAIT and LISTEN. When I try to read a
stranger, my logical brain doesn't have any information to
process yet, so it doesn't try to draw any conclusions and
interrupt.

However, when I do read them, I can read them very accurately.
Perhaps because it's a team effort between my psychic perception
of them, and my logical understanding of them as a person? I can
read a few things, and plug that information into my logical
brain, and spit out more interesting conclusions.

It's a good question - perhaps I'll stick that in the next poll
on PsiPog.net and see what others think about it (if I remember
to :-P).

Scott asks:
"Is it true that telekinesis is in everyone?"

My comments: Some people say yes, some people say no. Personally
I believe that anyone can do it. But it's sort of impossible to
prove either way, until we know what telekinesis actually IS.
Perhaps something in a person's genes trigger it, or enable it...
or perhaps it's just one of those things that come with being
human. Or maybe it's one of those things that come with
existing in general? I don't know. The only way to find out if
it can work for you is to try it out and see. There is no "pee
in a cup" test that we can do to see if you're capable :-P. Try
and find out!

Tenwing_Faia writes:
"A long time ago, I was speaking with a psi-enthusiast I had met
at the Renaissance Faire. She suggested that psi powers are
'locked' and that those of us who study psi will come to a point
in our life when it will unlock. Of course, she had said, minor
feats would be attainable beforehand, but at a point in our life
(puberty, or around age eighteen) we will get a flow of power,
in which we can perform bigger tasks (if we have enough exercise
in control) or the smaller ones become a lot easier. She also
mentioned that where some people can't even do the little
experiments well, it is only that they have a tougher lock. All
in all, quite an interesting theory. I was wondering, what are
your thoughts are on this?"

My comments: Hmmm... I don't know if there is a "turning point"
in a psion's life where they notice an increase in "power". My
understanding is more of a gradual and punctual change type of
thing. By this I mean... I believe we make gradual changes
and advancements when we practice. Then every once in a while,
we can make a punctual change which is more sudden and noticable.
For example, if you practice telekinesis, you can sit around and
try to move the pinwheel all day and get no results. But you are
gradually getting better. Then one day, you get it to spin! And
bam, a punctual change. Now you gradually get better at the
pinwheel, and eventually get the balls to try to move something
harder like a pen. But you can't move the pen. So you keep
practicing, and gradually get better, until one day BAM - a
punctual change and now you can move the pen.

Maybe the person at the Renaissance Faire was thinking of one
specific punctual change... One idea that comes to mind is that
person might have been labelling the change from "psi might be
real", to "holy shit it really is REAL!". That is a big change
for a psion, and can change their outlook on psionics which could
increase their success. What do you think?
