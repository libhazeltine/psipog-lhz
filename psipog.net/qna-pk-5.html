Rainsong replies on July 13, 2005, 2:21am:

I don't think I could claim that it is _easy_ to learn PK, regardless of how old or young you are.

It is not yet known whether telekinesis is something that everyone can learn. The other main categories of psionics seem to be able to be learned by just about anyone, given sufficient time and practice, but there is some doubt as to whether this is true of telekinesis.

Shielding, in itself, is a separate skill from telekinesis, and is much easier to learn. If your ambition is to create force-bubble types of shields, however, then some skill in telekinesis would be required.

It _might_ be possible to lift oneself into the air telekinetically. I personally cannot do it.

Gizmo asks on July 12, 2005, 2:39am:

Is it possible to over concentrate when your doing TK, because I tend to take things too seriously sometimes and concentrate extremely hard, and when i'm more relaxed and in a better mood I seem to get better results.

Rainsong replies on July 12, 2005, 12:20pm:

Yep. It seems to happen more in microPK than macroPK, but trying _too_ hard can sabotage your efforts. This is one of the reasons that people are often advised to maintain a "playful" attitude when practicing. It is certainly possible to make PK work by sheer force of will, however; there is simply a better-than-even chance that too _much_ force-of-will will backfire. (As an example, when I do microPK, if I "try too hard", I normally get statistically significant results, but in the wrong direction.)
