SKainuiG asks on February 4, 2004, 10:26am:

Peebrain,

What inspired you or why did you make this website? I mean there are a lot of other psions out there that don't care if you believe in psi or not.

Thanks

Peebrain replies on February 6, 2004, 1:03pm:

When I first started, I wanted to find a website like PsiPog to help me figure things out. I used to think to myself "someone should make a site without all that religion, and make it more scientific, that normal people can understand and learn how to do things..."... etc...

Eventually I came across Active Psy, and thought it was great. It presented the material in an easy to understand manor, without any emphasis on religion, and it sounded like it was based on personal experience. I liked the teaching philosophy... Then one day, a friend emailed me about this site he was going to make, where people post articles online about Psi. He asked me to write an article on telekinesis for him, so I did. It was pretty cool... After a while he said he wasn't going to be able to make the site he was hoping to make, so he gave up on the project. At the time, I had already made a few websites (I'm a computer nerd ), and already had one dedicated to how to make a psi ball. So, I took my psi ball website down, took my unpublished telekinesis article, and asked around for people to help to start making my own site. Rainsong started helping right away, and I put the site up...

I guess what originally inspired wasn't that I wanted other people to believe... I really don't care if other people believe (as you said). I am more interested in making a type of website that I would personally like to visit. I've held a lot of different opinions concerning psi at different points in my life, and I want to make a site that would be useful to those points of view, because I understand where people who think that way are coming from (since I once thought that way).

Long answer..? heh. I guess it's a build up of a lot of things...

theAlias asks on December 13, 2003, 5:25pm:

What do the scores under the articles and the questions in Q&A mean? Which is the best score, 1 or 4?

Peebrain replies on December 20, 2003, 4:18pm:

"Score" means how many times it found the text in the article/Q&A thing. So like, if you search for "dog":

Hi, this is my dog, spot.

That gets a score of 1, because it has 1 dog in it.

Who is your dog? Is that your dog?

That gets a score of 2, because it has 2 "dog"s in it. So, higher score generally means "better".
