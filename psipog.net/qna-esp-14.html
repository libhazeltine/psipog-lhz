Peebrain replies on December 3, 2004, 5:14pm:

Good question. Usually, to correctly test a shield, you'll need someone else to verify it for you, with scanning. Sometimes that's hard to come across someone willing to do that. For me, I just stick my hand out, and I can "feel" my shield.

Start with simple constructs, and find ways to test to make sure you're doing those right. Then moving up to shields, it's less likely you're screwing it up, because you can already make smaller constructs correctly.

If you're only blocking out empathetic stuff, I bet you can make the shield fine. If you can feel it, it most likely is up correctly. Bubble shields aren't terriably complicated. When you decide to learn empathy, just move slow if you're nervous about the risks.
