Stefox asks on November 1, 2003, 7:39pm:

err... My question may be a little bit dumb but what is the exact difference between Telekinesis, Psychokinesis and telepathy?

Rainsong replies on November 2, 2003, 4:27pm:

Telekinesis and Psychokinesis are exactly the same thing. The confusion comes because sometimes people decide to use the terms as though they referred to different effects. Originally, "telekinesis" was coined to refer to a different effect entirely, in another language. Therefore, for official scientific literature, "psychokinesis" is the preferred term, to avoid ambiguity.

Telekinesis/Psychokinesis is the act of moving or altering an object with your mind by way of "psi".

Telepathy is communication between two or more minds, again presuming to use "psi" instead of more conventional means.

In both cases, "psi" is nothing more or less than whatever not-well-understood mechanism allows to mind to do this. There is some evidence to point toward "psi" being a substance/particle given off as a byproduct of nervous function.
