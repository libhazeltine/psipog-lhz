Perception
Analytical overlay is just as bad as not being able to sense
what something is, but sometimes it's all just a matter of
perception, what someone might see as new someone else can
say it's old. As a matter of fact most things in everyday
life are only what we perceive them to be; such as some pets
are harmless until they bite, the world was flat and the yeti
exists (or doesn't).

Recently I've noticed simple hallucinations due to simple
analytical overlay; basically I see something in my peripheral
vision and take it as something else, like a dog instead of a
shadow or something moving when its not, it's really not a big
deal and it happens to allot of people only that they don't
notice it. But my point is that the brain has the power to
subordinate our senses to what it thinks it is A case allot
of readers should be familiar with are the blind spots in
both our eyes (if you don't know about it look it up), we
have a point to our right in our left eye and vice-versa
were our eyes cannot see, however we don't notice it since
the blind spots don't overlap, on top of that we don't
notice it because our brain fills in the spot with a vague
or clear image of what it thinks should be there.
So basically we can sense something how it isn't, how it
really is, or just make something up, but two things are
for certain: it can be as clear or more as if you were
actually seeing it with you're eyes or other sensory methods,
and we can have control over this, we can actually see
something instead of having an image in you're mind or think
someone else's thoughts instead of having unclear references.
I have experienced the before mentioned myself, so I'm not
saying this as a theory.

I could go on for pages but to make this short I'm going to
simply give the reader an exercise to actually see what I
mean:

1) Go to a dimply lit place (to make things easier).

2) Start meditating with you're eyes open.

3) Eventually you will stop paying attention from you're
eye's sensory input (for me it all gets black).

4) After you achieve this try it again but in a more alert
state.

5) Try spicing things up; see if you can make yourself see
simple lines or dots.

*if you want you can try with other senses though I haven't
tried so myself.

This exercise should help people that take too long to sense
something, and it also helps boost confidence since its
something you can actually use you're senses with.

My comments: This was another nice write-up from Obsidian, who
always does a great job on laying out helpful information in
understandable ways.

Thomas wonders:

I have been wanting to tell you about this for sometime, but
never got time. I thought I would send it in this time right
after I read the newsletter. I don't practice psychic stuff
nowadays because I seem to have lost motivation... I made a
topic about this in the forums a few months ago....but it didn't
work ;) .

Well anyway, a few weeks ago, something happened to me that I
am sure was an OBE. I have tried my hand at OBE many months
ago...but I usually got disturbed by a mosquito or something...
This time though I had no intention to do it, and I hadn't even
thought of it!!

Well this night, I had a very bad cold and throat problems
and a slight fever... well my health wasn't too great... anyway,
I was too tired to think about anything so I went to sleep right
away. I don't know how many hours passed but I woke up sometime
in the night. I felt that my legs were floating up. It felt
really odd...since if I usually tried to lift my legs while
lying down... it would put a lot of pressure on my muscles
around the middle. And I am not that much of an athletic guy,
considering I have made a permanent a**print on my computer
chair.. Anyway.. I felt as if something was lifting my legs and
holding it at some 35 degree angle from the bed. Then I had
another odd feeling. I felt as if I was being dragged towards
the top of the bed. But the really odd thing was that the edge
of the bed never came, even though I usually sleep with my head
up to the edge of the bed.

I frequently have Lucid dreams. I have had them years before
I even knew that was what they were called. But I am sure this
one was not a dream. It felt too real..I usually know when I
am dreaming and this one felt very real.

I then tried to sort of look towards the top edge of my bed
or something and suddenly woke up and found that my legs were
down as usual. I realized it was an OBE only later, when I
thought about it.

My comments: There are any number of things of what this could
actually be. It is always hard to actually decided for sure
what something is unlesss you have some past experiences
to compare it with. But the mention of your legs floating and
being pulled backwards through the beds sounds like one of two
things I've experienced previously. One, it was either sleep
paralysis, which seems to be a popular occurance in psionics.
I've had some boughts of sleep paralysis where it felt as though
I were falling through my pillow, and sometimes just falling,
the whole time, this all began from a waking state. The other
possibility is that it could very well be and OBE, and you would
just need to work on getting some control on what is going on
if you want to start enjoying it. The easiest way is to
let yourself know before you are going to sleep that if anything
happens you are going to be ready for it, and then remember the
event you are talking about. This helps your mind remember
better in situations when you can't really think quick enough
about what to do.
