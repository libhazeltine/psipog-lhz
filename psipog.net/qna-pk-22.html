immortal_87 asks on May 7, 2004, 1:55am:

Please forgive me if this question has been answered somewhere within the depths of the Q&A area, but searching through 12+ pages for one answer isn't exactly my idea of a fun night. Anywho, on to the question. I have been practicing PK for awhile now, and I am getting sort of adept in spinning the psiwheel. What I am wondering is, when I don't have something covering it, I can control it rather well, though it does have the tendancy to spin counter-clockwise, but when I cover it, I can't make it budge. I know it isn't air currents, ect., because I have control of making is stop, go, somewhat with direction, and speed. So I guess what I am asking is: How do I kill this mental block? And how can I get it to spin clockwise better? Thanks.

Rainsong replies on May 7, 2004, 4:32pm:

I'll answer the second one first: to get the clockwise spin to work better, you might need to give it more time in the practice. Most people have a direction that they work better in, for spinning in figure skating, direction that sketched animals face, and yes, for pushing psiwheels with bits of oomph. Don't worry about it. As long as you can change the direction even a little bit, you have the basic tk stuff under control. Finesse comes with practice.

As for the mental block, be sneaky. Start with just a little "wall" in front of the psiwheel.....in fact start with a wimpy wall. Hand a paper towel or a handkerchief in front of the wheel, and peer over the top of this improvised drapery while spinning the wheel. Then, repeat with a book or loose-leaf binder standing on end, in front of the wheel. Add another wall, by standing the book on end open at roughly ninety-degrees....and so on. If you have access to an empty plastic pop-bottle or clear plastic tumbler that you can cut, make a partial dome by cutting a whole "for the oomph to get through", and put the dome over the wheel. When you can make it spin in the cheater-dome , _then_ you should be ready for a "real" dome enclosure.
