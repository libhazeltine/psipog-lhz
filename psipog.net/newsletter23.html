sas01 writes:
"Hi PsiPog.net I've just joined your community after wanting to
learn about telekenisis. My family has had a strong history of it
and we often see future events in our dreams and/or have déja vu.
I attempted the psi wheel exercice and was very disapointed when
I saw little to no results. This is my first day but I thought
I'd be able to do a little more. I have recently been ill and I'm
tired. Does that make a difference? Are there any other exercices
or techniques? I'm pretty sure I can bend candle flames as I was
doing so in church today except it may have been coincidence but
the flame was bending in the right direction. Any advice then?"

My comments: Some people do get results on their first day, but
most don't - so don't let that discourage you. Being ill will
have an effect on your ability. For more techniques, read the
articles on PsiPog.net, and also http://ppsociety.com has good
information on PK as well. Bending candle flames is fun to do -
but as you observed, it could have been coincidence. Buy a
candle and experiment with it. Control the flame in SPECIFIC
directions, at SPECIFIC intervals. This can help determine if
it's a coincidence or not.

Patrick writes:
"Greetings fellow believers! I have yet to begin any psi
development. I am still researching as much as I can first.
However, I believe I've noticed a correlation between psi
phenomenon and traditional Chinese martial arts, i.e. Chi. If you
know about martial arts, you may know about "chi". Chi is the
"life essence" in our bodies that, obviously, keep us alive. It
can also be described as breath, heat, spirit, or bio electricity
by modern western scientists ("chi" has no satisfactory English
translation). The only difference I can see, at this point, is
that in martial arts, you use your "chi" to injure or heal,
instead of manipulate as we are doing here at PsiPog. In MA you
focus on moving your chi/psi into, say, your fist to give it more
power and invulnerability to damage. Or likewise into another
person to heal a damaged organ or blockage of energy. Although
this is just a hypothesis, I would love to hear some feedback."

My comments: Some people do think that psi and chi are the same
"type" of energy - others don't. You can find evidence on both
sides of the spectrum. I personally hold the opinion that
whatever psi and chi are, that there is a more complicated and
deeper understanding needed than the current one. Our current
understanding does not account for everything we witness in
reality, and therefore our current theory can not be correct.
Whatever you are being told about psi and/or chi, always remember
that no one has it entirely figured out - so if we want answers,
we need to figure them out. Keep an open attitude, and continue
to think about what psi could be :-).

"Hi, my name is Ben and I've been reading this for a long time. I
have been trying to make psi balls for a year or two now, but I
can't seem to concentrate and the most I can ever get when I try
to channel energy is this breeze type feeling going down my arm.
Got any suggestions?"

My comments: You've been trying for a "year or two"? Heh. A
breeze feeling is a good start, but something isn't right if it's
taking you that long. I suspect that you haven't been practicing
for a year or two STRAIGHT :-P. A normal psi ball should only
take a few weeks to a month to get (given constant practicing).

FOR YOU. It really shouldn't take that long. If you feel
something strange (like a breeze, or anything else), then it
might be psi, or it might be something else. Continue to try new
things and figure out what is what.

Zell writes:
"When you depatternize a shield, what happens if the shield is
too strong (not likely but it could happen!), or if it was
programmed to resist psi? How would you go about taking it down?"

My comments: There are some methods, although some border on
psychic combat (so I won't go over those :-P). However, we can
talk about some common techniques for dealing with stubborn
shields. You can wear down a shields programming over time by
making very small adjustments to it's programming. For example,
if you programmed your shield to resist psi, then you can start
by convincing the shield's programming that "psi" is something
else. Over time, you can convince the shield that psi is
something completely different (like a specific emotion or idea).
The shield will be fulfilling it's programming by resisting "psi"
but it will think that "psi" is (for example) the emotion
"boredom". By redefining what the shield is programed to do in
little steps, you can confuse the programming, and then take
advantage of this confusion to remove the shield.
