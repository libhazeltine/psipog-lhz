EXCERPT 1: The most common explorations of PK potentials include levitation, influencing the throwing of dice, controlling random generators, spoon or metal bending, producing visual images on a photograph, the raising or lowering of temperature, splitting or dissolving clouds in the sky, and of course, the outright movement of an object. Sometimes even miraculous healings, illuminosities and the appearance of apports are credited to someone?s psychokinetic ability. As you explore psychokinesis in depth, it becomes difficult to deny the existence of a very real mental ability. Today, even scientists claim psychokinetic power is a proven phenomenon.

But let?s begin with the basics. The components of our physical universe are atoms, which in turn are made up of electrons, protons and neutrons. Everything in the universe is made up of this matter and energy. Matter is potential energy ... and PK is thought by my some scientists to be the ability to affect the energy surrounding matter to generate the desired result. So instead of moving an object with your mind, you?re harnessing the surrounding energy to move the object.

The term psychokinesis is of Greek origin, meaning ?motion produced by the mind.? And if you care to label some Biblical manifestations as PK, occurrences of the phenomena have been documented for a very long time. Even the shackles of Paul and Silas falling away might have been due to a psychokinetic influence.

In the 1800s, PK was elaborately investigated in both England and America, primarily in association with Spiritualist activities. The ability of mediums to levitate, move furniture, and produce floating heads and hands was investigated by the British Society for Psychical Research and its American counterpart -- the ASPR. And although some fraud was discovered, most of the cases were labeled authentic.

Duke University Professor J.B. Rhine began conducting his famous PK experiments in 1934. Rhine did establish that people could psychokinetically influence the throwing of dice, and went on to learn that in any one session, the longer a subject worked at it, the lower the control factor. So fatigue or maybe boredom were valid factors in the ability to demonstrate PK, just as it often is in other PSI areas. PSI, P-S-I being the undefined force resulting in psychic phenomena. Dr. Rhine first coined PSI to mean ESP-- extra sensory perception, and then later to mean PK and ESP, which he described as ?a general term to identify a person?s extrasensorimotor communications with the environment.?

Dr. Rhine reported that PK did not appear to connect with any physical process of the brain, nor was it subject to the basic laws of physics. He concluded that it was a nonphysical force of the mind and like extra-sensory perception, it was independent of space and time.
