Like plenty of others have said, don't worry about it yet. I started worrying about my future in third grade...needless to say, it ruined my childhood. By the time you are 18, you will be a different person, and you will know what you want to do by then. Besides, your life may change completely by then, you never know. I'm 18, in college...my dad always said I had to move out when I turned 18, but he ran off to Florida, and now I'm living at home with my mom, and will be probably until next year. I have held different jobs (about 8 ) since I was 14, and have developed a good sense of "financial smarts." I know how to save, and the whole scha-bang, so that's an advantage. I suggest you learn how to as well.

Also, try checking out the book called "Nickel and Dimed." It's about [not] getting by on minimum wage here in America, and is very informative and interesting. It also may show you what to do, and what not to do.

Bottom line: Don't worry about it just yet. Yeah, it is good to plan ahead, but don't obsess over it. Things change, and you will too. Unless you are a master pre-cogger, there is no way to tell exactly what will happen in the future, so just go with it. It also may help to take an accounting class (which is hell, by the way) and start learning how to save and budget, as well as other life skills that will aid you in the future...But, try not to worry about it too much. You're still young, and need to live out your childhood (adolescent-hood lol). Trust me, don't end up worrying too much about the future and missing out. I did. And I regret it.
