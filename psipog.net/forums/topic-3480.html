I've never gone in for sensory deprivation (though I do close my eyes during telepathy practise). However, this idea is being used very successfully in the scientific world for testing ESP. It is called 'Ganzfeld' (A German word meaning 'whole field'). The eyes have halved ping-pong balls on top of cotton wool taped over them, a red light is shone on the ping-pong balls, 'white' noise is played through headphones and the subject lies on a comfortably-angled chair. Someone in another room then sends a set of images and the subject speaks out loud all the thoughts that enter their head. At the end of the sending period, the subject is brought out of sensory deprivation and shown a series of pictures, including the ones sent, and is asked to identify any (s)he saw. The results of the identification and the content of the subject's thoughs are analysed to see if there is any correlation.

Incidentally (though I can't remember where I read this) musicians score on average 50% higher than non-musicians in Ganzfeld testing.
