WhiteRaven wrote:

and... you can turn lead into gold, or just about anything, by nuclear means, in fact you can even combine elements to make new ones


Yes, but it takes a LOT of heat and pressure. That's why we're having so many problems getting fusion working; we can't find a way to make the fusion generator output more energy than was put in. That's why all our nuclear plants use fission instead of fusion, and incidentally why cold fusion is generally considered pseudoscience.

Also, fusing iron into gold would be kind of difficult, since Gold has an atomic number of 79, which would mean that you would need to combine iron with iodine in order to get gold. Obtaining the materials probably wouldn't be too difficult, but actually performing the fusion would require technology that humanity currently doesn't have (it would take a LOT more heat and pressure than simple hydrogen fusion, which we CAN do).
