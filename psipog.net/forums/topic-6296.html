Lleu wrote:

That would be the best guess. Seeing as how that's what electricity IS, the movement of electrons... yeah, Electro-PK would most likely have to do with that.

And for those of you who don't know, electrons are those tiny little negatively-charged particles that swarm around atoms, which are the building blocks of life. There are, in a perfect atom, the same amount of electrons as there are protons, which are positively charged, and reside within the nucleus of the atom along with neutrons, which have no charge.

By the way, if you ever look at a periodic table of the elements, the atomic number of each respective element tells how many protons are in the nucleus. Which means Hydrogen, at Atomic Number 1, has 1 proton and 1 electron.

But anyway, to sum up, electricity is the movement of electrons from one atom to the other. Which is easier for some than others, particularly with molecules such as with polymers, i.e. rubber and plastic, which are very resistant to the flow of electricity (and heat).

Also, electricity does NOT conduct easily in completely pure water (i.e. distilled water). It needs the presence of electrolytes, such as salt (which breaks apart into ionized particles in water).
