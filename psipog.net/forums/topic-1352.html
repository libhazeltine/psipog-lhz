I own a Tokyo Marui P90 upgraded to 380 FPS, with an M4 barrel (protected by a silencer). Take my advice, if you're going to be playing airsoft with other "professionals" that own good guns, don't waste your $50 on a piece of shit spring loading crap gun, you need an AEG (automatic electric gun). Furthermore, if you want any chance of competing with the rest of the guys, you need at least 350 FPS. I mean with the stock 250 FPS guns you'll find, you might as well be spitting the bb's at the enemy.

For a decent gun with an m120 spring, metal bushings, and AT LEAST a 9.6v battery (10.8v is better, but will wear out your gearbox 3x faster), you'll probably drop upwards of $400.
Oh, I forgot, you're gonna want a hi-cap magazine too. The stock mags usually hold somewhere around 50 bbs. I have a hi-cap for my P90 that holds something like 1200. :-)

As for where to get your gun, the cheapest guns come from Chinese websites (www.redwolfairsoft.com), but the problem with these sites is shipping. Airsoft guns are known to be stuck in US customs for months on end because they look exactly like a real gun. Your best bet is to buy your gun from an American store. You'll end up paying $20 - 30 more than the Asian gun, even after shipping, but you won't have to go through the hassle of customs.

A good US retailer that I've purchased from in the past is www.21stcenturyairsoft.com.
Here's a list of a ton of online airsoft retailers sorted by country:
http://www.intrudershop.com/showpage_eng.asp?page_name=wheretobuy

One more thing, since most guns need to be upgraded before they are useful, many of those sites offer upgrade packages. If you're not comfortable with taking apart your gun and making the necessary modifications, you can go ahead and pay them something like $50 to upgrade it for you. Most new guns (not pistols, but actual AEGs) have something called hop-up. It puts a backspin on the BB so that it shoots farther because its trajectory drops down more slowly over time. If you hold a gun with hop-up sideways and shoot it, you can see the BB curve to the side (because it doesn't have gravity to straighten its path).
