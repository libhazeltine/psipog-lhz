Hello everyone,

Remote viewing coordinates are used to for two main things; a connection to the target using TRN's (target reference numbers) and to avoid AOL. TRN's were used throughout the remote viewing projects that the government hosted (StarGate, Sun Streak, Grill Flame).

When the government first started experimenting with remote viewing they used map coordinates. The map coordinates would be given to the remote viewer and the viewer would do a session. It was not until a scientist asked "What if these guys just memorized the map coordinates across the globe?" when they started to experiment with random sets of numbers (two sets of four numbers).

You may ask why the acronym CRV was changed from Coordinate Remote Viewing to Controlled Remote viewing. The reason is because the government no longer used map coordinates as there target reference numbers (TRN).

Now, I believe that pyro and Enjin may not be fully understanding what Peebrain is trying to say. You can use any numbers. If you can count then you can assign TRN's. Please note that coordinates do not have to relate to the target at all. You can choose any numbers you wish.

Like I said, if you can count you can assign target coordinates. Now, I try to help out as much as I can.
