Tank: Well, I don't understand the whole I/You subject switch, but there's probably a reason for it. If it's in a CYOA style, then I'd suggest putting all of the narration in 'You', and if it's otherwise, clarify it within the narration.

Beyond that, I really like this idea, of sharing spooky stories. I'll add one I wroter a few years ago that I really like...

~
She was successful, rich and powerful. I never knew anything about her, not even her name, but she knew everything about me. She knew who I was, where I worked and lived, and my route home each day. She had some sort of animosity towards me, some hatred, but that was all I knew. I went naïvely about my day, never thinking of it until that fateful afternoon...

I was walking home one warm spring day, when I got the oddest feeling. I sensed something nearby, but I couldn’t feel it. I shrugged off the feeling, and entered my apartment, never realizing I was being followed until I heard a click. I spun around, and she was standing in the doorway, aiming a gun at my head. I rushed toward her and wrestled her to the ground. Breathing hard, I rested a minute, and for too long. I never saw it coming, not the fist that connected with my temple. Suddenly, everything went black. I came to a few minutes later. She had disappeared.

I went on with my regular life for a month or so, when I had been invited to a party with a staff member at the firm where I worked. On Friday night, I drove over to my colleague’s house, not expecting more than a fun night with relaxation. I never expected what was to come.

The room was dimly lit, loud with music and laughter. I danced for a few hours, and finally sat down to have a break and a drink. Suddenly, the lights went out. A scream startled the crowd. When the lights came back on, it parted like the Red Sea, revealing none but her and her cronies. They advanced across the path laid before them. When I saw them coming toward me, I knew I had to find a way out. I ran through the door, jumping into my car with them on my heels. It was pouring rain, with thunder louder than I’d ever heard before. They dashed into their own car as I sped out of the driveway, and on to the street. They were going to catch me! I never knew that that sheet I picked up off my boss’s floor would lead to this. They drove up beside my car, cutting me off, and with them parked across the road along the cliff edge, there was nothing I could do. They came to my car and opened the door, dragging me out. They then shoved me into then drivers’ seat of the black car that was their own. Holding me at gunpoint as I drove them to my office, they questioned me about that sheet. When I told them I didn’t remember anything, the leader glared at me.

"We have ways to find out the truth." She said. In my fear, my hands slipped on the steering wheel, and the car slid. I tried to stop it, but I just couldn’t. I lost control of the car; it crashed through the guard rail and sailed over the cliff. The fall to the bottom was a wild ride that destroyed the car. Amazingly, the woman didn’t get a scratch. As I took my last dying breath, she checked my pulse and closed my eyes. She turned and walked away.

~Epilogue~

Karien Cortra died on October 17, 2008. Even with all of the strange events surrounding her death, all hoped it was nothing more than an accident. Years of investigation were put into the cause of her death, but it was never really solved. Investigators could not find all the pieces. All evidence pointed to her death being an accident, all save two things: A single set of footprints leading away from the crash site, and a typed note lying on the rubble that read simply: You will never be safe.
Signed, The Immortal.
