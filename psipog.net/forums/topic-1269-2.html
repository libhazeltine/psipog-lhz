I'm not sure if talking to the subconscious is a "psi" thing as much as it is a relaxation/meditation thing. This is something I've done frequently.

Basically, you meditate like normal (deep breathing, silence the inane babble in your mind, etc). Once your mind has calmed, you visualize that you are in a secluded sanctuary that is just for you -- basically, daydream that you are in a relaxing, safe, soothing environment. Don't "create" it. Instead, allow it to appear. For me, this is a tiny little hut deep in the woods by a stream of water. I enter the hut, and my mentor is there. Again, allow him/her/it to appear. On your first visit, 'knock' on the door to your training hall (the 'hut' in my case) and allow your mentor to answer the door. You may be surprised to find that you are not surprised who/what your mentor turns out to be. The whole process is a bit silly, frankly, but the results are incredible.

The mentor is an amazing resource. You can ask for advice (and the advice will always be just what you needed, right when you needed it), or you can ask questions. I don't know about anybody else, but my mentor is incredibly Socratic -- my mentor nearly always answers my questions with a question that instantly reframes my thinking and leads me to the correct answers on my own.

I found this process in the book "Life 101", if you wish to read more about it.
