Before I joined PSIPOG, I was a member of the website bzpower.com. If you look at the forums, you will see a bar that is partially filled in under the person's avatar, by the personal information.
A full bar looks like this:
A neutral bar looks like this:
An empty bar looks like this:

Moderators can increase or decrease a user's "proto energy," which here should be called "anti-fluffy-ness" or something, based on the user's actions. Obviously flaming, and major spam will decrease proto energy, but also leaking false information, having huge sigs, creating multiple accounts to vote on things will decrease proto energy. If you hit minus five energy (0 is neutral) then you automatically get suspended whlie mods review you to decide what happens next. On this site, doing things like lying about skills, providing false information about the location of lost objects (though this is hard to prove), invoking religion, etc, would warrant a decrease. Helping people out with information, techniques, and new theorires that make sense could boost your energy. What does having a low bar do other than push you closer to suspension? Well, nothing in reality, but I know from being a member there for a long time that those with low bars aren't believed as much, won't be helped as much, and you generally are less excited when a low bar person posts in your topic. This obviously has its downsides, but it is sure a good way to keep the site less fluffy. What do you think of this?
