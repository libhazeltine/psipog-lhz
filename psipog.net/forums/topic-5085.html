Not only is it possible, in my opinion, to distort reality, but it wouldn't take unbelievable quanta of energy either. Energy is not necessary to change perception; perception is based purely upon thought, and action which requires no physical energy to take place (again, in my opinion). It takes only realization of the bendable nature of the world around us to do such things. Think about it, we do it all the time -the succesful ones of us that is- with PK or RVing or telepathy, but the only reason we break the laws of physics is because they exist; otherwise, any unexplainable action performed by a conscious being is simply natural.

For example, in my chemistry class I sit next to one of the two main doors to the classroom, the one people tend to use more often. Both doors are wooden, with large windows in the center, and they stay locked from the outside all day long. For the longest time, I had to get the door when people were late or coming back from an errand. So one day, after having to get out of my seat to open the door for a few people I really dislike, I just decided to open it without touching it. I was totally confident, and when a friend of mine arrived shaking the door handle, I just made a gentle wave at the thing from a few feet away and it popped open. He wasn't touching the door when I did this. Now, if this happened all the time, the laws of physics would explain it very well and locks that wouldn't open would be wierd. You see, it all revolves around the way a person percieves the world.

I have actually had many other weird experiences like that one, some apports, some fire drills, and of course large PK events but in all of them I was just just very confident and gentle about it, and I succeeded and didn't think much about it. To someone else, that's altering the laws of physics; to me, that's life.
