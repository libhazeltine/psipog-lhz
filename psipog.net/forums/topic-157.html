Healing opens a small can of worms.

In some states, you need a license to perform healing/Reiki. I'm not sure on the specifics of the law. I also agree with this law - I don't think random people should be going around "healing" people. Healing doesn't follow the same pattern of other skills on PsiPog... for other skills, we encourage people to try things out, experiment, and come up with cool ideas. I most certainly don't want people to take this attitude towards healing. I don't want kids to get hurt, and not go to the doctor, so they can experiment on themselves with healing. I don't want to attract people who are hurt, and are seeking alternative forms of medicine. I don't want people coming into the chat room, and ask for someone to heal them like it's no big deal. Having someone mess around with your bodies healing process is a big deal. Having someone send out vibes to alter your body is a big deal.

If someone is actually certified in Reiki, then I have no problems with them talking about what they're allowed to talk about (according to their certification). Annie, the author on PsiPog who wrote about Reiki and healing, was certified and a licensed massage therapist. Which is why it wasn't a problem for her to write about the topic of healing/Reiki. How many people here are really certified? Do I really want to spend time and energy on figuring out if someone is really certified, or lieing? (No). Annie was a special case because I knew her for a long time before she started writing about Reiki, and I knew about her massage business.
