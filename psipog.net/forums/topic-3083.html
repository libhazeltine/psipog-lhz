I was practicing with my psi wheel again yesterday and it still didn't move the way I wanted it to.
We probably all know that emotions are a killer for psi because they distract us.
But after about 5 minutes of constant left and right turning I tried another methode - I got angry.
I yelled at in my mind, I screamed (in my mind) and it immediately stopped.
I was able to re-create this scene everytime it didn't move the way I wanted it to.
I was even able to force it into one direction at a speed I never had before.
Don't get me wrong I knew how to "soft" control one, I did it before but this was different.
It wasn't the soft and void control I had learned some months back.
No, this was VERY different it felt like my psi would get drained out of my body.
After some time I felt cold and tired and I decided to stop.

What do you guys think? Was it just my mind? Or are emotions really capable of releasing such powers?

Emotions are very inportant for results of your training, but experimenting with them is very dangerous.
It may be like this : A weightlifter tries to pull up 50 kg. Its rly hard for him, often he fails. Then he become angry and starts to pick 50 kg really fast, several times. After that his muscles are totally out of use.
it may be same with telekinesis. I guess thats why in articles everywhere are tips like : have fun, let it go, take it easy blah blah
Your safety is much more important then results you get, keep it in mind. Also remember that you practice even if youre not moving wheel, every safe training is step forward.
