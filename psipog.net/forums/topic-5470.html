Yeah, my experience coincides with the wormhole or space folding explanation rather than the "demolectularization" idea. I know this simply because I had done phasing before my series of teleporting accidents so I knew what it felt, especially since it's quite distinct in the pain it causes. Teleportation had no such feelings or pain, just a sense of disbelief and being so sure I was just somewhere else.

In my first case, I teleported from one stairway to another on the other side of the school. There was a sort of "meshing" of the world around me. Like I was walking down the stairs, then as I stepped out onto the 2nd floor, it was on the other side of the school. No break in the physical surroundings, no feelings, just a sort of trance (which I invoked during my more spectacular feats) and a seemless transition. It was a weird feeling coming down the stairs and expecting to see the arts room on my right only to find the science lab was there then looking behind me to see a stairway I knew I hadn't stepped out of.

My second occurance, I teleported along with my bike so I guess that illustrates both animate and inanimate objects as possible items to be teleported. As for Wingmakers, it certainly wasn't my motivation nor intention but I saw nothing wrong with their technique but of course a technique needs not perfectly describe what actually happens so that's kind of a moot point.
