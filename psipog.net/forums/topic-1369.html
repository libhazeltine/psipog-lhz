Well, let's look at this at a microscopic level.

On your desk you have a piece of paper. In this solid object there are many condensed atoms.

Above the piece of paper is air. It consists of many non-condensed atoms.

To make the paper trade places with the air, in the sense of moving it, you have a few options:

1. Physical force
2. Psi (Mental force)

The act of teleporation would simply be the effect of causing atoms to "jump" across each other and trading places.

To actually cause the paper to "teleport" into the air, you'd have to have some way of causing the atoms of the air to trade places with the atoms of the paper.

Am I wrong in any of this?
