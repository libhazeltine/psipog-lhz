I think the major problem with making a new psi-species is that the forces of evolution WILL act on it. And considering that psi is an insubstantial energy (I personally think it ties in with the basic energy that makes up the universe) which can be channeled in massive quantities from any form of matter, they could become absurdly, absurdly, ABSURDLY numerous very quickly. If they use mitosis, splitting whenever they gather enough energy, and they can gather enough energy in one minute, then you have an exponential growth curve operating on 2 to the mth power, where m is the number of MINUTES the species has existed. Assuming a standard rate of evolution, that means you would have several hundred times the biodiversity of earth in a matter of years. And Earth has had life for 2 billion years.
