Something I learned from peebrain and a lot of other people that, for some reason, don't seem to be able to put this principle into words that I could easily understand, it may be able to help you.

When you "visualize" the psi, that's really just a way of getting your mind to move it. If you can move it without visualizing it, you can free up that part of your mind and focus it on moving it more efficiently. I'm going to try to explain the process here in as few words as I can...

You know the feeling when you move your arm? Just sit there for a minute and flex your bicep like you're doing curls. Pay attention to what you're thinking subconsciously, basically your brain sends the signal and your arm reacts. Try to get a feel for what that process feels like. Then, imagine your psi, try to become aware of your entire body (by feel only) at the same time, or if that's too much for now, just start at your feet and work your way up. Become aware of them and how they feel. Then reach out and grab the psi with your mind in the same way your mind grabbed your bicep to move it. Try and replicate that thought. Pull all of the psi gradually into your solar plexus (the place that hurts a LOT when you get hit in the middle of your chest; the highest point in the middle of your chest where there are no ribs) or somewhere else if you prefer, but I think psi has to do with nerves, and there's a nerve cluster there. Just give that a shot, then start pulling it from the rest of your body in the same way. Keep concentrating on keeping that clump of psi in your solar plexus, though, otherwise you may lose it. After that, just use that same command as your bicep curl to move the psi into your hands; You may feel it accumulate in your solar plexus and then move. Then just spit it out of your hands.

I admit, I'm not really good at this, but it's worked a heckuvalot better than anything else I have tried so far. And I think it's the principle everyone has been trying so hard to explain with words. I may not have done such a great job on it, but I tried. Maybe it'll help some other people understand.
