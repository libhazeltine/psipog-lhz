I had tried to put forth a lot of force to close the bathroom door, and had only succeeded in making it wiggle back and forth about 30 minutes ago before it happened. I went back to my computer after I had failed and went on about my business websurfing, when I heard a strange cracking noise outside. I had stopped typing and turned my right ear so it was facing the window directly behind me, so as to hear better. The creaking stopped. So I naturally shrugged it off and continued to type in my web search, about 4 minutes after that the creaking started again and a branch from the tree directly outside the window of my bathroom had fallen. It was fairly large in size, even a strong storm wind should not have broken it. I'd say it was about 7 to 10 centimeters thick. There was not even a slight breeze at the time it broke. I could have climbed a tree and sat on a branch the same size and I am 100% certain it would not have broken.

I'm not sure what I could consider this. A coincidence? The conditions were not right for the branch to break like that.

Even before I started practicing PK there was never an occurance like this, even during a bad storm.

I've been practicing PK every day for about 2 and a half weeks. I've gotten the psi-wheel to spin, both under a glass and outisde, I've learned how to stop it and change its direction.

I have not been successful in bending spoons yet, I can get it to bend about one centimeter down, over a period of about 20 minutes. I can get doors to wiggle slightly, but I have not yet been successful in opening/closing them as well.

I know of some circumstances during these past few weeks where the psi-wheel should have spun but for some reason it didn't.

Should I consider this a strange coincidence? Or something more? Was there a lot of pent up energy?

Perhaps I'm jumping to conclusions way too fast. I may be acting foolish.

If you have any ideas as to what I should think of this, please respond. I'd really appreciate it.
