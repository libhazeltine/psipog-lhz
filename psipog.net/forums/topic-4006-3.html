One of the basic points of Shotokan karate-do is kimete (focus). The concept is to lock the heel, back leg, hips, arm, fist at the same time as striking (a punch), in order to deliver the maximum power. Instead of just hitting someone with your hand (which is really, rather in-effectual, and I don't care how strong you are), you strike with your whole body.

Layered on top of this is the idea of kimete, focusing your bodies energy to strike through the opponent (performed by an instant tighten-relax of your muscles upon impact). Performed with kiai ('spirit shout'), a proper attack (be it punch or kick) can be incredibly powerful. Considering that it's all energy (psi, ki, chi blah, call it laundry for all I care), there is an element of it in Karate-do.
