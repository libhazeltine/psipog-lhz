Same problem here ... though I usually fall asleep even while lying on the back (I usually sleep on the side). I found out one important trick while practicing for couple of years: I rise my head a little by putting another pillow underneath it so my head is a little higher than the rest of my body. There are two reasons I do this, the first one is I'm in a slightly uncomfortable position with my head and I won't fall asleep so easily, and second, I tend to clear my mind easier while in this pose and I can mantain consciousness for much more time.

The only thing I'm really not good at is putting a lot of will power into it. After about an hour of steady meditation and afterwards trying to get out of body, I usually give up, turn myself a couple times in bed and fall asleep almost instanteneously. There are times when remarkable things happen and sometimes I even make it out, though rarely.

I think the only hards part is getting from awakened body to body asleep, mind awake (thats a good mantra actually, try repeating it for an hour one time, I'm sure you'll experience something interesting). And also people usually rush into it and try to force it. NEVER try to force an OBE, it has to come by itself. When it's time you will know, no doubt about that.

Anyways good luck people!
