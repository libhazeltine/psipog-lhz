You're half correct: by that age, most brain development has stopped. However, if what you are saying was in fact true, and no new synapses formed, it would be impossible to learn or remember anything. Clearly this is not the case!

In fact, you are born with as many neurons as you're going to get, but the configurations and layouts of these do change - quite heavily in children, less in adults.

After reaching mature age, learning new tasks is more difficult, but not impossible. A good example of this is language learning, which becomes much more difficult after age 13, and even more difficult after age 20. It is not, however, impossible.
