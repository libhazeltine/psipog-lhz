I had a few interesting ideas:

1: Couldn't you just use some form of biotelekinesis to live forever?

2: Maybe kids with imaginary friends at a young age would be better at subcoscious communication.

3: I read an article once (don't ask me where) about a study where two groups of kids were tested, each with equal experience, which was probably zero, in basketball. Every day for a week, I don't know for how long per day, one group was asked to shoot baskets and the other was asked to pretend to shoot baskets. At the end of the week, the ones who shot baskets in their minds were able to get more accurate shots. Maybe this could apply to psionics practice. I know psionics isn't so much a physical skill, but maybe just pretending that you are stronger at psychokinesis, imagining yourself using it, than you are would make you stronger. No, I don't mean pretend you can lift a car. Just move a piece of string or even a tin foil wheel.

4: If younger kids are introduce to psionics at a youg age, without even knowing the possibility that it might not exist, if the kid's parents are moderately skilled psionicists who could use psychokinesis and communicative telepathy, maybe that kid would grow up to be an extremely powerful psionicist. I remember an article being discussed at this forum about, I think kindergarteners who were asked to to throw imaginary baseballs at some bottles that were set up, and some were able to knock them over.
