Pepsiboy was trying to help but thats not hypnagogia. When you are falling asleep your brain closes down on sensory input, so that electical impulses in your brain can simulate your senses in what becomes a dream. While you are falling asleep, or waking up, your brain will start the process but sometimes it goes slowly or does it in chunks instead of altogether. Which results in disattenuated thoughts. And what seems like real events are actual hallucinations. You can choose to believe in the spirits or ghost or whatever 90% of the replies are about, but I go through hypnagogia, consciously, everynight on purpose. It happens to everyone whether your conscious of it or not.

From wikipedia:
The hypnagogic state can be accompanied by or associated with anomalous phenomena such as alien abduction, extra-sensory perception, telepathy, apparitions, prophetic or crisis visions. This conduciveness to anomalous phenomena can be correlated with the initial increase of alpha and the later increase of theta brainwaves.
