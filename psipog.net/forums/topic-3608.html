I'm pretty sure it's the visualisation. It doesn't matter where you get your energy from. Once you have it, it does whatever you program it to do.

My first successful psiball was warm, as are all my psiballs. And then I managed to change it to cold. It was the same energy, I just changed it's programming. I havn't been able to make it cold since then, but this proves to me that the source doesn't matter. And heck, you don't even have to be already decided on what your construct is going to do. You can change a contstruct's programming once you have already programmed it.

I think that the reason the source matters for some people is because they visualise the energy as being hot or cold because it is coming from a hot or cold source. They believe that the source makes a difference, and therefore it does make a difference for them.

I think that everyone who uses different sources for different temperatures should practice using the same source. Perhaps something that doesn't have a reputation of having a certain temperature. Perhaps the earth. Or whatever. Practice making hot or cold psiballs from the same energy source. Heck, even attempt to change a psiball's temperature once you have already created it.
Trying these things should give you practice and experiance and in the end will help you to become more skillfull and make you better at visualisation and programming. You will also be able to break free from the limitations of believing that you have to use a certain source for a certain result.
