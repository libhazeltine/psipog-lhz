I started when I was 15, and told my parents right away. They thought I was stupid/crazy and going through a phase. So I stayed quiet for about a year I think. Then I had to ask for money to host PsiPog.net, so I told them again. They gave me the money, but ultimately wern't supportive and suggested that I stop spending so much time on it. Fast forward another two years and I'm a little more open about it. I start predicting stuff and reading my dad's thoughts. He gets freaked out, and then starts to accept it.

My mom never really thought it was something amazing, but she never really told me it was stupid. She took the position of, "if it makes you happy, go for it". She was never really concerned with what I could DO... she seemed more interested in knowing what I cared about. She liked hearing about the relationships I've had with people (I think).

My dad liked the cold hard evidence... which took a few years to provide, but ultimately was cool to finally have his acceptance.

I suggest that everyone tell their parents and get it out in the open. I know that different people have different situations... but ultimately, if it's something that you really enjoy doing, and you can see yourself doing it for years (or you've been doing it for years already), then it's an important part of YOU.

I know some parents aren't supportive, and it does suck to have the people you love looking at you like you're an idiot (I know, I've been there). Then you notice them look at each other, and you can tell they are having a conversation between themselves about how silly this "phase" is... yadda yadda yadda. I know. I've been there .

Back to the point: if it's an important part of you, then give your parents the opportunity to share that with you. If they decide not to share it with you, then that's their decision and you have no control over it. But at least give them a chance - don't just assume they will shut you down.

End small rant. .
