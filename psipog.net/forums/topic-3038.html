Sometimes mine is at a bad angle and just rolls off the pin...but I doubt it would happen that often. For the spinning out of the corner of your eye, put a mark or something (if you are allowed to, that is) by one of the edges. If you see it "move" check to see if it is still lined up with the edge.

It may help to "color-code" each edge (one edge blue, one green, etc.), so you know if it's the same edge or not. I'm probably going into way too much detail then is necessary, but I'm just trying to think of all the ways you could dismiss it, and prevent that from happening.

I know this is most likely going a bit too far, but you could write down which colored edge was lined up so you would remember it easier.

With practice, you will be able to control it and make it spin when you want to...just keep practicing!
