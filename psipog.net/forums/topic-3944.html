I've personally never been a big fan of books and things that are on interperating dreams. Some concepts are probably more universal than others however there will always be some differences. You're best bet is to see what they mean to you. For example, I saw a book once that said that swimming in deep water meant you must be feeling "out of your depth" and things in your life are out of control. Thats fine for some people, however for others being in deep water dosen't worry them in the least and may mean something completely different, it's all a matter of perpective. *shrugs* if you really think these dreams of yours are trying to tell you something, I'd recommend sitting down and thinking over them. I can't speak for anyone else but I often find if it's something particularly important and I don't get it the first time round, it'll recur so I wouldn't stress.

There is a very clear way to interpret dreams, and that is to derive not the optical but emotional meaning from it. For example:

I climb down a hatch and end up in a chamber that?s filled with corpses, I want to get out but can?t as it?s too difficult to climb up again. Meaning abstractely:

I got myself for (interpret reasons) into a situation that I found to strongly dislke and am afraid of, and I am struckling and having serious difficulties to get out of again.

- Take that abstract and look arround what in your life most closely resembles this particular emotion. - There you have your dream interpreted.

The visual scenario in a dream is usually (when it comes to subconscious dream-interpretation) not interesting, the very roughly abstracted emotional circumstances are. This will lead you to immensly accurate interpretations of your personal dreams.

Things like close friends accurately point into a particular direction though. So if these friends played a role in your life recently, there is a very good chance these characters resemble what this dream is about. In this particular case there is a certain problem with the closely related social environment, which?s particular meaning no-one can dig up but yourself.
