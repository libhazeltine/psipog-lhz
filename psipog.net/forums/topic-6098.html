Many have heard of the saying, or have been told, “if you believe in it enough, it will come true”. Well, this is true in many ways. The saying goes in two ways, if you believe that you are horrible at something, then, chances are, you are. But, if you believe that you are the best, then you are. It all has to do with the subconscious mind. The subconscious takes up 92% of your brain, leaving the conscious mind with only 8%. In other words, the subconscious mind dominates the conscious mind, but the conscious mind has the ability to reprogram the subconscious mind to it's liking. Some of the works of the subconscious are, but not limited to:

-Habits (good and bad)
-Involuntary actions (breathing and in some cases, walking etc.)
-The certain feeling you receive when you think of something in particular.
~Example: When you think or hear the word 'mother' (or someone else close), you receive a certain feeling.
-Dreams

The subconscious mind is a very powerful tool to have. As stated above, it takes what you say, and makes it possible in it's own way. When you say, “Aw, I can't pass this test!” and you really believe it, more than half of the time, you end up failing. Because you believed that you couldn't do it, so did your subconscious, and it found a way for you to fail. Same goes visa versa.

Your subconscious is also responsible for habits. For example, a woman drives to and from work everyday, she doesn't switch routes, just the same route. After a few months of driving to and from work, she begins to feel as though she is traveling in less time and doesn't remember the trip to or from. What's happening here is her subconscious has gotten her everyday route to and from work, and does it itself. When the conscious mind and the subconscious mind aren't battling for control, many things can be achieved. Another example, when you first start a sport, and you have no prior experience, getting into the swing of things can be a bit hard. After you practice for some time, it begins to get easier. Have you ever found yourself making the perfect touchdown, or making the perfect goal, and you weren't all there? This is the subconscious, once again taking over.

When you are able to shut your conscious mind up and let your subconscious take control, you will be able to accomplish more than you can imagine. The point isn't to fully obey your subconscious and be it's slave. You should let your subconscious in and let it play it's role. When you are able to be on cue with your subconscious, things will be a lot easier, but when your conscious and subconscious are constantly battling for domination, the subconscious will win, no question, it can still be a headache.

Some of the ways that the subconscious can help you out is, for example, weight loss. You can say all you want that you are going to loose that weight and you are going to do it now, but if you aren't really feeling that and believing in that then, nothing will change. You will still eat that cheesecake like its nothing, and not even think twice. This is because you haven't gotten through to your subconscious. Yeah you're talking the talk and looking at the right foods, but that doesn't make a difference if your not making the slightest effort. When you start believing that you can loose weight or whatever your situation is and you actually make an effort, you will see changes.


Ways to Reprogram Your Subconscious:

-Deep meditation.
-Try communicating with your subconscious directly. (Pendling or ask and receive)
-Do the Alpha State Routine (please take it upon yourself to Google it, don't make me do all the work)

Note:
-When communicating with your subconscious, you may receive answers before you complete your question.
-The answers you receive won't always be in words, bit more like feelings or images.
-There is an article on PsiPog on Pendling.

If there is anything you feel I have left out or haven't fully explained, please let me know.
