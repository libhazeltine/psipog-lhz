I got a slight variation that seems to work really well for me.
Best is to lay down but you can sit too if you want, just basically get into a comfortable position. Close your eyes. At this point don't just count but picture the numbers as they come up, it takes some practice but you should be able to visualize strong enough to sorta even see them (you know how even when you're not dreaming vague images can sometimes come up when you close your eyes, same idea except you're making them). I enjoy meditating... *shrugs*

I know when you first do it, it's really a pain in the ass (sometimes literally, if you're sitting still for a while!). Now I meditate almost every night. I find it really relaxing, and it helps me to just calm down and stop thinking about worldly problems for a little while. Plus meditation is nearly required for OBEs.

Do people HAVE TO meditate? No. If it doesn't interest you, then don't bother. However, there are different ways to meditate - not just the sitting still and think of nothing method.

One method I use a lot is walking meditation. Basically: go outside, go for a walk, and relax. Enjoy your time. Or active meditation, where instead of thinking of nothing, you are intent on thinking about one subject as deeply as you can. For example, if you got mad earlier that day because someone told you to piss off, then you can meditate on it by just sitting around and saying, "Why did that make me angry?". Come up with as many answers as you can, and ask "Why?" for each answer. Get as deep as you can to understanding it.

~Sean
