I have a problem: even after a lot of practice, my constructs can only exist if I contain the energy between my hands. The moment I take away either hand, or "open a crack in the containment field" the energy disperses almost instantly. I've tried envisioning a steel ball, a self-contained energy sphere, and many other images, but none of them worked.

The main purpose would be to create a construct that will absorb ambient energy from its surroundings, and use ~60% of it to sustain itself, and the rest to exert an outward force; plus it should adhere to walls.
I've envisioned a spider on the wall, energy streaming into the ball, and the force it exerts outwards(equal to the force acting on it). I kept charging the ball until I could barely feel my hands, then proceeded to stick it onto my wall. When my hands have "cooled off" a bit, I checked for its presence, but my met no resistance whatsoever where the ball should have been sitting...I say no resistance because when I create just an unprogrammed psi-ball, I know it's there because I can feel a resistance when I try to push my hands together.

I have managed to heat up a bal, but cold and everything else still needs work. Right now, I just want to create a self-conatined construc, that doesn't need the containment of my hands...then I think I can move on to one-handed psi-balls...
