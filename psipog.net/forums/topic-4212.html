The whole idea of it pivots on the neck area itself. It's sensitive, and therefore it is probably more prone to conveying a tactile sensation, even with the slightest nudge of psi.

The back of the neck-area on some rodents (including rabbits) are photosensitive. This is a mechanism that allows the rodent to recognize when a shadow is cast over them. Said shadow could be cast by a predator, such as a hawk, and so the rabbit will know when to move it's ass. I remember talking about this in PSC, so Rainsong is probably the one I can attribute this method to.
