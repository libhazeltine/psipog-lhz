"Because" is the answer because, just like "Why," it's not a whole sentence. When someone says something and you ask why, what you're really asking is "Why is whatever you said true?" "Why" is only the first part of a question ("I stole the cookies." "Why did you steal the cookies?"), just like "Because" is only the first part of an answer ("Why did you steal the cookies?" "Because I was hungry.").
