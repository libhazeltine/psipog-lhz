I personally say it's fake, look at it this way, it may appear real, but try playing the video at ultra low speeds. The trick that he was using was friction by rubbing his fingers very minutely and fast along the spoon. Also the type of spoon that he was using is a very cheap steel alloy that doesn't hold up worth a damn in anykind of heat.

As for the tip breaking off, in the video it shows as when it bends he wiggles it back and forth with force which greatly weakened the metal structure, his fingers were close to that weakened spot and when he heated it up, by shaking it back and forth while in the weakened state and friction still being applied, perfectly natural for it to have broken off.

Sorry to bust your apple Appolo, but sometimes requires common sense on material types and movement types. Also the placement of the spoon was pretty much a dead give away, if I was going to do a spoon bending video I would be holding the spoon between two finger tips at the base of the handle. Thus that would not allow for any doubt at all unless get into the doctoring of these things.
