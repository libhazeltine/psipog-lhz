I'm glad to hear you've mad esome progress. One thing I've learned is that we all progress in different ways, at different rates.

Personally I've been actively practicing TK for just over a full week now and while I do still have trouble moving the Psi wheel from time to time (mostly due to a lack of focus) most days I simply put my hand near it and think the direction to make it move. I've discovered that it's important to understand the way that YOU believe TK works, and then apply that. For me it's very much based in my hands - which is probably a side effect of growing up watching too much star wars, but I understand that and I accept that it works that way for me. Now that isn't to say that I don't practice without my hands, I understand the use of not needing to use them and that it's something for me to develope, but for now that's the way mine works.

I've also not had any luck sliding or rolling objects yet - now I said before I've only been at it a week (and took 2 days off for much needed rest, which paid off BTW) but I understand your initial frustration at not getting something to move. I think a large part of moving objects is making your mind understand that this CAN move. The Psi wheel is easy to spin because you can see it spin due ot the wind, and without touching it - that's easy to ingrain in your mind, but another object can be quite difficult.

Anyway, I'm rambling again. Off with me.
