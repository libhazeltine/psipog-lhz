it depends on what kind of meditating you do and how high of a level you get on your own. Lower level basic meditations pose no danger. These are meditations such as staring at a point on the wall or repeating a phrase over and over. But if you begin to practice advanced forms of meditation such as attempting to leave your body, delve into your subconscious, contact internal/external beings, recite 'magical' phrases etc. then it's best doing it under supervision.

As a general rule, however, you're probably not advanced enough to hurt yourself through meditation.

I've been meditating in various ways for the past... must be 5 years, maybe 6. During this time I personally have never gone skitzofrenic or experienced any kind of mental trauma, but there were a few occurances with others in the group that were potentially damaging (but fortunately not) For example, I had a friend who one time during a group session accidentally left her body and then when she came back her spirit-head wasn't well aligned with her real head and our guide had to walk her back straight.
