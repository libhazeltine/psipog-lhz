It depends, I would think, on the circumstances when you perceive your awareness.

Do you wake up, recall your dream, and then think that you were concious during the dream because you remember parts of it? It's pretty easy to fool yourself once you're awake into thinking you were completely aware. You start to make things up without realising it.

It's also common for dreams to feel very real but not be lucid. You know what's going on, you feel your heartbeat quicken, you smell some pie on a window sill.. but unless you reach some point during the dream where you fully realise "Dude, I'm totally dreaming this!" it's most likely a dream where you just go along for the ride.

Some people will say you need to be able to control your dream for it to be lucid *ahem, oneta*. In most cases, I agree. Personal experience has taught me of special exceptions to the rule.
