When you say you stop breathing, do you have any of the usual effects of not breathing, e.g. lack of air, discomfort, trying to get a breath, etc.?

While I haven't stopped breathing before, I have had my heartbeat "stop" - I simply ceased being aware of my heartbeat, which I am ordinarily very aware of when lying still in bed, even in a trance.

The big thing to remember here is that your body will not - I repeat, will not - allow you to run out of air. Try it. Breathe out, and don't breathe in. The longest I can simply sit and not breathe without my body forcing a breath is seven seconds (note that I can of course hold my breath for much longer - I'm talking about the amount of times it takes for my body to feel the need to breathe in). If you begin to run out of oxygen, your body will take in a breath.

The worst that will happen is that somehow you will not breathe for a minute, two minutes, whatever, and you will black out and then start breathing again. It is physiologically impossible to hold your breath long enough to kill yourself without external help.

Hope that helps.
