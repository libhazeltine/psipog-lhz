Possible? Anything is possible. Practical? I'm doubtful. Demonstrable? That can be debated. Easy? Hell no.

I've had very limited success gathering enough energy to cause a "flare", or visual trace of the energy I've gathered. However, I'm glad someone else out there is considering the possibility of forming a strong enough psi-pet to flare regularly.

They're bloody hard to photograph, as Peebrain has explained, but I've had some beautiful "lens flares" in odd places when having my picture taken. Sadly, my most recent one is an outdoor image and has an over-exposed region that could very easily be attributed as the source of the lens flare. I offer it only for amusement and maybe a bit of hope for your new project, so please keep in mind that I'm not interested in explanations of technical possibilities (indeed, probabilities) of the flaw in the following image. Like the content of PsiPog's media page, there are hundreds of way to fake it, and as I said, there's an obvious source (overexposed sky) for the unusual lens flare.



At the time I was taking this picture, I was up on Dragon's Tooth mountain in Virginia after hiking in partial meditation for several hours, admiring my psi-pet and just talking to it. The thicker region of its body is roughly four feet long, which nicely matches the horizontal purple haze to my right in the image (left side of image, in front of the smaller rock). I had visualized it laying crouched on all fours with tail draped across the rock towards me as it basked in the sun, looking off into the distance with me.

Oh, that reminds me. I believe it's a good idea to make constructs, especially psi-pets solar powered! I really don't think it'll mess with the sun too much, and helps keep track of them because you can feel their warmth when they sit on you!

I find it highly amusing that a "lens flare" occured precisely where I had visualized my psi-pet sitting while my picture was taken. Certainly far from proof, but still fun. I hope nobody minds my sharing. I'll try and get indoor photographs that include the unusual glows...

I wouldn't expect your wolf to show up on camera with you any time within the first year, though I think it depends on your talent and dedication. Confidence and persistence will be key to your success. I've been working on mine for more than two years now.

EDIT: PM or IM me if you want the pic. I killed my bandwidth uploading these to the forum.
