I don't have much SCIENTIFIC experience with it, but I do have personal experience with the phenomenon, as well as a general knowledge from what I've read thus far. I take it you were in that small area between being awake and asleep?

Generally that's the area when you can often hear, feel, even see things. I've had moments where I'm watching TV and I start to doze off, only to find myself still watching TV (oddly enough, what I hear from the TV has NOTHING to do with what's actually on, as I notice when I actually shake off the sleep).

Often during this period, you can also have that sudden feeling that you're falling, causing a reactionary jerk to try and catch yourself - which can also wake you up.

I've also woken up many times from dreams because of sounds WITHIN my dreams (I swear, they're sounds that could have never occurred around the house).

I've also found that I cannot talk in my sleep, because I end up waking myself up, haha.

And on regular days when I wake up normally, I usually have a song left over from my dreams (apparently, my dreams have a soundtrack).
