bigger (duh)
source to draw from or a very strong shell (assuming that this is supposed to be permanent)
make sure that it is set to stay wherever it is (bind parts of the construct to the walls, ground, roof, whatever).

Like I said in my hello post, I'm a bit of a "wilder" in the sense that my constructs and such have been mostly improvised. I built a shield from a bonsai ficas tree I keep in my dorm room... kind of magnified the energies I felt in the the tree and walled off my room with it. It makes my room very peaceful because it blocks "thought pollution" (I'm not sure what you all prefer to call it, but the random thoughts normal people broadcast when they're emotionally unstable) from a dorm of seventy guys... sitting in my room is like being out in the middle of a forest alone. It's very peaceful, and nice when I miss our forests back home.

The shield should last as long as the tree survives, or until I mess it up with something else... it's stayed strong for about two months and my "sensitive" friends still comment on it even if they don't see the tree.

I have a piece of labradorite (blue-green silicate stone, egg-shaped) that I've centered dozens of shields on, and I just stretch them out a bit when I set the stone down. I usually carry it with me and keep the shields small, but I surround my room with them when I take it out of my pocket. I'm not sure how great an idea it is to bind them to a stone, though, as I feel more vulnerable when I have to recreate the shields because I've left the stone at home, etc.
