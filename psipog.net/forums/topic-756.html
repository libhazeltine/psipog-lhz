How to be a Good Leader:
1. Be Nice
2. Be Involved
3. Be Honest
4. Be Intelligent
5. Be Fair
6. Be Understanding
7. Be Willing to Work

How to Respect Others:
1. Do not talk about religion/spirituality
2. Do not talk about psychic combat
3. Give proper credit
4. Do not violate copyright laws
5. Avoid the mentality of "I'm right, everyone else is wrong"
6. Don't try to manipulate others
7. When in doubt, communicate

How to be a Good Author:
1. Use correct spelling and grammar
2. Have a clear purpose and stay on topic
3. Be precise and concise
4. Research your topic in depth
5. Use pictures
6. Organize sections intelligently
7. Understand your audience

How to be a Good Skeptic:
1. Recognize the source of information
2. Understand the limitations of your source
3. Research evidence without bias
4. Understand different perspectives
5. When in doubt, ask for more evidence
6. Present reasonable ways for others to validate your claims
7. Doubt everything and ask questions

How to be Credible:
1. Avoid creating new terms
2. Avoid re-defining terms
3. Cite your sources of information (including personal experience)
4. Use reputable sources of information
5. Listen to people who disagree with you
6. Explore multiple perspectives
7. Avoid preaching "the truth"

How to be Responsible:
1. Do not advocate substance abuse
2. Do not give medical advice
3. Do not post obscene information
4. Do not post personal contact information (other than e-mail)
5. Do not advocate breaking the law
6. Hold yourself to high standards
7. Maintain a "PG-13" level of decency

How to Keep Things Fun:
1. Don't attack others
2. Don't look down on others
3. Don't force your beliefs/opinions on others
4. Avoid taking things personally
5. Keep things in perspective
6. Realize we're all in this together
7. Laugh

These are the new rules. I am open to suggestions on how to improve them, but I think they cover mostly everything. I will be using these same rules for future restrictions regarding articles, so feel free to pick them apart and try to find ways someone could argue in favor of something against common sense.

For more information on how to be a respected member of the forums, please see PantsParty's article located at http://psipog.net/show.php?cmd=wp&id=38 .

~Sean
