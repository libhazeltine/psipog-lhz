Recently, I was browsing the web when I found a site with a variety or -kinesis abilities. If I remembered the site anymore, I'd post links. I'd google it, but I'm too lazy for my own good.

Anyway, among the abilities was chronokinesis. I looked into it, and what it basically does is alter one's perception of time. For example, if you slowed your perception, everything would appear to be in slow motion. However, to those around you, you would be a speeding bullet. A really big one, too. The opposite could be achieved, as well, according to the site. Make yourself slower and the world around you bullet filled.

Now, after some time, a question occured to me. Say you needed more time, so you slowed your perception of time so that you could work much faster. How would this affect the aging process? There are three reasoning points, as far as I can tell.

The first being that, since you slowed your perspective of time, aging, an aspect of time itself, would slow, as well. Therefore, you'd age slower, sort of keeping up with this new perspective you've placed on yourself. This would actually add to your years, although you wouldn't really realize it. Say you spent what seemed to be a week in the slowed state. Maybe it was really two weeks. However, due to the aging process, you only age that one original week.

Then there's the aspect that you'd be unaltered. That time would actually flow normally, as would aging, so you'd be moving really fast, things would be slower to you, and you'd age as though unslowed. However, since you seem to be going through time so much slower, it would appear to gradually wear away at your life. You would perceive things much slower than they really occur, so you'd feel like you'd spent that same theoretical appearing week, when in reality you'd aged that full, real two weeks.

Last, but not least, there's the theory that if you speed your body up (which is what the skill seems to do - speed you up so time only seems slower), the aging process will speed up with you, causing you to age the fastest of all. Since in all the other examples, perception was slowed to 1/2 normal, I'll keep it that way for this example to make things easy. Now you've spent what you think is a week in the slowed state. However, it was really two. But, since you're aging so much faster, it really turned out to be 4 weeks, almost a full month, of aging.

Now, of course, these numbers are all hypothetical, and I don't really expect someone to spend a straight week in this state. However, over time, they could accumulate such time.

So, what is the theory for the rest of you? Slowed aging? What about normal aging? Perhaps it was actually sped up?

I don't really know what to think myself. They all seem probable and likely.
