This is kind of a pointless discussion if you consider what you're arguing over....

Pyro-PK and Cryo-PK are the same thing: changing the temperature of an object. The only separation between the two is relative terminology. For example, you could say you are heating an object relative to its original temperature, but it could still be much colder than the surround environment.

A similar argument would be: Which is more useful, multiplication or division? These two 'separate' operations could emcompassed as the same thing. For example.... 4 / 2 = 2, but you could also write this as 4 * (1/2) = 2
