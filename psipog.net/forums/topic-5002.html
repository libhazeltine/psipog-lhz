While Chaos Magic is not exactly my cup of tea, it really is not that different from psionics, on the grand scheme of things. In fact, if you are using Chaos Magic to produce an effect that can be observed by a third party, it _is_ psionic, by the basic broad definition of the latter.

There seems to be an idea floating around that psionics is inherently better than other forms of energy work. In this case, I am using the word "psionics" in the relatively limited sense referring to the approaches to energy work typified by the methods espoused here, and in related sites, as distinct from various forms of sorcery, witchcraft, ceremonial magic, chaos magic, and the like. This idea that it is "better" is horsepucky.

If it works, run with it. I'm a psionicist, and I generally prefer psionic methods. However, I am familiar with ceremonial magic, and practice it semi-regularly. I've practiced assorted kinds of folk magic, including the ever-popular candle magic, knotwork, crystal-workings, and elemental magic generally. I've pulled random spells from role-playing game rule-books and made them work. The structure of psionics is such that you can make most magic systems work, if you have any kind of skill at all.

My knowledge of chaos magic is much more limited, but I would not dismiss it as fluff based solely on what I've read online. Take a look at a good ninety-percent of psionics sites and try keeping a straight face when explaining why psionics isn't fluffy.

End of rant...for now.
