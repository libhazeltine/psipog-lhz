Well, there are a lot of names for it. Obviously, wherever he's from, they have a different word for it.

In English, at least, the two terms are telekinesis (movement from a distance) and psychokinesis (movement with the mind).

I think scientifically speaking, most people use psychokinesis nowadays, but telekinesis is still used interchangeably with the other.

I'm not familiar with "parakinesis," but I do know pyrokinesis (manipulation of fire) is definitely not PK/TK.

I know two different meanings of parakinesis, both of which are not in my area of expertise, but I can briefly define them:

In medicine, parakinesis is an abnormality of nervous control over movement, the production of movements. These movements are involuntary and usually stiff and seem unnatural.

In parapsychology parakinesis is a form of telekinesis. A parakinetic must touch an object before he can move or otherwise manipulate it with his mind. For example Uri Geller is known for his supposed parakinetic ability of bending spoons.
