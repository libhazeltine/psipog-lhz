Mark555,

Here's an idea for helping your PK: Instead of working on getting it to move, how about working on some control? Set up your psi-wheel(assuming you have one) in some place with a constant(but gentle) flow of air, such as in front of a far away fan on low. Get into the position you'll be in for doing PK, and observe the wheel for a little while. Get a feeling for how often it changes direction(if ever), how often and how long it stops, etc. Once you've gotten that down, begin trying to influence it left, right, or even try to make it stop!

If you're careful with the results(trying to weed out the coincedences(sp?)), this can be very effective(well, it was for me, at least). Personally I can't move a psi-wheel worth crap, but I'm pretty good at controlling one already in motion. I was able to make it stop completely for one full minute before losing concentration and letting it return to spinning. Now tell me the chances that it just happened to stop for one full minute, in the light wind of my fan, beginning and ending the MOMENT I started concentrating/lost concentration?



Anyway, not sure if this technique works for many people, as PK isn't my specialty(I seem to have a natural affinity for manipulation; I discovered it on my own before even reading about Psionics. )
