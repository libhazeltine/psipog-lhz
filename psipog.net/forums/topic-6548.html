I figured that I would take this opportunity to make at least one last post before the forums, and Psipog in general, closes shop. I'm not quite sure why you'd want to bring this up at this moment in time, or why you couldn't devise any other intelligent topic of conversation, but I'm sure you have your reasons.

In regards to "lightening up", I'm not sure what you mean. If by "lighten up" you mean "undermine my serious study into anomalous cognition by humoring the painfully inane musings of the occasional half-wit," then no sir, I will not lighten up. If by "lighten up" you mean "let unfounded, unsupported, and logic-void arguments stand tall upon fallacious legs," then no sir, I will not lighten up. If by "lighten up" you mean "attempt to inject a shred of intelligence and order into otherwise murky waters," then once again, no, I will not lighten up.

Perhaps the defining characteristic of Psipog was the community appeal. I remember a time when this wonderful website was populated by mature critical thinkers who would rather think first, and then speak, and not vice versa. I've watched as they were figuratively chased out, one by one, by a new wave of buffoons who devise these ludicrous techniques and try to pass them off as valid methods of psychic development. To make matters worse, it usually comes to my attention (in a roundabout way) that these "teachers" are utterly incapable of the abilities they profess to master.

Psipog is widely regarded as a website for the "newbie." Certainly this is appropriate, but there is this constant problem where a "newbie" just learns introductory techniques and completely glosses over learning the appropriate mindset for furthering their studies. I don't believe you come to Psipog to just learn how to make a psiball. You come to Psipog to learn how to make a psiball and also how to critically view psychic science in general so that you do not fall into the fluffy pitballs that other websites prominently exhibit. This is why there's no religion. This is why there's no spirituality. Critical thinking, my friend, is the way to start, and if in your later studies you find some value in the former ideals, then you explore them. It's not the other way around.

The current generation of Psipoggers has totally lost sight of this goal, or they have missed it completely. This is the message that I try to convey in my discussions and arguments here on the forums, and since it is a message that I deeply believe in, I will not "lighten up."
