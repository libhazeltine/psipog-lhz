Swords of psi.... too many people want to go posing saying, "Look I have a psi-sword!" Well, I have decided that since in is inevitable that this will happen, why not give them a practical use in training?

What is a construct?
Something you make with psi for a specific purpose (even if its only purpose is to be a box).
What is a psi-sword then?
Thats right! A construct!
What happens to most constructs if you swing them about?
They fall apart.(I haven't really done this but once [and then only to back up this idea])

So then, a good psi-sword (or construct) would be one which could hold together.

How is this done?
Shelling.
So then, what skills do we learn from making psi-swords?
We learn how to manipulate psi into a set shape, and we learn how to shell these constructs very strongly.

What do we learn from tossing a psi ball?
The same thing.

Swords (when used simply as a more tactile form of practice) are just as useful to make as a psiball meant for throwing (or psiball paintball as it may be).

If they insist on such amusements, it's often a good idea to point out how long it takes to fashion a material sword. Have them spend an hour or so folding the construct and "hammering" it into shape, by which I mean pulsing more energy into the psi-sword and concentrating on the concept of it being flat and hard. If they expect any kind of tactile response from their psi-blade, it's going to require a bit more concentration than idle amusement would normally entice.

Games can be fun, though, if the individual is seriously motivated.

Oh, and if you do find someone who over-enthusiastically requests to spar with you, ask to examine their psi-blade. If you can't sense it easily, then it's unlikely they made it "correctly" (by whatever means they rely on) or that your awareness of it will be thorough enough to have anything more than a battle of imaginations...

Not that I have anything against that, but that's not exactly psionic training and isn't relevant here.

I have never been struck with one myself, but I imagine you could cause some degree of injury with it if you were a talented psychokineticist.
