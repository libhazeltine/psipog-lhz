I recently read a book about Shaolin kung fu and different methods of attaining unique kung fu skills, and one of them was "punching flame art," or punching at a candle and extinguishing it from a far distance.
It involves focusing on the dantien (that's where psi/ chi comes from, the point in the abdomen a few inches below the navel) whle in the horse stance and punching in the direction of the candle, which, after practice, will extinguish the candle. Once it can be put out in one try whenever you feel like it, increase the distance by eight paces, continuing the practice in this manner until the flame will go out from a very far distance; then, the art is mastered. I takes about a year to master, which is a very small amount of time for the various skills.
They say once it's mastered, you can punch people without touching them, but I think it's some kind of metaphor. You could potentially accomplish the same training in a chi stance, pushing out an open palm, but if you feel like doing it, I suggest you follow the Shaolin training as close as you are able.
