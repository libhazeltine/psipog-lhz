okeydokey....

a1) could be boredom. i found that if i just practice because i have to, i start losing concentration, because i know i can do it. eventually, i get to the point where i dont really care about it and lose interest.

my advice: do your practice like you did when you first started, when you were excited and this was all fresh to your mind!

a2) yes. it could be linked with enthusiasm, like i said above. set yourself a new challenge....

a3) yep. i started pk before xmas, got good, then fell ill for like 6 weeks. so no pk. when i started back up again i was back at novice level. also i wasnt to bothered about moving objects....

a4) yes, over the past month i have been practicing alot more. reason being is that i taught alot of people how to do it, and it encouraged me to be one step ahead lol (competition)

also i got alot of questions about it, and in order to get an answer, i would use their method of practice etc.

ANOTHER thing is that i set myself new goals. i could move the psi-wheel, it is a piece of piss...to the stage where it became boring. now iv moved onto bigger objects, i strive to move them. even if im unsuccessful, its that ambition, that strive that keeps me going

hope this has helped.
