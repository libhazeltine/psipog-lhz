Stop dividing people. Remove the "us vs. them" mentallity. How do you know it isn't the other way around, where we are the church, and the skeptics are Galileo? Because we know the "truth" and they are just fools?

Just because someone disagrees with you doesn't make them an idiot. One thing that I've learned is that people believe things for a reason. Not just to be difficult and annoying, or that they're incapable of understanding your point of view. Skeptics believe what they do for a reason, and they do so intelligently. You can either sit there and complain about how wrong they are, and how right you are - or you can take their different perspective constructively and understand their position and use their ideas to benefit yourself.

The skeptical mindset is sharp, and the ideas are useful. It's GOOD to doubt strange experiences, and ask: "is there a way I can confirm this is happening? Is it possible that I'm hallucinating this somehow? Am I fooling myself? Are there alternate explainations that make sense?". You should be asking these questions.

sgtpsion wrote:
I was reading through the "Is psychokinesis real?" thread, and I started wondering, why do so many people waste their time making fake videos to try and prove us wrong? Do they just have too much time on their hands, or do they all have superiority complexes? What's WITH them?


I find this to be enticing a response from the person you are talking about, drawing them into a flame battle. However, since you did follow up with a question, I'll give what little input I have. This is in no way speaking for or about anybody except for general archetypes I've seen in my experience.


1: Assholes: People with such firm commitment to their views that it borderlines on zealotry. They are right, you are wrong, end of story. Discussion with them makes you an idiot in thier mind. Usually degrades in an insult tossing match, and is enough to piss off the most level headed. What they say has NOTHING to do with what you think, but only the views they already have. It is not worth your energy to approach them.
2: The Teacher: Some people actually like to educate others. They believe that thier truth, which is usually widely accepted, is the way that things should be done. The usually confront opposition from 'wrong' people in a way that they consider to be harmless and with understanding, but come off as condescending and pretensious. Either way, they do look down on you in a sense, and it is therefore difficult to get them to hear you out on anything. The best way to approach them is with confidence, intelligence and cold, hard, solid proof.
3: The Equal: They understand you are on equal ground. Although they believe in something they are willing to challenge thier beliefs and see things from all sides. They are easy to talk to and in the end are fun to debate with, win or lose.

Of course, there are in betweens, omissions, and all that junk, but these are usually what you find in opposition to your beliefs and find a need to confront.

As to why they do the things they do, some people believe beliefs are worth fighting for, and some do it in different ways.
