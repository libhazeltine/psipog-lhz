To understand this, first off, understand that nothing ever touches anything else. If I lay my hand on a desk, the atoms in the desk repel the atoms in my hand, creating a tiny, atom-wide space between my firmly pressed hand and the desk at any time.

Have you ever wondered why the atoms in your hand don't just go around the atoms in the desk? This is why, I believe.

Now, theoretically, if you eliminated the apposing forces, you could simply pass your arm through the desk, and the atoms would go around each other.

That's the psuedo-scientific, *logical* version. But if we take in account the astral dimension...

Imagine two balls, which are three dimensional. Now imagine that they each "phase" into each other. Now, they are still, individually, three dimensional but the space between them is now four-dimensional, since there IS a space, but not in 3d-space visibly.

The most logical way of explaining it is that the particles that are phasing into each other become astral instead of physical momentarily, and this also relates with teleportation.

Is this what's really happening? I have no idea. But those are two possibilities.
