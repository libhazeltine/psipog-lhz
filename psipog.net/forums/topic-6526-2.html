Edit: OK TinMan, removing someone’s memory of something such as a phone number may seem trivial and without too large consequences, but research into how the brain accesses memories and on the effects of amnesia has shown that this may not be the case.

I don’t want to go into it all in super detail, but, recent research suggests that the brain mainly accesses memories through links with other memories, and that once the brain has developed to a certain level, new memories can only be accessed through links with pre existing ones. Now the part that relates more to my point. Research with amnesia victims has shown that if single memories can be restored through various therapy, it can lead to a cascade of various memories returning to the patient (I’ll search for the URL that shows the results for this experiment and post it later if anyone wants to see it).

Considering this, if someone were to erase a part of someone’s memories, they could potentially also remove that persons ability to access a number of other memories as well, which could possibly lead to changes in behaviour and personality (effects I think I should note are often observed in victims of even mild amnesia).
