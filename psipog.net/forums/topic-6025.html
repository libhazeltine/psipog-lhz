Feeling the object, or getting a sense of the object, is often mistaken for becoming one with the object. Both are very different. Becoming one with an object will only occur after alot, I repeat, alot of practice in the skill of concentration and bringing forth one-pointedness of the mind.

What happens when you are getting a sense of the object, is that your energy communicates, in a way, with the object of choice. You sort of receive the energetic info, or energy from the object as your energy blends with, or hits the energy of the object. This will allow you to move the object more easily than normal, but it won't give you that much benefit, in my experience.

Then there is the true becoming one with, as I see it. Normally we have three stages in meditation, or in concentration upon an object. you got:
1: The meditator >>> 2: Process of meditation >>> 3: the object meditated upon.
When you truly become one with the object, those three stages collapse. But before that becomes a possibility, ones mind must become as clear as a pure crystal. There should be no desires and no thoughts present, that are able to disturb your mind. This is reached after extensive practice of concentration. When the mind becomes ready to not only hold just one object and its aspects steady, but just one single impression/thought/image/concept in mind, without any dynamic thoughts about its aspects, but only that one single impression, then you have established one-pointedness of mind. After you have practiced this, it may occur to you that you become one with the object. literally. I do not mean that your body blends in with the object. I mean that your consciousness becomes fully absorbed in the object of choice, so that there is no longer a seperation between 1: the meditator and 3:the object meditated upon. Hence: the 2: process of meditation is not there either, because how can there be a process of meditation if you have only one thing? Cause in this state consicousness is fully absorbed by the object itself. You forget about everything, you lose your sense of time, space and even your sense of Self. You know only that object, and nothing more. You will realise hidden information, or secrets about the object in this state, through direct-knowledge.

This is what the true "becoming one with" method means to me. People underestimate the power and the benefit of concentration. They think they have already established good concentration. No. Not may have. Our minds are too disturbed by selfish desires and thoughts that crave for objects outside of us that give us little pleasure. Real concentration is the key to any succes and any ability.
