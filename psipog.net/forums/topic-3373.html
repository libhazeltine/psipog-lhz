color is what you see objects as. Color means:

Quote:
That aspect of things that is caused by differing qualities of the light reflected or emitted by them, definable in terms of the observer or of the light, as:

1. The appearance of objects or light sources described in terms of the individual's perception of them, involving hue, lightness, and saturation for objects and hue, brightness, and saturation for light sources.
2. The characteristics of light by which the individual is made aware of objects or light sources through the receptors of the eye, described in terms of dominant wavelength, luminance, and purity.

I have found when scanning that something I see as black can be seen by others as red, or vice versa.

In mundane areas, people see colours differently. ("I love your blue shirt - you should wear that to the party!" only to see them wearing their green shirt they think of as blue.) While it can be fun to try to pigeon-hole field colours, it is also important to realise that we all see colours in our own way.

Even if we pretend we can all agree on which colour is which, the meaning of each colour is determined by which site or 'authority' you are using.
