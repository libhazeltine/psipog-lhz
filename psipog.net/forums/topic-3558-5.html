Alright, Big Roy is gonna break it down for you:

1) The premise is babbled and poorly connected, but it seems like you want to create a group of psions to train for an impending doom in 2012 or to regulate the psionics community where combat is concerned. Correct me if I'm wrong.

2) No offense, but what you don't know about psionic combat could probably fill the Atlantic. How are you going to moderate something you don't understand?

3) Speaking of paranoia, how are you going to be able to tell the difference between a psionic-n00b who's actually been attacked and runs to you for help, and a psionic-n00b who's been doing psionics for a few weeks, feels a little tired, and FUCKING FREAKS OUT THINKING THERE ARE SCORES AND SCORES OF PSIONIC-VAMPIRES WHO ARE OUT TO GET HIM, SUCKING HIM DRY OF ALL HIS PSI AND HIS SOUL ? I felt like emphasizing that last part to better reflect the mindset of the aforementioned person. So, said n00b goes to you for help, picks a random name/person out of his head because he "feels" or "thinks" that it is the person who is doing it, and then you and your "squadron" dish out all the psi-nukes, flaming psi-swords, and artificial intelligence psi-pet sabretoothes on some unsuspecting kid. Or say you even shield the kid tighter than a drum. It doen't matter what you do. God forbid any influence you put on this UNPROVEN attacker has an abnormal effect on him. You'd be guilty of the same thing you are trying to regulate. The same goes for the kid you'll try to protect.

4) I'm seriously sick of hearing all this unfounded speculation about 2012. Everyday I hear a new 2012 theory: "OMFG, I just read that on 2012, a race of flaming 20 foot tall monkeys with battle-axes for arms will rise from the Rainforest to destroy mankind for its hubris and anti-environmentalism!" Give me a break.

5) It's nice to see that you're trying to organize a group of psions for a beneficial purpose, don't get me wrong. As much as I bash the idea, I enjoy the spirit in which you propose it. It's just that you're trying to create a group of self-important, fledgling psions with an overexaggerated sense of self-responsibility toward the conservation and protection of the earth from astronomically-improbable disaster scenarios and the rarely occurring behaviors of idiot psions that seem to be so popular in online circles. Most people come to psipog for self-improvement and discovery, not to save the world from meteors.
