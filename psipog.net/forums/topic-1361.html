One time of those two, I didn't even open my eyes, I just became aware of my five senses again (could feel my body lying on bed, smell what the pillow smelled like, hear clock tickin, etc) And I heard a voice, that seemed to be right by my ear (but not in my head) and it said "soooon. Sooonnn the 12 years will be complete, and we will have it" Not sure about last lines. The "soon" and "12 years will be complete" im positive of.
This voice sounded extremely menacing, deep and booming and non human sounding.

The second time I remember, it was the same voice. It sounded like it was coming from over by a shelf in my room. I woke up, and had my eyes closed, but I could see the room I was in through my eyelids. I think it may have been a mental copy, because when I looked around, it seemed to take an extra split second for the scene to alter the position correctly, and the visuals seemed not quite like when my eyes are open. More washed out looking.

The voice said "Foooocuuuusss... Foooccuuss... Yesss.. Foocusss.. *evil maniacal laughter* yesssss. YESSSSS."

Well after hearing that I realized I wasn't opening my eyelids because I couldn't. It seemed like I was being held there like that. I struggled, and instinctively concentrated on my midsection, in the solar plexus. I'm not sure what mental process I went through to do this, but I felt a huge welling of energy form there and I sort of "burst" awake. I was sweating, it was fucked. Especially considering prior similar life experiences.

I feel like I should throw in mention of a person?s very own way to scare the hell out of itsself. (and the opposite.)
All day we?re actively responding to whatever remains getting through unfiltered to our conscious part of the mind. We tend to pick things up quite quickly, apply new filters and lead thoughts down a path usually based on experience, anxieties hopes or wishes. A quick example would be an image of a cheeseburger triggering a rampage for food whereas without seeing that image you might still be reading these forums or whatever. Now attempting OBE?s by evading sleep basically requires you to stop responding to whatever your mind throws at you, but remain in the observer?s state which is a pretty powerful one no matter what you do. Everyone halfway successfull on consciously resisting loosing control over dream states knows how quickly the mind seems to lose all constraints and bombards you with sounds, images and feelings. I?ve heard my name beeing whispered since my childhood, and just a couple of years ago stopped freaking out to look for people in my room. What seems to be reoccuring for me lately is seeing faces, which then morp into all kinds of other faces. Sometimes, utterly utterly disturbing and very scary stuff, keeping me on a "bad track" (leading down the pathway I had just set). On a positive note I also start hearing all kinds of music and tunes never heard before, which have a weird but very interesting feel to them.
The point is, your mind is starting to do things differently else the dream state wouldn?t differ so much from beeing awake. Your conscious part of the mind over the day has what I like to call a "critisism-aspect" to it. Whatever you see is beeing compared to fit into your established reality model. If it?s not fitting in slightly, it?s usually "argued" to fit in, if it doesn?t seem to fit in at all it?s beeing marked as utter nonsense if it even gets through to your consciousness. When the body starts to sleep this part is beeing turned off very quickly, allowing your dreams to breeze through without your questioning mind interfering with anything (because it?s apparently just not what your body / mind is looking to do during sleep).
What happens if you try to stay awake whilst starting to dream? That?s right, your filters are all the way there, and you just start freaking out over anything that doesn?t usually work in the awake state. Usually that?s most intense over dangerous matters that you?d be sure to run the hell away from to save your life when awake. Now I can understand why this part of the mind is beeing turned off then during sleep, as well as why sleep paralyzation sets in additionally. Because the things you experience in at least the first parts of dreams most of the time aren?t what you are supposed to respond to as if it was real.
I?m not trying to dismiss any theories about whatever some of these encounters could be, but I thought I?d share my experience on what the mind is actually doing during that state and maybe calm some people down when encountering voices and images or feelings. I know it can be scary, but appart from looking out for interesting informations, let the mind do it?s work and don?t interfere by identifying yourself with the content too much. You?re watching and waiting to get past, allow your unconscious part of the mind to do what it needs to do and be passive about it.
