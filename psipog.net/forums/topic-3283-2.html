Check out JoeT's experiment that is sticky in this forum. My point is not that lighter things are supposed to be just as easy as heavy things to move, but only that my personal experience leads to our subconcious mind putting limitations on what our concious mind can direct for safety reasons.

With that being said, something lighter is usually much easier to move, reason being that your subconcious believes that to be true. Remember your subconcious doesn't just take anything the concious mind thinks and does it, you must convince your subconcious that what you ask of it is a good idea. I would recommend reading this article.

Try things that have less friction and are lighter because they make sense to be easier to move. I mean they are easier for you to move physically right? Once again, makes sense to the subconcious.

Please don't mistake what I just said to mean that you can move anything you'd like if you just realize that you can. This is not how your subconcious works. But don't take my word for it, try out many different objects and see what works for you, the great thing about TK is that your feedback is visual.
