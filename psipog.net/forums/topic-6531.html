To me, time travel to the past logically will never be discovered. Let's assume for moment that it will be discovered at some point. Also, at some point the end of the world will arrive. Whether we cause it ourselves or end up waiting until the sun goes nova, it will happen. Now, with time travel available, people will go back in time and either prevent the end of the world or continue the species in some other fashion (i.e. going to a new planet). Then logically we can assume that people will do this every time our extinction is near, which will mean our species would last for an infinite amount of time. Given that our species is lasting infinitely, we would have an infinite number of people in our species with access to time travel, which would mean it's infinitely possible that I would be staring a time traveler in the face right now. Since there are no time travelers anywhere in my vicinity, we can conclude that our original assumption is false.

Now, hypothetically, if it was possible to have a negative mass, traveling backwards in time would be possible. Since time is relative to gravity, and gravity is relative to mass, if you were to change your mass to a negative number, time would be going backwards for you. However, I'm no physicist, so feel free to correct me if I'm wrong.
