I made this technique for beginners, it'll help you to visualize and get the right feeling for PK, it's easy and the success chance is quite big.
If you're already into PK then you probably won't get any useful results out of this.

So far I taught this technique to 2 of my friends which both had small but positive results on the first try.
(They did use the psiwheel before but they were PK newbies.)


Quote:
Alright let's get it started, first of all you will need 2 things before we begin - 2 (weak) magnets that fit in your hand.

Take the magnets in one hand each, hold them about 3 inches apart and begin to move them closer and further away, repeat that some times.
Feel them attracting each other, REMEMBER that feeling!

Now for the more difficult part, put the magnets aside and get something very light like a hair or a small piece of tin foil. (A psiwheel works too)

Imagine the magnetic force from before on the tip of your index finger (that worked best for me but it's up to you), feel it attracting the object you want to move, feel the space between your fingertip and your object start to get smaller and smaller, like a little blackhole.


As always you should start with small things and then work your way further up.

For more advanced people, try this technique with reversed magnets to "push" objects away.


Additional Notes :

- It's not magnetism (it's psi if you couldn't have guessed), it's just the best name I could think of.

- Tin foil or metal like objects seem to work best because you can imagine that it really is a magnetic force.

- The chance of success with this technique is high because you feel that "invisible power" and then recreate it using your own "invisible power".
