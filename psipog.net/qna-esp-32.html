SKainuiG asks on December 21, 2003, 1:40am:

Is it common to mistake a clairvoyant for a telepath?

Rainsong replies on December 21, 2003, 1:55pm:

Yes and no. One of the big problems with clairvoyance experiments is that telepathy cannot be ruled out. Also, in some contexts, clairvoyants are sometimes referred to as telepaths (especially in the area of military remote viewing). As for mistaking an individual's main talent, that is very common indeed, especially if that individual doesn't particularly want their skills to be widely known. However, you'll find that a lot of clairvoyants are also telepathic, and vice versa.

Wisher16 asks on December 15, 2003, 8:53pm:

When dealing with empathy, do emotions that you perceive tend to linger on or stay attached to you for a while? And if this does occur, how can you get rid of them one they're there?

Rainsong replies on December 15, 2003, 11:40pm:

Quite often, the emotions are transient, and go away once the person is out of range, or when you block the incoming signal, or when another stronger signal overpowers it.

As for getting rid of them once they are there, aside from blocking --which is sometimes not an option, admittedly-- you would do so the same way you bring your own emotions under rein...whichever method you use for that.
