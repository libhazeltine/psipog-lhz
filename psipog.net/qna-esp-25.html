genesis asks on April 4, 2004, 7:27am:

A few questions about precognition:

All of my life, I've had episodes of precognition. When I was younger, I would recieve actual 'visions' (sort of appeared like an out of the blue visualization accompanied with a strong sense of 'knowing'). These 'visions' would occur during normal waking conciousness, and would occur on a semi-frequent basis (roughly 2-3 visions in a 5 week period). The experiences were occasionally useful, but more often, they were simply frustrating (try telling someone that they're gonna get into a minor fender-bender on the way home when you're a cheeky 7 year-old). These 'visions' lasted until I was 14. I never figured out how to control them. One day, after a particularly horrifying episode of precog., I suddenly lost the ability. It went away for about 4 years. I understand that my subconcious probably shut it off, cuz I wasn't ready to deal with it. However, roughly six monthes ago (I'm almost 19 now) the precog. has returned. This time, it is not nearly as frequent, however, and it manifests now in an extreme sense of 'knowing'. If this makes any difference, my mother and father both have a history of psionic 'hiccups'. The one skill they both share in common is precog.

With that horribly long introduction to my experiences with this ability out of the way (I apologize for my lack of brevity), here are my questions:

1) is it common for the ability to manifest and go dormant spontaniously? I cannot think of anything I did to re-awaken it (outside of training with psychokinesis).

2) Do psychokinesis & precognition both use the same area of the brain? I'm not sure if this is a question that can even really be answered, but I felt the need to ask.

3) Is there any way to control this ability and use it 'on command'? I don't mind the ability itself, I only mind the fact that I can't seem to control it. Granted, I've never tried (no idea how I'd even start to go about doing that, actually)

Thank you for your time, and once again, I apologize for the lack of breivity.

Rainsong replies on April 4, 2004, 5:26pm:

1) Common? Not really, but it does happen from time to time. It seems more common for it to "disappear" permanently after the teenage years, rather than have a temporary dormancy.

2) There is not sufficient data to answer this definitively. _However_ training one aspect of psionics _does_ tend to increase skill in apparently-unrelated aspects.

3) Precog is one of the easiest to practice, although not the easiest to explain. Start with the basics. Each evening, write down three "guesses" about the headlines of the next day's news, preferably televised. Include such details as what the reporter is wearing, and which reporter is covering the story. Don't worry if it seems like you are making it up (and, yes, much of the time you _will_ be making it up). Just guess, and note whether it "feels" right. For some people, clearing the mind then asking a question ("Will Christiane Amanpour report on CNN at 5pm EST tomorrow, and if so, what will she report about?") will get the job done. Check your "guesses" against the actual news coverage. Repeat this simple exercise for a few months.

As for the "lack of brevity" of your post....Questions with detailed backgrounds are much easier to answer. This Q&A is supposed to be _about_ psychic abilities; it isn't suppsed to need to _use_ them to divine the nature of the question.
