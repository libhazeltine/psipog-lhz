Rainsong replies on November 16, 2003, 5:04pm:

Around the time that the word "psionics" was first coined (ie, middle of the 20th century), "psi" was used as a variable to denote the mechanism or mechanisms that allowed psychokinesis, telepathy, and similar effects to occur. The mechanism was pretty much unknown. Although several hypothetical models for it were proposed, none of them withstood the experimental testing. For example, "psi" has some characteristics in common with electromagnetism, but telepathy was demonstrated to occur in conditions where electromagnatism could not function (If, at some future date, psi is demonstrated to be electromagnetic after all, several "known" factoids about electromagnetism will be altered.)

Short version: if you can use it to do PK or telepathy, it is "psi".

By extension, over time, "psi" also came to be used to describe the effects themselves. (Also, in some circles, it is used to describe the people who can demonstrate such effects.)

The psi vs prana vs chi vs ki arguement gets complicated. It is largely rooted in the various theories about the nature of "energies" used in "energy work". The terms come from different cultures, and do not translate easily or directly, as a result of this simple fact.
