Rainsong replies on July 1, 2004, 12:39am:

Very good. (Could have gone in "Perception" as easily, but this one is also logical.)

Practice in perceiving psi-shapes, balls and otherwise, is similar to perceiving the field sometimes called the "aura".

A small point first, though: some methods for seeing "auras" are nothing more than optical illusions. Don't waste your time with those. If it involves seeing "opposite colours" or a consistent "bluish" halo about an inch thick around everything, it is nothing more than eye-straing. Remember to blink, and don't stare fixedly when looking at things.

To get some feel for "psi stuff", this works for some folk. (Some don't feel anything at all, though, and can still perceive psi-stuff, so don't panic if it doesn't work for you at first or at all).

Make as dense a psishape as you can manage. Put twice as much oomph into it as you think it needs. Include programming to hold its shape when set on the table...if only for a minute or two.

Set it down, and relax. Put one hand _slowly_ through the shape, and then the other hand, one at a time. Feel anything? Look at the psishape directly, blink a few times, and look again. Heat distortion, haziness, anything? Try it on several different days and record your results. Compare and see if there are any patterns.
