Little Badger writes:

"Can you put something about contacting your subcounsious? I've
tried it but I don't know if I'm really contacting it or I'm just
making up the answers, so could you help me out? Thanks!"

- - - - -

My comments: Well, you ARE making up the answers.

Remember that the subconscious is just another part of YOU. That
part of you is delivering messages to your conscious mind every
second. Even RIGHT NOW. I would suggest clearning your mind of
as much thought as possible. Get all that junk out and just stay
quiet for a few minutes. You need to be good at getting your
mind to shut up. After you can do that pretty good, pose
yourself a question, and WAIT for the answer. Don't create an
answer. If you receive nothing, then that's ok - just keep
waiting. Don't think to yourself "Hmmm... I bet I'll receive
something like Blah, or that I won't receive anything at all."
Keep your mind quiet, and sit there and wait.

Speaking of shutting your mind up, I received a message about
meditation. The next message will tie in with what I'm saying
here, so keep reading! :-P

Rasputin writes:

"I have noticed, that though many people (writers to be specific)
on PsiPog mention meditating before doing stuff... But there is
not a very clear How-to or steps mentioned for meditating. So I
thought I would mention it and maybe you will write something
about it.

And I will also mention my favourite meditations.

First of all the world famous HamSa meditation...
In any relaxed posture in a quiet place focus on the sound of
breathing. It sounds like Ham or Hum when going in and Sa when
coming out......... Thats it.

There is another one I do specifically for Telepathy... I don't
know if it really counts as a meditation. I stare at a candle
flame in a dark place for some time, and breath slowy throught
nose, and eventually, my mind just gets blank.... Maybe its for
focus, maybe not, but it works for me ;-)"

- - - - -

My comments: Good stuff :-). There are a lot of meditation
techniques, but the idea is pretty simple. All you want to do is
just slow your head down for a second, and relax.

Breathe deep, close your eyes, and get in a comfortable spot.
Just relax. It's so simple, it's confusing :-P.

One meditation technique that I use is called progressive
relaxation. What you do is lay down, close your eyes, and start
breathing in deep and slowly. After you're comfortable, just go
to each body part, and tell it to relax and sleep. It takes a
while to get through the entire body... But don't rush it. The
point is to just relax, so take your time.

For example, I first start with my feet. I tell my feet, "Hey
guys, you did a great job today, but now it's time to just relax
and fall asleep." I relax all my muscles in my feet, and just
let it go. Then I move to my calves. Then my thighs. My hips,
abdomen, chest, arms, neck, shoulders, back, and face. If you're
interested, you should be able to find some more indepth tips
online or at your library.
